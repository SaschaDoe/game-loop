# Heresy and Reformation

As a player, I want to challenge established religions, create new sects, or be branded a heretic, so that religious gameplay includes rebellion and theological conflict.

## Details

- **Heresy Triggers**:
  - Worshipping an opposing deity while in another deity's temple
  - Publicly questioning a deity's teachings (LLM dialogue with priests)
  - Using forbidden magic in holy areas (blood magic, necromancy)
  - Completing quests that undermine a religion's power
  - Discovering evidence that a deity's origin story is false
- **Heretic Consequences**:
  - Branded: temple services denied, holy NPCs hostile, inquisitors dispatched
  - Excommunication: lose all devotion with that deity, divine powers revoked, temple faction becomes enemy
  - Persecution Quest: survive being hunted by religious zealots; hide, fight, or find allies among other heretics
- **Reformation Path**: gather evidence of religious corruption; rally dissenters; confront the high priest; either reform the religion (become its new leader) or destroy the institution
  - Reform outcome: religion changes its tenets based on player's arguments; NPCs react to the new doctrine
  - Destruction outcome: temples close, faithful NPCs become despondent or angry, power vacuum creates chaos
- **Create a Sect**: write your own religious doctrine (LLM generates tenets based on player's description); attract followers; build a shrine; your sect may thrive or be crushed by established religions
  - Sect growth: followers increase if the player performs miracles (high-level divine or arcane feats), helps communities, or has high Charisma
  - Sect conflict: established religions view you as a threat; inquisitors, debates, sabotage
- **Religious Debates**: LLM-powered theological arguments with priests, theologians, and other religious leaders; argue philosophy, ethics, and the nature of divinity
- **Atheist Uprising**: at max Heresy reputation, unlock a quest to rally all non-believers to challenge divine influence

## Acceptance Criteria

- [ ] Heresy triggers brand the player correctly
- [ ] Excommunication revokes deity powers and changes faction standings
- [ ] Reformation questline allows changing or destroying religious institutions
- [ ] Player-created sects attract followers and face opposition
- [ ] LLM theological debates respond to philosophical arguments
