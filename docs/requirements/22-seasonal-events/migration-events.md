# Migration Events

As a player, I want seasonal animal and monster migrations to change which creatures appear in each area, so that the world feels dynamic and hunting is seasonal.

## Details

- **Seasonal Migrations**:
  - **Spring**: herds of deer move north through forests (easy hunting, leather/meat surplus); bird flocks return; bears emerge from hibernation (aggressive, hungry)
  - **Summer**: sea creatures move to shallow waters (easier fishing); desert creatures expand territory into plains; dragon mating season (dragons are more aggressive, pairs appear)
  - **Autumn**: wolf packs grow larger (more dangerous); harvesting creatures raid farms (goblins, boars); migratory birds carry messages between distant lands
  - **Winter**: most animals go dormant or migrate south; ice creatures expand from tundra; desperate predators venture into towns for food
- **Monster Migrations**:
  - Goblin tribes relocate seasonally; a cleared goblin cave may be reoccupied by a different tribe next season
  - Undead surge during the autumn equinox (thin veil between worlds)
  - Elemental migrations follow weather patterns (fire elementals during droughts, water elementals during floods)
- **Hunting Season Quests**: rangers' guild posts seasonal bounties matching current migrations
- **Ecological Impact**: overhunting a migratory species reduces their numbers permanently; population recovers over 2-3 seasons if left alone
- **Trade Impact**: seasonal ingredients affect market prices (winter herbs are expensive in winter, cheap in spring)
- **Migration Paths**: predictable routes across the map; setting up camp on a migration path guarantees encounters
- NPCs discuss seasonal changes: "The wolves are coming down from the mountains early this year..."

## Acceptance Criteria

- [ ] Creature populations shift per season correctly
- [ ] Monster migrations change encounter tables by area
- [ ] Hunting impacts are tracked and affect future populations
- [ ] Seasonal bounties match current migration patterns
- [ ] Trade prices reflect seasonal ingredient availability
