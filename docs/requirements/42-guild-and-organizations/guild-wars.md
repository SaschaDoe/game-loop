# Guild Wars

As a player, I want guilds to wage wars against each other that I can participate in or influence, so that faction dynamics create large-scale conflict.

## Details

- **War Triggers**: resource disputes, assassination of guild leaders, territory encroachment, player actions that pit guilds against each other
- **War Phases**:
  1. **Tension** (50 turns): hostile dialogue between guild NPCs, trade embargoes, minor skirmishes
  2. **Open Conflict** (100 turns): guild members attack opposing guild on sight, quest to sabotage/defend guild assets, territory control battles
  3. **Resolution**: one guild surrenders, a truce is negotiated, or one guild is destroyed
- **Player Involvement**:
  - Support your guild: complete war quests (sabotage supply lines, defend guild halls, assassinate officers, rally allies)
  - Betray your guild: secretly help the opposing side for rewards (double agent)
  - Mediate: attempt to negotiate peace (requires high Charisma and reputation with both guilds)
  - Stay neutral: avoid both guilds' territories; neutrality is respected but limits quest access
- **Territory Control**: guilds control town districts; war shifts control — merchants change, safe houses flip, patrols change
- **War Economy**: weapon prices rise, healing supplies become scarce, mercenaries are in high demand
- **Casualties**: named NPCs can die permanently in guild wars; their shops/services may become unavailable
- **War Crimes**: excessive violence against civilians or surrendered enemies earns a "War Criminal" reputation debuff
- **Victory Spoils**: winning guild gains territory, gold, and political power; player receives rank promotion and unique war veteran equipment
- Multiple guild wars can occur simultaneously, creating complex shifting alliances

## Acceptance Criteria

- [ ] War phases progress through correct stages with appropriate events
- [ ] Player involvement options all lead to different outcomes
- [ ] Territory control shifts affect available services
- [ ] NPC casualties are permanent and affect the game world
- [ ] War economy modifies trade prices
