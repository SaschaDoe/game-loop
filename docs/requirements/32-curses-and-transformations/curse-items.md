# Curse Progression

As a player, I want curses to progress through stages with escalating effects and unique cure quests, so that curses feel like serious narrative threats rather than simple debuffs.

## Details

- **Curse Types and Progression**:
  - **Curse of Decay**: stage 1: -1 CON; stage 2: equipment degrades 3x faster; stage 3: flesh visibly rots (NPC horror), -5 CON; stage 4: body crumbles (death)
  - **Curse of Misfortune**: stage 1: critical fail chance +10%; stage 2: loot quality reduced, traps trigger more often; stage 3: companions are injured randomly, quests fail unexpectedly; stage 4: everything goes wrong (effectively unplayable)
  - **Curse of Silence**: stage 1: -2 CHA; stage 2: cannot use dialogue options (only gestures); stage 3: cannot cast verbal spells; stage 4: fade from existence (NPCs forget you, become invisible and intangible)
  - **Curse of Hunger**: stage 1: food provides half nutrition; stage 2: constantly starving regardless of eating; stage 3: begin consuming non-food items involuntarily; stage 4: must consume other beings to survive (alignment shift, NPC hostility)
  - **Curse of Echoes**: stage 1: hear whispers; stage 2: sounds from the future (spoiler alerts for traps and ambushes — helpful but unsettling); stage 3: sounds from other dimensions (sanity drain); stage 4: become an echo yourself (trapped between realities)
- **Curse Sources**: cursed items (equipping without identifying), breaking sacred oaths, angering deities, witch NPC hexes, tomb desecration
- **Cure Quests**: each curse has a unique cure quest generated by the LLM based on the curse's origin:
  - Decay: find the Fountain of Renewal in a hidden temple
  - Misfortune: return a stolen relic to the deity of luck
  - Silence: speak the True Name of the curse-caster
  - Hunger: defeat the Hollow Lord in a feast-themed dungeon
  - Echoes: find and destroy the resonance crystal in the dimensional rift
- **Curse Resistance**: Constitution saves reduce progression speed; Remove Curse spell resets 1 stage; full cure requires the quest
- **Cursed Items**: powerful but carry a curse; player must weigh the benefit vs the curse cost; some builds embrace curses for the power

## Acceptance Criteria

- [ ] All curse types progress through 4 stages with correct effects
- [ ] Cure quests are generated contextually for each curse
- [ ] Curse resistance and Remove Curse spell function correctly
- [ ] Cursed items carry both benefits and curse effects
- [ ] Stage 4 consequences create serious but recoverable game states
