# Living Dungeon

As a player, I want to explore a dungeon that is alive — shifting walls, digesting chambers, and an immune system that hunts intruders, so that the dungeon itself is the enemy.

## Details

- The dungeon is a massive creature: walls are muscle, floors are membrane, acid pools are stomach chambers
- Map shifts: walls close and open randomly every 10 turns, paths change
- Hazards: digestive acid rooms (timer to escape before damage), constricting corridors (crush damage), mucus floors (slowed movement)
- Immune system enemies: white cell blobs that heal the dungeon, antibody hunters that target the player, parasite worms (neutral, also fighting the dungeon)
- Objective: reach the heart of the creature and kill it (or reason with its brain — LLM dialogue if Intelligence is high enough)
- Killing the dungeon: it dies and collapses — timed escape sequence to get out before it crumbles
- Reasoning with it: the creature releases you with a rare symbiotic item (living armor that grows with you)
- Environmental interaction: cut through walls with slashing weapons, burn through membrane with fire
- Unique loot: organic materials (living metal, nerve fiber, crystallized blood) for bio-crafting

## Acceptance Criteria

- [ ] Map dynamically shifts during exploration
- [ ] Environmental hazards function with timers and damage
- [ ] Immune system enemies behave differently from standard monsters
- [ ] Both kill and diplomacy endings are achievable
