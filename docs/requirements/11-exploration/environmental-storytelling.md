# Environmental Storytelling

As a player, I want to discover environmental details that tell stories without words, so that the world feels rich and rewards attentive observation.

## Details

- Abandoned campsites with journals hinting at what happened
- Bloodstains leading to a hidden body with loot and a note
- Crumbling statues of forgotten heroes with inscriptions
- Overgrown ruins showing a once-thriving civilization
- Monster nests near picked-clean bones of former adventurers
- Graffiti on dungeon walls from previous explorers ("Turn back!", "The king lies")
- Items arranged to suggest a scene (a table set for two, chairs overturned)
- These details are examinable for flavor text and occasional quest hooks

## Acceptance Criteria

- [ ] Environmental storytelling elements are placed in dungeons and overworld
- [ ] Examining elements provides flavor text
- [ ] Some elements connect to quests or lore
- [ ] Details are varied and non-repetitive across areas
