# Exotic Goods

As a player, I want to trade in rare and exotic goods with special handling requirements and extreme profit potential, so that high-level trading involves unique merchandise.

## Details

- **Exotic Good Categories**:
  - **Monster Parts**: dragon scales, phoenix feathers, kraken ink, unicorn hair; sourced from defeating or befriending the creature; alchemists and enchanters pay premium prices
  - **Magical Artifacts**: minor enchanted items, spell scrolls, cursed curiosities; must be handled carefully (cursed items may activate during transport); mage guilds and collectors buy
  - **Living Cargo**: exotic animals (miniature dragons, singing birds, glowing fish), rare plants (fire flowers, dream vines); require feeding and care during transport; die if neglected (100% loss)
  - **Cultural Treasures**: artwork, religious relics, historical artifacts; value depends on buyer (a dwarven artifact is priceless to dwarves, worthless to orcs); some are stolen (selling stolen treasures = fencing, criminal reputation)
  - **Precursor Tech**: ancient devices, data crystals, energy cells; extremely valuable to artificers and researchers; attract bandits and thieves; some governments want to confiscate
  - **Contraband Luxury**: banned substances, forbidden books, outlawed magic items; illegal in most jurisdictions; 5x normal profit but risk of arrest, confiscation, and criminal reputation
- **Special Handling**:
  - Fragile goods: break if caravan is attacked or crashes; padded containers reduce breakage (extra cost/weight)
  - Temperature-sensitive: ice crystals melt in warm regions, fire flowers die in cold; insulated containers required
  - Dangerous goods: volatile alchemicals, cursed items, aggressive creatures; may harm caravan crew; containment measures needed
  - Perishable: food delicacies, fresh herbs, live specimens; shelf life of 100-200 turns; spoilage = total loss
- **Exotic Merchants**: specialized NPCs who only deal in exotics; located in hidden markets, floating bazaars, and inter-dimensional trading posts; require reputation or introduction to access
- **Appraisal**: exotic goods require appraisal before sale; INT + relevant knowledge check; poor appraisal = sell below value; expert appraisal = maximize price; some goods are fakes (detection prevents buying worthless items)

## Acceptance Criteria

- [ ] All exotic good categories have correct sourcing and buyer types
- [ ] Special handling requirements cause loss/damage when not met
- [ ] Contraband goods trigger legal consequences when detected
- [ ] Appraisal checks correctly determine sale prices
- [ ] Exotic merchants require appropriate reputation to access
