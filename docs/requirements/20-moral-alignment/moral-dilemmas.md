# Moral Dilemmas

As a player, I want to face quests with genuine moral dilemmas that have no clear right answer, so that my choices feel weighty and I'm challenged to think about consequences.

## Details

- Dilemma examples:
  - A village is plagued by a monster. Killing it solves the problem but it's a mother protecting her young
  - A thief stole medicine for his dying daughter. Turn him in (lawful) or let him go (compassionate)?
  - A corrupt noble offers you riches to frame an innocent man. Refuse and make a powerful enemy?
  - Two factions both have legitimate grievances. Siding with one dooms the other
  - A powerful artifact can save the kingdom but requires sacrificing an NPC companion
- No "correct" answer; all options have meaningful consequences
- NPCs remember and judge your choices
- LLM dialogue allows players to propose creative alternative solutions
- Post-dilemma: world state changes reflect the choice for the rest of the game

## Acceptance Criteria

- [ ] Multiple dilemma quests exist with no objectively correct answer
- [ ] All choices produce distinct, lasting world consequences
- [ ] NPCs reference past moral decisions
- [ ] Creative LLM-facilitated solutions are possible
