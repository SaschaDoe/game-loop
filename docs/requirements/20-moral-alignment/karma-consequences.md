# Karma Consequences

As a player, I want the world to respond to my accumulated karma through visible changes, so that being good or evil has tangible, long-term effects beyond dialogue.

## Details

- Good karma effects: animals don't flee from you, merchants offer discounts, children follow you around towns, flowers bloom near your path (subtle ASCII detail)
- Evil karma effects: animals flee, NPCs lock doors at night, bounty hunters pursue you, dark creatures are less hostile, crows follow you
- Neutral karma: balanced world, no extreme reactions
- Visual indicator: player symbol subtly shifts color (gold shimmer for good, reddish tint for evil)
- Karma affects companion willingness: good companions leave evil players and vice versa
- Karma-locked endings: the final story resolution changes based on accumulated karma
- Redemption and corruption arcs: extended quest chains to drastically shift alignment

## Acceptance Criteria

- [ ] World visually reacts to karma level
- [ ] NPC behaviors change with karma
- [ ] Companion compatibility is karma-dependent
- [ ] Karma-locked endings are distinct
