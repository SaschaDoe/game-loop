# Lucid Dreaming Skill

As a player, I want to level up a Lucid Dreaming skill that gives me increasing control over dream encounters, so that mastering sleep becomes a unique character progression path.

## Details

- **Skill Levels** (1-10, gained by surviving dream encounters and making choices in dreams):
  - Level 1-2: recognize you're dreaming (UI indicator), can wake up voluntarily
  - Level 3-4: control your movement in dreams (no random teleportation), summon a light source
  - Level 5-6: reshape small dream elements (create doors, remove obstacles), summon a specific NPC to talk to
  - Level 7-8: create dream items that persist when you wake (limited, 1 per dream), control weather and time in dreams
  - Level 9: design your own dream environment (choose the dream map layout)
  - Level 10 (Dreamwalker): enter other NPCs' dreams to learn their secrets, fears, and hidden motivations; plant ideas (Inception-style)
- **Dream Combat**: at higher levels, dream combat becomes more controllable — summon dream weapons, weaken dream enemies by disbelieving them
- **Dream Training**: practice real combat skills in dreams with no consequence; grants 50% of normal XP for dream training sessions
- **Nightmare Resistance**: higher lucid dreaming skill reduces nightmare frequency and severity
- **Dream Crafting** (level 7+): spend dream turns crafting impossible items (items that can't exist in the real world but work in dreams; some carry partial effects to waking)
- **Dreamwalker Quests** (level 10): enter the dreams of quest-critical NPCs to discover hidden information, heal their trauma, or manipulate their decisions
- **Dream Addiction**: using lucid dreaming excessively (sleeping when not tired just to dream) risks preferring dreams to reality — stat penalty in waking world, bonus in dreams

## Acceptance Criteria

- [ ] Skill levels unlock correct dream control abilities
- [ ] Dream training grants XP at reduced rate
- [ ] Nightmare resistance scales with skill level
- [ ] Dreamwalker ability (level 10) allows entering NPC dreams
- [ ] Dream addiction triggers from excessive use
