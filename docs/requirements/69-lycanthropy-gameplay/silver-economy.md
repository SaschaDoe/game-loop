# Silver Economy

As a player, I want silver to be a strategically important resource that's essential for fighting lycanthropes and other supernatural threats, so that material choice matters in combat preparation.

## Details

- **Silver Properties**: silver weapons deal 2x damage to lycanthropes, vampires, and certain fey; silver ammunition (arrows, bolts) can be crafted; silver coating on existing weapons (temporary, lasts 20 hits)
- **Silver Sourcing**: silver ore mined from specific deposits; silver coins can be melted down (10 silver coins = 1 silver ingot); silver jewelry deconstructed at a forge; some merchants specialize in silver trade
- **Silver Crafting**:
  - Silver weapon: silver ingot + weapon mold + forge; lighter than steel but less durable (degrades faster with normal use); exceptional against supernatural targets
  - Silver bullets: for slings and experimental firearms; silver ingot yields 20 bullets
  - Silver chain: for binding lycanthropes and vampires; target can't transform while bound; requires grapple + DEX check to apply
  - Silver mirror: reveals true forms (see through shapeshifter disguises, detect vampires); portable hand mirror or wall-mounted
  - Silvered armor: provides supernatural damage resistance; expensive (3 silver ingots per piece); heavy
- **Market Dynamics**: silver price increases when supernatural threats rise in an area; hoarding silver during peacetime and selling during crisis = profit; some regions restrict silver trade (werewolf-controlled territories ban silver weapons)
- **Silver Allergy Mechanic**: if the player is a lycanthrope, touching silver items causes pain (1 damage per turn held); can't equip silver weapons; silver coins in inventory cause minor irritation messages; creates inventory management challenge
- **Silver Alternatives**: for non-silver options against supernaturals: blessed weapons (holy damage, requires priest), cold iron (effective against fey), garlic (vampire-specific); silver remains the most versatile
- **Silver Mines**: discoverable locations; player can claim and operate a silver mine; produces silver ingots passively; attracts lycanthrope raids (they want to shut it down)

## Acceptance Criteria

- [ ] Silver weapons deal correct bonus damage to supernatural creatures
- [ ] Silver crafting produces all listed item types from correct materials
- [ ] Market prices fluctuate based on regional supernatural activity
- [ ] Lycanthrope players suffer correct penalties from silver contact
- [ ] Silver mines produce resources and attract appropriate raids
