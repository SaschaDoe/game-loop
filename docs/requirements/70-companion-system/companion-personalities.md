# Companion Personalities

As a player, I want each companion NPC to have a distinct personality that affects their dialogue, combat behavior, and reactions to my decisions, so that companions feel like real characters rather than stat blocks.

## Details

- **Personality Traits** (each companion has 2-3 from this list):
  - **Brave**: charges into combat eagerly; volunteers for dangerous tasks; disappointed if player retreats; +10% damage when outnumbered
  - **Cautious**: suggests scouting ahead; warns about traps; disapproves of reckless actions; +15% trap detection when in party
  - **Greedy**: comments on loot; suggests looting everything; disapproves of giving away gold; +10% gold find when in party
  - **Compassionate**: heals injured NPCs; objects to harming innocents; suggests peaceful solutions; +10% healing effectiveness
  - **Sarcastic**: makes witty comments about situations via LLM; lightens mood during dark moments; can offend serious NPCs; +5% critical hit (comedic timing)
  - **Scholarly**: comments on lore, architecture, and history; identifies unknown items faster; suggests research-based solutions; +10% XP from discovering new locations
  - **Hot-headed**: starts fights easily; insults NPCs; impatient with puzzles; but loyal and protective; +15% damage on first strike
  - **Mysterious**: speaks cryptically; has hidden backstory that reveals over time; occasional prophetic hints about upcoming events; +10% magic resistance
- **Personality Interactions**: companions react to each other; compatible personalities bond (+party morale); clashing personalities argue (-party efficiency); rival companions may demand you choose between them
- **Approval System**: each companion tracks approval of your actions (-100 to +100); high approval unlocks personal quests, romance options, and combat synergies; low approval leads to arguments, refusal to follow orders, and eventually abandonment
- **LLM Dialogue**: companion dialogue generated by LLM based on their personality traits, current situation, approval level, and relationship history; companions comment on quests, locations, NPCs, and player decisions organically
- **Personality Growth**: companions can develop new traits through shared experiences; a cautious companion who survives enough battles may gain bravery; a greedy companion who witnesses suffering may develop compassion
- **Personality Clashes with Player**: certain player actions strongly affect specific personalities (killing innocents enrages compassionate companions; fleeing combat disgusts brave ones; wasting money horrifies greedy ones)

## Acceptance Criteria

- [ ] All personality traits produce correct behavioral patterns and bonuses
- [ ] Companion interactions reflect personality compatibility
- [ ] Approval system tracks decisions and unlocks content at correct thresholds
- [ ] LLM generates personality-consistent dialogue
- [ ] Personality growth triggers from correct shared experiences
