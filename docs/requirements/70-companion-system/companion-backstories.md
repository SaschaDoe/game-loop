# Companion Backstories

As a player, I want each recruitable companion to have a rich backstory that unfolds through dialogue, personal quests, and discovered lore, so that companions are narrative investments.

## Details

- **Backstory Reveal Pacing**: companions don't dump their history immediately; backstory reveals over 5 stages:
  1. **Introduction** (recruitment): basic name, class, surface motivation ("I need gold" / "I seek adventure")
  2. **Opening Up** (approval 20+): share childhood memories, how they learned their skills, why they're traveling
  3. **Trust** (approval 50+): reveal a painful secret (lost family, past crime, hidden identity, curse); unlocks first personal quest
  4. **Deep Bond** (approval 75+): share their deepest fear and ultimate goal; second personal quest chain
  5. **Full Truth** (approval 90+): final revelation (they're royalty in hiding, they're seeking revenge on a specific NPC, they're slowly dying from a curse); triggers climactic personal quest
- **Personal Quests**: each companion has 3 personal quests tied to their backstory:
  - Quest 1: resolve an immediate problem from their past (debt, pursuer, lost item)
  - Quest 2: confront a figure from their history (mentor, enemy, family member)
  - Quest 3: achieve or accept their ultimate goal/fate (reclaim throne, forgive betrayer, cure curse, accept mortality)
- **Backstory Integration**: companion backstories weave into the main world; their family member is a faction leader; their enemy is a quest-giver; their hometown is a visitable location; discovering these connections organically feels rewarding
- **Dialogue Triggers**: backstory conversations trigger from: reaching approval thresholds, visiting relevant locations, encountering related NPCs, resting at camp, or player asking directly
- **Companion Journals**: each companion has a readable journal in their inventory; entries added as backstory reveals; player can review missed dialogue through journal entries
- **Multiple Companions, Interwoven Stories**: some companion backstories intersect (two companions know each other from the past, one companion's enemy is another's ally); discovering these connections creates drama

## Acceptance Criteria

- [ ] Backstory reveals gate correctly behind approval thresholds
- [ ] Personal quests unlock at correct stages and resolve meaningfully
- [ ] Backstory NPCs and locations exist in the world for organic discovery
- [ ] Companion journals record all backstory conversations
- [ ] Intersecting backstories create correct dramatic interactions
