# Underground Civilization

As a player, I want to discover an entire underground civilization beneath the surface world, so that exploration reveals a hidden second world with its own culture, economy, and conflicts.

## Details

- The Underdark: a vast underground network of caverns, tunnels, and subterranean cities
- Underground races: Deep Dwarves, Mushroom Folk (Myconids), Shadow Elves, Cave Goblins
- Underground cities: carved into cavern walls, lit by bioluminescent fungi and crystal formations
- Unique economy: surface goods are rare and expensive underground; underground goods (gems, rare ores, fungal food) are cheap
- Political tension: underground factions distrust surface dwellers; earn trust through quests
- Environmental challenges: no natural light, toxic gas pockets, underground rivers and lakes
- Unique dungeon types: crystal caverns, fungal forests, lava tubes, drowned ruins
- Access points: hidden cave entrances, collapsed mines, portals in deep dungeons
- Underground-exclusive monsters: cave fishers, purple worms, mind flayers, crystal golems
- Trade routes between surface and underground are lucrative but dangerous

## Acceptance Criteria

- [ ] Underground world is accessible and explorable
- [ ] Underground cities have NPCs, shops, and quests
- [ ] Underground-exclusive content (monsters, items, lore) exists
- [ ] Surface/underground economy creates trade opportunities
