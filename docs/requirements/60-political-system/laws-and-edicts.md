# Laws and Edicts

As a player, I want kingdoms to have dynamic law systems that I can influence, exploit, or overthrow, so that the legal landscape is a living part of the world.

## Details

- **Law Categories**:
  - **Criminal Law**: defines crimes and punishments; theft (fine + jail), murder (execution or exile), smuggling (confiscation + jail), magic misuse (mana binding), treason (public execution)
  - **Trade Law**: tariffs, banned goods, merchant licensing, price controls; affects economy and player trading
  - **Social Law**: curfews, weapon restrictions in cities, racial discrimination, class privileges; affects daily gameplay
  - **Military Law**: conscription, desertion penalties, war declarations, mercenary regulations; affects combat options
  - **Religious Law**: approved religions, banned practices, tithe requirements, heresy definitions; affects worship and magic use
- **Law Variation**: each kingdom has its own legal code; what's legal in one kingdom may be criminal in another (e.g., necromancy legal in the dark kingdom, banned everywhere else)
- **Player Influence on Laws**:
  - As a noble/advisor: propose new laws or repeal existing ones through the court
  - As a rebel: overthrow the government and rewrite laws
  - As a merchant: lobby for trade-favorable laws using gold and favors
  - As a criminal: simply ignore laws and deal with consequences
- **Law Enforcement**: guards enforce laws within city walls; wilderness is lawless; enforcement quality varies (corrupt guards look the other way for bribes, zealous guards enforce every minor infraction)
- **Edicts**: temporary laws issued during crises (martial law during war, quarantine during plague, curfew during crime wave); edicts expire after the crisis passes or can become permanent
- **Legal Consequences**: crimes have escalating consequences (warning > fine > jail > exile > execution); players can hire lawyers (Persuasion-skilled NPCs) to reduce sentences; trial by combat available in some kingdoms

## Acceptance Criteria

- [ ] All law categories apply correct restrictions and penalties
- [ ] Laws vary between kingdoms appropriately
- [ ] Player influence mechanics change laws through correct channels
- [ ] Law enforcement varies by location and guard quality
- [ ] Edicts trigger during correct crises and expire appropriately
