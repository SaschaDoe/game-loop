# Companion Personal Quests

As a player, I want each recruitable companion to have a deep personal questline, so that investing in a companion rewards me with story, character development, and unique abilities.

## Details

- Each companion has a 3-part personal quest chain:
  - **Act 1**: companion reveals their problem/goal after reaching friendship level
  - **Act 2**: player helps investigate or pursue the goal (travel to a location, confront a person, gather information)
  - **Act 3**: climactic resolution with a meaningful choice that determines the outcome
- Examples:
  - **Kira the Ranger**: hunting the poacher who killed her wolf pack. Act 3: spare or kill the poacher (affects Kira's personality permanently)
  - **Dorn the Paladin**: his order is corrupt. Act 3: reform the order, destroy it, or join the corruption
  - **Zara the Mage**: her sister is trapped in a lich's phylactery. Act 3: free the sister (weakening Zara) or absorb the power (strengthening Zara but losing the sister)
  - **Pip the Thief**: owes a massive debt to the Thieves Guild. Act 3: pay it off, assassinate the guild master, or turn Pip in
- Completing a companion quest unlocks their ultimate ability
- Quest outcome permanently changes the companion's personality, dialogue, and combat behavior
- Failing or ignoring a companion's quest may cause them to leave the party

## Acceptance Criteria

- [ ] Each companion has a 3-act personal questline
- [ ] Player choices in Act 3 produce distinct outcomes
- [ ] Ultimate abilities unlock on completion
- [ ] Companion personality changes reflect quest outcome
