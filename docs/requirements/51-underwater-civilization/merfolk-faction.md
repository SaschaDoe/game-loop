# Merfolk Faction

As a player, I want to interact with a merfolk civilization that has its own politics, quests, and culture, so that underwater exploration has deep narrative content.

## Details

- **Merfolk Society**:
  - Ruled by the Tide Council (5 elders, each representing a sea domain: Shallows, Reef, Open Sea, Depths, Abyss)
  - Caste system: warriors (sharks), artisans (octopi motif), scholars (jellyfish), scouts (dolphins), outcasts (anglerfish — deep-sea exiles)
  - Capital city: Pelagos, built in a massive underwater cavern with air pockets for surface visitors
- **Merfolk Quests**:
  - **Diplomatic**: mediate a border dispute between merfolk and surface fishing villages
  - **Military**: defend the reef against a kraken incursion or pirate raiders
  - **Exploration**: map uncharted deep-sea trenches; recover lost artifacts from the Abyssal Fortress
  - **Political**: choose which elder to support in a succession crisis; each choice reshapes merfolk policy toward surface-dwellers
  - **Cultural**: participate in the Tidal Festival (underwater race, coral sculpture contest, bioluminescent light show)
- **Merfolk Relations**: initial disposition depends on player's actions toward the sea (pollution, overfishing = hostile; sea monster kills, gift-giving = friendly)
- **Merfolk Companions**: recruit a merfolk warrior or scholar as a companion; they struggle on land (movement penalty) but excel underwater
- **Merfolk Language**: unique language learned through immersion; partial understanding at first, fluency unlocks deeper dialogue options
- **Trade Goods**: merfolk trade pearls, coral, waterbreathing potions, enchanted shells; they value surface metals, books, and fire-related curiosities
- **Merfolk Magic**: tide magic (control currents), coral shaping (grow structures), deep song (sonar communication, echolocation in dark water)

## Acceptance Criteria

- [ ] Merfolk society is explorable with all castes present
- [ ] All merfolk quest types are completable
- [ ] Player actions toward the sea affect merfolk disposition
- [ ] Merfolk companions function with land/water trade-offs
- [ ] Merfolk language learning progresses through interaction
