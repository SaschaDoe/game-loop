# Coral Crafting

As a player, I want to harvest and shape living coral into weapons, armor, and structures, so that underwater crafting has a unique organic material system.

## Details

- **Coral Types**:
  - **Fire Coral**: red, deals burn damage when touched; crafted into fire-resistant armor and heat weapons; found near volcanic vents
  - **Brain Coral**: patterned, mildly psychic; crafted into INT-boosting headgear and telepathy focus items; found in deep reef
  - **Staghorn Coral**: branching, strong and light; crafted into spears, tridents, and framework for structures; common reef material
  - **Black Coral**: dark, absorbs light; crafted into stealth armor and shadow weapons; found in midnight zone; rare
  - **Singing Coral**: vibrates with harmonic frequencies; crafted into instruments, sonic weapons, and communication devices; found near merfolk cities
  - **Living Coral**: still alive when harvested; can be grown into custom shapes (living armor that heals itself, growing walls for player housing); requires coral cultivation skill
- **Coral Harvesting**: use a coral knife (special tool); Perception check to identify coral types; careless harvesting damages the reef (angers merfolk, reduces future yield)
- **Coral Shaping**: combine coral at an underwater workbench; coral + binding agent (kelp sap) + heat (volcanic vent or fire magic) = shaped coral item
- **Coral Properties**: lighter than metal, doesn't rust, naturally buoyant (reduces encumbrance underwater), degrades on land unless treated (waterproofing wax)
- **Living Coral Structures**: grow coral walls, bridges, and barriers underwater; takes 50 turns to grow; can be shaped while growing; permanent and self-repairing
- **Coral Enchantment**: coral absorbs magical energy naturally; items made from coral have +1 enchantment slot compared to metal equivalents
- **Ecological Impact**: overharvesting coral bleaches the reef; bleached reefs have no fish, no resources, and merfolk reputation drops; reefs recover over 500 turns if left alone

## Acceptance Criteria

- [ ] All coral types have unique properties and craft into correct items
- [ ] Harvesting uses correct skill checks and tools
- [ ] Living coral structures grow over time at correct rates
- [ ] Coral items degrade on land without waterproofing
- [ ] Ecological impact tracks harvesting and reef health
