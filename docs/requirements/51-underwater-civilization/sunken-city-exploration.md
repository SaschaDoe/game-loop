# Sunken City Exploration

As a player, I want to discover and explore a sunken civilization beneath the ocean, so that underwater areas feel like fully realized worlds rather than just obstacles.

## Details

- **Sunken City Types**:
  - **Coral Metropolis**: living city built into a massive coral reef; merfolk inhabitants, bioluminescent lighting, kelp gardens, pearl currency
  - **Drowned Ruins**: human city that sank centuries ago; air pockets in sealed buildings, waterlogged loot, undead drowned sailors, collapsed architecture puzzles
  - **Abyssal Fortress**: deep-sea stronghold built by an ancient race; pressure effects (deeper = more dangerous), alien architecture, eldritch guardians, void-touched artifacts
  - **Volcanic Vent Settlement**: city built around thermal vents; warm water zones, obsidian buildings, fire-resistant sea creatures, rare mineral deposits
- **Underwater Navigation**:
  - 3D movement: swim up/down in addition to cardinal directions; vertical dungeon design
  - Current system: water currents push the player in specific directions; can ride them for speed or fight against them (stamina cost)
  - Pressure zones: deeper areas deal damage without pressure-resistant gear; gear tiers unlock deeper exploration
  - Light fades with depth: surface (full vision) → twilight zone (reduced) → midnight zone (bioluminescence only) → abyss (zero without light source)
- **Underwater Economy**: pearl currency, coral crafting materials, waterproof enchantments (regular items degrade in water without protection)
- **Air Management**: breath meter is critical (see oxygen-and-breathing); air refill stations, air bubble spells, gill potions
- **Underwater Mounts**: giant seahorse, manta ray, dolphin, submarine construct (magitech)
- **Unique Loot**: tridents, coral armor, pressure-resistant gear, bioluminescent potions, waterbreathing enchantments

## Acceptance Criteria

- [ ] All sunken city types are explorable with unique layouts
- [ ] 3D underwater navigation works with vertical movement
- [ ] Pressure zones deal depth-based damage without correct gear
- [ ] Light diminishes with depth correctly
- [ ] Underwater economy uses pearl currency
