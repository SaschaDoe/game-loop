# Seasons and Harvest

As a player, I want crops and farming to follow seasonal cycles with planting, growing, and harvesting phases, so that farming rewards patience and planning.

## Details

- **Seasonal Calendar**: Spring (planting) → Summer (growing) → Autumn (harvest) → Winter (dormant)
- **Crop Growth Stages**: Seed → Sprout → Growing → Mature → Harvestable → Overripe (quality degrades)
- **Seasonal Crops**:
  - Spring: wheat (bread ingredient), carrots (HP food), herbs (alchemy)
  - Summer: tomatoes (cooking), sunflowers (oil ingredient), cotton (cloth crafting)
  - Autumn: pumpkins (festival ingredient, high value), grapes (wine making), mushrooms (alchemy)
  - Winter: nothing grows outdoors; greenhouse required (expensive to build, allows year-round farming)
- **Farming Actions**: till soil (hoe) → plant seeds → water daily → weed occasionally → harvest when mature
- **Crop Quality**: affected by watering consistency, soil quality, fertilizer use, weather; Poor → Average → Good → Excellent → Perfect
- **Threats**: drought (water more often), pests (set scarecrows/traps), disease (apply pesticide or lose crop), theft (fence or guard)
- **Harvest Festival**: end of autumn, town celebration; player can enter crops in competition (quality judged, prizes for best crops)
- **Crop Processing**: raw crops → processed goods (wheat → flour → bread; grapes → wine; cotton → cloth)
- **Profit**: farming is a reliable income source; rare crop varieties (found as quest rewards or bought from traveling merchants) yield premium prices
- **Farm Upgrades**: irrigation system (auto-water), scarecrow (deters pests), greenhouse (winter farming), hired farmhand NPC (tends crops while you adventure)

## Acceptance Criteria

- [ ] Crops follow correct seasonal planting/growth/harvest cycles
- [ ] Crop quality is affected by listed factors
- [ ] Threats impact crops and are preventable
- [ ] Harvest festival competition is functional
- [ ] Farm upgrades provide correct automation benefits
