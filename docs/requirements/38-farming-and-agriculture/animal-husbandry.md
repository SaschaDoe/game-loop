# Animal Husbandry

As a player, I want to raise livestock on my farm for resources and profit, so that farming extends beyond crops into a full homesteading experience.

## Details

- Animal types:
  - **Chickens**: produce eggs daily (cooking ingredient), low maintenance, cheap
  - **Cows**: produce milk daily, can be slaughtered for meat and leather, moderate maintenance
  - **Sheep**: produce wool (crafting material for cloth armor), moderate maintenance
  - **Pigs**: produce nothing daily but valuable meat when slaughtered, eat anything
  - **Bees**: produce honey (alchemy ingredient, cooking buff), require a hive structure
  - **Rare**: phoenix chick (produces fire feathers, alchemy material), moon rabbit (produces moonstone shards)
- Animals require: shelter (build a barn), feed (crops or purchased grain), care (interact to maintain happiness)
- Happy animals produce more; neglected animals produce less or die
- Breeding: pair animals to produce offspring, rare trait inheritance system
- Predator attacks: wolves or goblins may raid your farm (build fences, hire guard)
- Animals can be named; companion-like attachment (NPCs comment on your animals)

## Acceptance Criteria

- [ ] Animals are purchasable and produce resources
- [ ] Happiness system affects production
- [ ] Breeding produces offspring with inherited traits
- [ ] Predator raids occur and are defensible
