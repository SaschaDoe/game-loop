# Vampire Society

As a player, I want to discover and navigate a hidden vampire society with its own politics, ranks, and conflicts, so that vampirism opens an entirely new social world.

## Details

- **Vampire Courts**: each major city has a hidden vampire court; located in mansions, catacombs, or abandoned theaters; accessible only to vampires or those with vampire contacts
- **Court Hierarchy**:
  - **Prince/Princess**: rules the city's vampires; absolute authority; assigns territory, settles disputes, declares wars; player can challenge for leadership (combat or political)
  - **Primogen Council**: advisors to the prince; one per vampire bloodline; influence court decisions; can be joined through bloodline loyalty
  - **Sheriff**: enforces vampire law (the Masquerade — keeping vampire existence secret from mortals); hunts rogue vampires; player can be appointed sheriff
  - **Neonates**: newly turned vampires; lowest rank; must prove themselves through service; player starts here
- **Vampire Laws (The Masquerade)**:
  - Never reveal vampirism to mortals (penalty: blood hunt — all vampires ordered to kill you)
  - Never feed publicly (penalty: territory restriction, forced starvation period)
  - Never turn a mortal without the Prince's permission (penalty: both sire and childe destroyed)
  - Never kill another vampire without sanctioned cause (penalty: blood hunt)
- **Vampire Bloodlines**: 5 bloodlines with different specialties:
  - **Sanguis**: social manipulation; bonus CHA, Mesmerize enhanced
  - **Nosfera**: monstrous appearance; bonus STR, terrifying presence, can't pass as human
  - **Umbra**: shadow manipulation; bonus stealth, shadow magic, disappear in darkness
  - **Bestia**: animal kinship; command animals, beast form enhanced, feral fighting style
  - **Mentis**: psychic power; bonus INT, telepathy, telekinesis, mind reading
- **Vampire Politics**: bloodlines compete for influence; player can ally with a bloodline, play them against each other, or try to unite them; political quests involve espionage, assassination, and diplomacy
- **Vampire-Hunter Threat**: mortal vampire hunters periodically threaten the court; player helps defend or can secretly aid the hunters (betrayal path)

## Acceptance Criteria

- [ ] Masquerade violations trigger correct punishments
- [ ] Bloodlines provide correct bonuses and specializations
