# Time Periods

As a player, I want to travel to distinct historical eras of the game world, each with unique environments, NPCs, and challenges, so that time travel provides rich and varied exploration.

## Details

- **Available Eras**:
  - **Primordial Age**: the world's creation; raw elemental chaos; no civilization; massive primordial creatures roam; extreme environmental danger; rare elemental resources unavailable in other eras
  - **Age of Dragons**: dragons rule the world; dragonborn civilization at its peak; dragon riders, aerial cities, hoards everywhere; non-dragon races are enslaved or hiding; dragon-scale equipment abundant
  - **Golden Empire**: a vast magical civilization at its zenith; advanced magitech, floating cities, portal networks; powerful artifacts available in shops; but strict magical law enforcement
  - **The Cataclysm**: the event that destroyed the Golden Empire; war, magical disaster, dimensional tears; extreme danger but unique loot from the chaos; time-limited (must escape before the final detonation)
  - **Dark Ages**: post-Cataclysm collapse; small settlements, fearful populace, monster-infested wilderness; resources scarce but survival skills valued; rebuilding quests
  - **Present Day**: the current game world; baseline era; all normal gameplay
  - **Far Future**: 1000 years ahead; outcome depends on player's present-day actions; could be utopia, wasteland, or alien invasion; unique future-tech items
- **Era-Specific Mechanics**: each era has unique rules (Primordial Age has no shops/NPCs, Golden Empire has anti-violence wards in cities, Dark Ages has extreme hunger/thirst rates)
- **Cross-Era Quests**: some quests span multiple eras (plant a seed in the past, harvest the ancient tree in the present; hide an item in the Dark Ages, find the ruin containing it in the present)
- **Era Travel Method**: requires a Chrono-Compass (rare artifact) + temporal crystal fuel; each jump costs 1 crystal; crystals found in temporal rifts or crafted from paradox energy
- **Butterfly Effects**: major actions in past eras change the present; NPC lineages, city layouts, faction power; changes visible when returning to present

## Acceptance Criteria

- [ ] All eras have distinct environments, NPCs, and resources
- [ ] Era-specific mechanics apply correct rule modifications
- [ ] Cross-era quests track objectives across timelines correctly
- [ ] Butterfly effects modify the present based on past actions
- [ ] Era travel requires correct items and resources
