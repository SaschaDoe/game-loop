# Teachers and School Life

The faculty aren't quest dispensers. They're people with histories, secrets, relationships with each other, and lives outside the classroom. School life extends beyond lessons — rituals, meals, assemblies, and daily rhythms make the Academy feel lived-in.

---

## Faculty Deep Dives

### US-AA-96: Professor Ignis — The Scarred Idealist

**Full Name:** Professor Corvan Ignis
**Age:** 52
**Teaches:** School of Elements (House Pyraclaw head)
**Office:** Ground floor of the Great Hall, east side. The door is warm to the touch. Scorch marks on the ceiling.

**Backstory:**
Ignis was a battle mage in the Border Wars before becoming a teacher. He fought for three years defending the Conservatory region from incursions. His hands are scarred not from teaching accidents but from a single moment: he held a collapsing ward with his bare hands to let civilians evacuate a burning village. The ward was made of pure fire. He held it for forty seconds. He saved eleven people and lost the feeling in three fingers.

He became a teacher because "destroying things is easy. Teaching someone NOT to destroy things — that's hard. That's worth doing."

**Secret:** Ignis has been secretly researching "void-flame" — a theoretical element that combines destruction (Energy stream) with unmaking (Void). He found references in a pre-Ascension text. If void-flame exists, it would be the ultimate weapon — and the ultimate prohibition. He hasn't told anyone. His research notes are hidden behind a loose brick in his office.

**Teaching Style:** Demonstration-first. "Watch me do it. Now try. If it explodes, we'll talk about why." He never punishes students for making mistakes — only for not trying. He gets genuinely excited when students surprise him.

**Relationships:**
- **With Ashveil:** Mutual respect, constant bickering. "She thinks magic should be controlled. I think magic should be FELT. We're both right." They argue at faculty meetings but always sit next to each other at dinner.
- **With Thornwick:** Fond friendship. Ignis brings Thornwick rare minerals from the practice dungeon. Thornwick brings Ignis herbal tea that "calms the fire in your hands" (it actually helps his pain).
- **With Maren:** Admiration. "She taught herself divination. HERSELF. I had three tutors and a war to learn fire. She just... looked harder."
- **With Veylen:** Loyalty. "The old man carries more than he shows. I've offered to share the weight. He always says 'not yet.'"

**Unique Interactions:**
- If the player visits his office after dark, they might catch him wincing and flexing his hands. "Old injuries. The fire remembers, even when I don't."
- If the player asks about the Border Wars: a rare moment of gravity. "I don't talk about the wars. But I'll tell you this — every fire spell I teach, I teach because I know what they can do. To people. To cities. Learn them. Then pray you never need them."
- If the player helps him (Professor's Pet quest): he trusts the player enough to mention void-flame. "There's a seventh element. Not in any textbook. I'm not sure it's safe to teach. But I'm not sure it's safe NOT to."

### US-AA-97: Professor Thornwick — The Gentle Alchemist

**Full Name:** Professor Bramwell Thornwick
**Age:** 67
**Teaches:** School of Alchemy (House Verdantia head)
**Office:** Upper floor of the Alchemy Tower, connected to the greenhouse.

**Backstory:**
Thornwick was a healer in a small village before the Academy recruited him. He's never been to war, never been in a duel, never cast an offensive spell. His entire life has been about growing things, healing things, and understanding how substances combine.

He came to the Academy forty years ago, the same year as Maren. They arrived as students together. He stayed because "the greenhouse needs me. Also, I tried to leave once and the mandrakes cried. I can't do that to them."

**Secret:** Thornwick talks to plants — and some of them talk back. Specifically, the East Wing specimens in his hidden greenhouse compartment. They communicate through patterns in their growth — words formed in leaf-veins, images in root-patterns. One plant has been slowly spelling out a message for twenty years. He's transcribed it: "THE SINGER IS NOT DEAD. THE CAGE DOES NOT HOLD WHAT YOU THINK." He doesn't understand it. He's afraid to ask anyone.

**Teaching Style:** Gentle, patient, never raises his voice. Will sit with a struggling student for hours. Gives second chances, third chances, infinite chances. Gets quietly emotional when a student brews their first successful potion. "Every potion is a small miracle. Don't forget that."

**Relationships:**
- **With Ignis:** Warm friendship. They eat lunch together every day. Thornwick is the only person who's seen Ignis cry (about the war).
- **With Ashveil:** Cordial distance. "She's brilliant. But she treats magic like a machine. Magic is alive. You can't precision-engineer a flower."
- **With Maren:** Deep bond. They were students together. He brings her tea every evening. She brings him a new book every month. Neither has ever discussed this arrangement out loud.
- **With Veylen:** Trust. Thornwick is the one faculty member who knows about the ancient ward (Veylen told him because Thornwick's greenhouse sits closest to the East Wing cracks).

**Unique Interactions:**
- If the player visits the greenhouse at dawn: Thornwick is singing to the plants. Off-key. "Don't tell anyone. They grow better when you sing. The plants, I mean. The students... probably also, but I haven't tested that."
- If asked about the East Wing plants: first deflection, then (after Verdantia quest or high friendship): "They're not normal plants. They think. I've been listening to what they say. I'll show you — but not yet. Not until you're ready."
- If the player brews a potion perfectly on the first try: a tear in his eye. "Celestine would be proud. She always said alchemy was the people's magic."

### US-AA-98: Professor Ashveil — The Precise Guardian

**Full Name:** Professor Seraphina Ashveil
**Age:** 45
**Teaches:** School of Enchantment (House Ironveil head)
**Office:** Rear of the Enchantment Lab. The desk is immaculate. Three quills, parallel. One chair. No decoration.

**Backstory:**
Ashveil was a ward-architect for the Mages' Guild — one of the elite specialists who build and maintain large-scale protective barriers. City walls, prison wards, diplomatic shielding. She was considered the best of her generation.

She left the Guild after she was ordered to build a ward that she knew was designed to imprison someone unjustly. She refused, was expelled, and came to the Academy to teach. "I build walls. I'd like to teach the next generation to build better ones — ones that protect the right people."

**Secret:** Ashveil monitors the Sealed East Wing wards weekly. She hasn't told anyone, but she's discovered they're degrading — not from age, but from something pushing OUTWARD. Whatever Aldric's experiment created or contacted, it's getting stronger. She's been quietly reinforcing the wards for five years. Alone. Without telling Veylen, because she doesn't want to frighten him. She is exhausted.

**Teaching Style:** Demanding. No praise unless earned. Makes students repeat ward exercises until perfection. But she stays after class with anyone who asks for help — and she adjusts her expectations for each student's actual level. She's hard because wards that fail get people killed.

**Relationships:**
- **With Ignis:** Constant arguments that neither takes personally. "Ignis thinks magic should be felt. I think magic that's 'felt' ends in third-degree burns." They secretly admire each other.
- **With Thornwick:** Respect for his patience, frustration with his softness. "He gives too many chances. Sometimes a student needs to hear 'do better' instead of 'try again.'"
- **With Maren:** Complicated. Ashveil knows Maren is hiding something about the library. Maren knows Ashveil is hiding something about the East Wing. Neither confronts the other. They exchange knowing looks at faculty meetings.
- **With Veylen:** Strained. She thinks Veylen should address the East Wing problem. He thinks she should trust his judgment. They're both right and both too proud to admit it.

**Unique Interactions:**
- If the player visits her office at night: she's not there. She's at the East Wing door, checking wards. If caught: "I... routine inspection. Don't concern yourself."
- If the player completes the Ironveil house quest: she drops her mask for exactly one sentence. "Thank you. I've been doing this alone for a long time." Then the mask returns. "That will be all."
- If a player consistently shows up to practice: the highest praise she gives: "Adequate." But Rod translates: "She said you were adequate! That's the best thing she's ever said to anyone!"

### US-AA-99: Librarian Maren — The Keeper of Secrets

**Full Name:** Maren Halcyon
**Age:** 62
**Teaches:** School of Divination (informal), House Glimmershade head
**Office:** The desk just inside the library entrance. The restricted section key hangs around her neck.

**Backstory:**
Maren arrived at the Academy as a student with no magical talent. Zero. She couldn't cast, couldn't brew, couldn't ward, couldn't light a candle. She should have been expelled by Day 10.

Instead, she sat in the library and read. Everything. Every book. Every scroll. Every faded manuscript. And somewhere in that ocean of text, she found a passage about divination — a form of magic that doesn't require power. Only attention. "Divination isn't about channeling energy. It's about noticing what's already there."

She taught herself True Sight from a book. She taught herself Reveal Secrets from a footnote. She taught herself Foresight from a dream journal she found pressed between two atlases. She never graduated — she was offered the librarian position before her exam year, because the previous librarian died and Maren was the only one who knew where everything was.

She's been here forty years. She knows every book's location. She knows which books whisper at night. She knows things she shouldn't know.

**Secret:** Maren has read Celestine's journal. She knows about the ward, the compact, and the founding's true purpose. She also knows that she is, technically, the Academy's current guardian — the one who maintains the compact. Veylen doesn't know this. Maren has been protecting the ward by ensuring the library's restricted section is never truly breached. The books in there are arranged in a specific pattern that forms a secondary ward-layer — a ward made of knowledge. If the books are moved, the ward weakens. This is why she's so protective of the restricted section.

**Teaching Style:** Socratic. She asks questions instead of answering them. "What did you see?" "What do you think it means?" "And what would you see if you looked again?" Her lessons feel like conversations. Students leave knowing more than when they arrived, but they're not sure how.

**Relationships:**
- **With Thornwick:** Deep, unspoken bond. Forty years of tea and books. She's the only person he'd trust with his plant-communication secret. She's never told him her secret either. Someday they'll trade truths. Not today.
- **With Ignis:** Amusement. "He burns things. I catalogue them. We're complementary."
- **With Ashveil:** Mutual awareness. They both guard secrets. They both know the other guards secrets. An unspoken truce.
- **With Veylen:** She was here before him. She'll be here after him. He respects her in a way that borders on fear. When he has a problem he can't solve, he goes to the library. He doesn't ask her directly. He just sits at a table. She puts a book in front of him. The book always has the answer.

**Unique Interactions:**
- If the player returns the overdue book (Library Card quest) AND reads it before returning: she smiles. "You read it first. Good. I was hoping you would."
- If the player sits in the library without talking to anyone for 20+ turns: Maren approaches. "You're listening. Most students don't listen. The books appreciate it." She teaches a minor passive: "Library Silence" — +1 sight radius while in any library area.
- If the player completes Glimmershade's house quest: "Lumi told you what she saw. Do you know what the pool showed me, forty years ago? This conversation. Exactly this conversation."

## School Life and Rituals

### US-AA-100: The Opening Ceremony
**As a** new student, **I attend** the opening ceremony on Day 1, **so that** I feel the weight and tradition of joining the Academy.

**Sequence:**
1. All students gather in the Great Hall
2. Veylen gives a speech about the Academy's mission ("Magic belongs to all")
3. Faculty are introduced — each professor gives a one-line greeting
4. The Sorting Ceremony follows (see US-AA-89)
5. Veylen lights the Great Hall's chandelier using the Eternal Flame — "Let the school year begin"
6. Students are dismissed to their house common rooms for the first time

### US-AA-101: Morning Assemblies
**As a** student, **I attend** brief morning assemblies in the Great Hall on lesson days, **so that** the daily rhythm of school is established.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- On lesson days, a brief Veylen announcement plays when the player enters the Great Hall in the morning
- Announcements include: lesson reminders, upcoming events, house point standings, and occasionally cryptic one-liners ("Be mindful of the greenhouse. Professor Thornwick reports the roses are biting again.")
- Attendance is not mandatory — the player can skip, but misses flavor and occasional quest hooks
- Other students are visibly present in the hall during assembly (background NPCs)

### US-AA-102: The Common Room as Social Hub
**As a** student, **I experience** my house common room as a living social space, **so that** the house feels like home.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Visiting the common room in the evening triggers brief social scenes:
  - **Pyraclaw:** Students sparring by the fire. Sera challenges someone to arm-wrestle. Rod is losing at a card game.
  - **Verdantia:** Quiet study session. Moss is repotting something. A student is reading aloud from an alchemy text.
  - **Ironveil:** Organized study group. Wrenn is quizzing other students on ward theory. Dorian is correcting someone's notes (uninvited).
  - **Glimmershade:** Candle-lit silence. Lumi is staring at the scrying pool. A student is dream-journaling. Someone is asleep on a cushion.
- These are brief message sequences, not full dialogues — atmospheric flavor
- Visiting daily builds a sense of routine and belonging

### US-AA-103: Meals and the Dining Hall
**As a** student, **I eat** meals in the Great Hall at meal times, **so that** school life includes communal moments.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Great Hall serves as dining hall during meal turns (turns 20-30 and 70-80 per day)
- During meals, tables are set up (change in map tiles)
- Students sit by house (Pyraclaw at the fire-end, Glimmershade near the windows, etc.)
- Interacting with the food tile heals 3 HP (once per meal) — "Hot stew. Simple, but filling."
- Dining triggers random social events:
  - Rod spills something. Again.
  - Elara eats alone at the end of her table. If friendship 3+, she waves you over.
  - Dorian makes a loud comment about someone's house. Sera shoots back.
  - Fenn reads the notice board postings aloud in a dramatic voice.
  - A background student asks you a question about the last lesson (flavor dialogue)
- One meal per week, Veylen dines with the students. His dialogue is always a subtle hint about something.

### US-AA-104: Curfew and Night Exploration
**As a** student, **I can** explore campus after curfew, **so that** rule-breaking yields discovery and risk.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Curfew: turns 90-100 of each day (night phase)
- Being caught outside your common room by a "patrolling" faculty message: -5 house points, stern warning
- Being caught twice: short detention (locked in common room for one morning — miss assembly)
- NOT being caught: free to explore — some secrets only available at night:
  - Dorian practicing alone in the arena
  - The library ghost sounds
  - The fountain's midnight Ley Line map
  - Ashveil checking the East Wing wards
  - Thornwick singing to his plants
  - The courtyard stars (Glimmershade pool sometimes reflects them differently at night)
- Rogue class gets a stealth bonus for avoiding detection after curfew

### US-AA-105: The School Rules
**As a** student, **I am aware** of school rules that I can follow or break, **so that** discipline adds a layer of consequence.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Displayed on a notice in the dormitory:
  1. "Students must attend all scheduled lessons."
  2. "The restricted section is off-limits without faculty permission."
  3. "The practice dungeon is available during daylight hours only." (breakable — night dungeon runs are harder)
  4. "The Sealed East Wing is absolutely forbidden."
  5. "Respect all faculty and fellow students."
  6. "Curfew is strictly enforced."
- Breaking rules has consequences (house point loss, detention, scolding)
- But some quests REQUIRE breaking rules (restricted section, night exploration)
- Fenn's commentary: "Rules are just suggestions with consequences. The question is whether the consequences are worth it."

### US-AA-106: Background Student Population
**As a** student, **I see** other unnamed students around campus, **so that** the school feels populated, not empty.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- 6-10 generic NPC students placed around campus (chars: `s`, various colors matching houses)
- Generic students have 1-2 lines of dialogue each, cycling based on lesson progress:
  - "Did you understand the ward lesson? I'm still confused."
  - "I heard the practice dungeon has new monsters this week."
  - "House [X] is leading in points. How?"
  - "The exam is coming. I'm not ready."
- Students are positioned in house-appropriate locations (Pyraclaw students near the arena, etc.)
- Creates the feel of a REAL student body, not just 5 named characters
- Some background students wear house colors (color-coded to match their house)

### US-AA-107: End-of-Year Assembly and House Cup
**As a** student, **I attend** the end-of-year ceremony where the House Cup is awarded and the school year closes, **so that** the house competition resolves and the school year has a proper ending.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Triggered after graduation ceremony (if graduated) or on Day 30 (if not graduated)
- Veylen announces final house point standings
- Winning house gets a visible celebration (house rep reacts)
- Player receives house cup bonus item if their house wins (see US-AA-92)
- Each classmate has a final speech:
  - Rod: "Best year of my life. Even the explosions."
  - Elara: "I learned more outside the classroom than in it. Thank you... for being there."
  - Fenn: "I'm writing a book about this year. Don't worry — I'll change the names. ...Mostly."
  - Dorian: (if respect earned) "You were a worthy rival. I won't forget that." / (if hostile) "This isn't over."
  - Sera/Moss/Wrenn/Lumi: house-specific farewells
- Veylen's closing: "The school year ends, but the Academy endures. What you learned here stays with you. What you discovered... well, some discoveries never stop unfolding."
