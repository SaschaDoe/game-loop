# School History (Discoverable Lore)

The Academy has 847 years of history. None of it is dumped on the player. All of it is discoverable through exploration, quests, conversations, and reading. Curious players are rewarded with deep lore that connects to the game's central mysteries.

---

## Lore Discovery Layers

### Layer 1: Surface History (Available to All Students)

### US-AA-81: The Official History
**As a** student, **I learn** the Academy's public history through campus elements, **so that** the school feels established and real.

**Discovery Methods:**
- Library book: "A Brief History of the Arcane Academy"
- Hallway portraits with name plaques
- Professor dialogue (each mentions the school's heritage)
- Veylen's welcome speech mentions Celestine Voss

**Content:**
- Founded 847 years ago by Archmage Celestine Voss
- Open to all — the first school to accept non-mage students
- Built on a Ley Line convergence that makes magic easier to learn
- The East Wing was sealed 200 years ago after "a magical accident"
- 6 headmasters since the founding, current is Archmagus Veylen
- Notable alumni include healers, battle mages, and one famous thief (Fenn loves this detail)

### US-AA-82: The Incident (Official Version)
**As a** student, **I learn** the sanitized version of what happened to the East Wing, **so that** a mystery is established.

**Discovery Methods:**
- Library book: "The Incident of Year 647"
- Professor Ashveil mentions it when asked about the sealed door
- The book in the library alcove (student journal) contradicts the official version

**Official Version:**
- A student named Aldric attempted an "unauthorized experiment"
- The experiment caused an "arcane overload"
- The East Wing was destroyed; Archmage Thessian died containing the damage
- The wing was sealed "for safety"
- Aldric "perished in the explosion"

### Layer 2: Hidden History (Requires Quests/Investigation)

### US-AA-83: The Student Journal
**As an** investigator, **I find** the student journal in the hidden library alcove that tells the real story of the Incident, **so that** the official version is questioned.

**Discovery Method:** Quest US-AA-60 "The Ghost in the Library" OR secret wall detection in library

**Content:**
- Written by a student named Kira, Aldric's lab partner
- Aldric wasn't unauthorized — Thessian secretly supported his research
- The experiment was a cross-school spell: drawing from all four schools simultaneously
- "For three seconds, Aldric was surrounded by light that contained all colors. Then the light turned WRONG."
- "I heard screaming — not Aldric's. Something else. Something that didn't want him to succeed."
- "They told us he died. But I saw his face in that light. He wasn't dying. He was SEEING."
- Kira was silenced by the new headmaster. The journal is all that remains of her testimony.

### US-AA-84: Celestine's Journal
**As a** restricted section visitor, **I read** Celestine Voss's private journal, **so that** the founding's true purpose is revealed.

**Discovery Method:** Quest US-AA-55 "The Forbidden Section" or post-graduation access

**Content:**
- Celestine didn't stumble on the location — she was guided by a recurring dream
- The dream showed a hill that hummed, and beneath it, a light older than the world
- "I built the school to protect the ward. The school is the cover story. The ward is the mission."
- She refers to a "compact" — the Academy stands as long as the ward is guarded
- She describes the ward: "Two streams, woven together. Not Order. Not Change. Both. Neither. Something from Before."
- Her final entry: "I don't know who made the ward. I don't know what it contains. I only know that if it fails, something fundamental changes. Perhaps for the better. Perhaps not. My job is not to decide. My job is to guard."

### US-AA-85: Aldric's Research Notes
**As a** restricted section visitor, **I read** Aldric's mathematical proofs, **so that** cross-school magic is established as possible.

**Discovery Method:** Restricted section access

**Content:**
- Dense, technical notes showing that the four spell schools are artificial divisions
- "The streams don't want to be separate. You can feel it when you cast — the fire wants to become a ward, the ward wants to become a vision. The boundaries are imposed, not natural."
- His key insight: "The Ley Lines predate the schools. Predate the traditions. Predate... everything the professors teach. What if the 'four schools' are just a convenience? What if magic is one thing, and we've been taught to see it as four?"
- His final note: "The experiment is ready. If I'm right, I'll prove that magic has no boundaries. If I'm wrong, I'll only destroy one wing. Acceptable odds."
- These notes connect directly to the game's lore about the Original Seven and the Ascended's control over magic

### Layer 3: Deep Secrets (Endgame / Post-Graduation)

### US-AA-86: The Ancient Ward
**As an** explorer of the sub-basement, **I encounter** the ancient ward and receive a vision, **so that** the Academy's deepest secret is hinted at.

**Discovery Method:** Quest US-AA-56 "Beneath the Academy"

**Content:**
- The ward is described through sensory experience, not exposition:
  - "The air tastes of copper and starlight."
  - "You hear a sound below hearing — a hum that vibrates in your bones."
  - "The sigil in the bedrock pulses once. Twice. As if it's breathing."
- Vision (triggered on first visit): a flash of impossible images:
  - Seven figures standing in a circle, sacrificing something into the earth
  - The same seven figures fading, becoming the ground, the sky, the rivers
  - A light in the earth — warm, persistent, patient
  - A crack in the light, and something cold seeping in
- The player receives no explanation. Just the vision. Interpretation comes later.
- **Mechanical effect:** "Ley Line Attunement" — permanent +2 max mana

### US-AA-87: The Convergence Prophecy (Academy Version)
**As a** reader of restricted books, **I discover** the Academy's version of the Convergence Prophecy, **so that** the endgame plot thread is seeded.

**Discovery Method:** Restricted section book OR Elara dialogue at friendship 6+

**Content:**
- "When the four schools speak as one voice, the sealed door opens and the singer returns."
- Faculty dismiss this as poetry. Elara does not.
- Elara's theory: "Aldric proved the four schools can be unified. The prophecy says someone will do it successfully. The 'sealed door' isn't just the East Wing. It's the barrier between what we're allowed to know and what's really there."
- This prophecy is a variant of the broader Convergence Prophecy (lore doc 06, doc 14)
- Player cannot act on this during the Academy arc — it's a seed for late-game content

### US-AA-88: Aldric's Echo
**As an** East Wing explorer (post-graduation), **I encounter** Aldric's magical imprint, **so that** the Incident is finally understood.

**Discovery Method:** Quest US-AA-62 "The Sealed East Wing"

**Content:**
- Aldric's echo is not a ghost — it's a magical recording, frozen in the moment of the experiment
- The echo speaks: "I saw them. Seven faces wearing masks. The masks are the gods. The faces beneath are — [static] — they're not what they — [the echo distorts] — the cage is real, the serpent is real, but the gods are — [silence]"
- The echo replays the experiment: light, color, WRONG color, explosion
- But now the player can see what Aldric saw: a flash of the truth about the Ascended
- The echo fades with one final word: "Remember."
- **This is the Academy's contribution to the core lore twist.** It doesn't resolve the mystery, but it confirms that the truth exists and that someone almost reached it.
