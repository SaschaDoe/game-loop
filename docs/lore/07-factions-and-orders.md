# Factions and Orders

## The Great Factions

The world is shaped by organizations that have risen around the central question — whether they know it or not — of who the gods really are. Some serve the Ascended's lie. Some seek the truth. Some have their own agenda entirely.

---

## 1. The Veiled Hand

**Type:** Secret order, divine enforcers
**Alignment:** Serves the Ascended directly
**Members:** ~200 worldwide, embedded in every major institution
**Symbol:** An open hand with an eye in the palm, veiled by a cloth

**Purpose:** The Veiled Hand is the Ascended's secret police. They are the only mortal organization that knows the *full truth* about the gods — and their mission is to ensure no one else ever learns it.

**History:** Founded in the first decade after the Ascension by Theron (God of Truth, former master liar), the Veiled Hand was originally a team of seven mortal agents, one appointed by each Ascended. Their job was simple: find and destroy any evidence of the gods' mortal lives.

Over millennia, the organization grew. Each generation of Veiled Hand members is inducted through a ritual where they witness a fragment of the true history — just enough to understand the stakes, not enough to pose a threat if captured. The most senior members (the "Fingers") know nearly everything.

**Methods:**
- Infiltration of libraries, archives, and universities
- Assassination of scholars who get too close to the truth
- Acquisition and destruction of dangerous artifacts and documents
- Manipulation of historical narratives
- Monitoring of Primordialist and Void Touched activity
- Planting false evidence to discredit truthseekers

**Internal Conflict:** Not all Veiled Hand members agree with the mission. Some believe the truth should be managed, not suppressed. Others have begun to wonder if the Ascended deserve their thrones at all. A faction within the Veiled Hand — the **Ungloved** — secretly works to weaken the organization from within.

**Player Interaction:**
- The player may encounter Veiled Hand agents as quest-givers, enemies, or allies depending on their choices
- Joining the Veiled Hand is possible (requires completing a loyalty questline)
- Betraying them triggers a worldwide manhunt
- At high reputation, the player can learn the truth from the Fingers — but is then expected to help maintain the secret

## 2. The Unmaskers

**Type:** Revolutionary movement
**Alignment:** Opposes the Ascended
**Members:** ~2,000 active, tens of thousands of sympathizers
**Symbol:** A cracked mask showing a human face beneath

**Purpose:** The Unmaskers believe the gods are hiding something. They don't know the full truth, but they've gathered enough fragments to know the official mythology is a lie. Their goal is to expose the Ascended's true nature and let mortals decide what to do with that information.

**History:** The Unmaskers trace their origin to a single woman: **Sera Thornhaven** — the survivor of Verath's genocide who hunted the future God of Justice across the continent. Sera never caught Verath (he ascended before she reached him), but she spent the rest of her life documenting what she knew and teaching her children to continue the search.

Over generations, Sera's family connected with other truth-seekers — survivors of the Brother War, Luminari descendants, Primordialist elders. They formed a loose network that eventually became the Unmaskers.

**What They Know:**
- The Ascended were mortal before claiming the thrones (but not who they were specifically)
- The thrones existed before the Ascended (proving the official mythology is false)
- Fragments of individual gods' mortal crimes (the Thornhaven documents, the Irongate inscription, Brother War forgeries)
- The existence of the Original Seven (from Primordialist oral traditions)

**What They Don't Know:**
- The complete identity of any specific Ascended
- How the Ascension worked
- The truth about the Void Serpent and the cage
- That restoring the Original Seven could destabilize reality

**Methods:**
- Archaeological expeditions to pre-Ascension sites
- Spirit world communication with Age of Silence ghosts
- Infiltration of temples and Veiled Hand cells
- Preservation and distribution of forbidden knowledge
- Recruitment of disaffected priests and scholars

**Player Interaction:**
- The Unmaskers are a joinable faction
- Their questline gradually reveals the truth about the Ascended
- The player must choose how much truth to reveal publicly
- At the questline's end, the player must decide: expose the gods, join the cover-up, or seek a third path

## 3. The Throne Seekers

**Type:** Power-hungry cult
**Alignment:** Chaotic self-interest
**Members:** ~500, in competing cells
**Symbol:** Seven thrones in a circle, one highlighted

**Purpose:** The Throne Seekers know the thrones can be claimed by mortals — because the Ascended did it. They want to *replace* the current gods by finding a way to eject the Ascended from the thrones and claim divinity for themselves.

**History:** The Throne Seekers originated when a Veiled Hand defector — a woman named Kyra Voss-Thane — realized that if the Ascended were once mortal, then the thrones could be taken *again*. She left the Veiled Hand (after killing her handler) and founded the first Throne Seeker cell.

The organization has fractured repeatedly because each cell leader wants a *different* throne. There is no unified leadership — just competing groups of would-be gods, each researching the Ascension process and gathering power.

**What They Know:**
- The Ascended were mortal (from the Veiled Hand defector)
- The thrones are the source of divine power
- The Threshold exists between all places
- Divine resonance is required to access the thrones (and they're trying to identify who carries it)

**Methods:**
- Hunting for mortals with divine resonance (sometimes kidnapping and testing candidates)
- Researching the Ascension ritual
- Seeking artifacts that could weaken an Ascended
- Assassinating competing Throne Seeker cells
- Occasionally cooperating with the Unmaskers (shared interest in the truth, opposite goals)

**Player Interaction:**
- The player carries divine resonance (they can hear faint Ley Line harmonics)
- Multiple Throne Seeker cells attempt to recruit or capture the player
- Joining a cell grants access to rare artifacts and forbidden knowledge
- The questline forces the player to confront: would you take a throne? Should anyone?

## 4. The Restoration

**Type:** Religious/philosophical order
**Alignment:** Serves the memory of the Original Seven
**Members:** ~300, mostly Primordialists and elder elves
**Symbol:** Seven stars in a ring with an empty center

**Purpose:** The Restoration seeks to reconstitute the Original Seven — to extract their essence from the Ley Lines, provide them new vessels, and restore the true gods to their thrones. This would mean ejecting the Ascended and fundamentally remaking the world's magical infrastructure.

**History:** The Restoration is ancient — older than the Unmaskers, older even than the Veiled Hand. It was founded within a generation of the Ascension by beings who remembered the Original Seven: elder elves, ancient spirits, and one dragon who had witnessed the Primordial War.

For millennia, the Restoration worked in absolute secrecy. They are patient in a way that human organizations cannot be — many of their members are effectively immortal, and they measure progress in centuries.

**What They Know:**
- The full truth about the Original Seven and the Ascension
- The location of three of the seven major Ley Line nexuses
- Fragments of the ritual that could separate the Original Seven's essence from the world
- The danger: separating the Original Seven risks destabilizing the cage around Xal-Nepheth

**What They Don't Know:**
- The location of four nexuses (the Ascended have hidden them)
- Whether the Original Seven want to return (they were principles, not people — they may not have opinions)
- Whether the world can survive the transition

**Methods:**
- Long-term Ley Line research
- Spirit world contact with pre-Ascension entities
- Guarding Ley Line nexuses from exploitation
- Training Primordialists in Old Magic
- Waiting

**Player Interaction:**
- The Restoration approaches the player only at high levels (they don't waste time on the unprepared)
- Their questline is the endgame: the attempt to restore the Original Seven
- The player must decide whether the risk is worth it
- Multiple endings based on the player's choice: full restoration, partial restoration, compromise, or preservation of the status quo

## 5. The Void Covenant

**Type:** Apocalyptic cult
**Alignment:** Serves Xal-Nepheth (the Void Serpent)
**Members:** Unknown (estimated 1,000-5,000)
**Symbol:** A serpent eating its own tail, dissolving into nothing

**Purpose:** The Void Covenant serves the Void Serpent and works to weaken its cage. Their endgame is the Unraveling — the dissolution of all reality back into the void.

**History:** The Void Covenant arose from the first Void Touched who listened too long and too deeply to Xal-Nepheth's whispers. Where the Cage Wardens learned to use Void Magic defensively, the Covenant embraced its destructive potential.

They are not insane (mostly). They are nihilists who have looked at reality — a world built on lies, governed by fraudulent gods, maintained by a cage that is slowly failing — and concluded that existence is a cruelty. The Unraveling is not destruction; it is mercy. An end to suffering through an end to everything.

**What They Know:**
- The truth about the Ascended (Xal-Nepheth told them)
- The location of weak points in the cage
- Void Magic techniques unknown to any other tradition
- That the cage is failing — slowly, but inevitably

**Methods:**
- Performing rituals at Void Scars to widen them
- Corrupting Ley Lines to weaken the cage
- Assassinating Cage Wardens and Restoration members
- Spreading nihilistic philosophy to recruit the desperate
- Seeking the Void Serpent's physical form (buried beneath the world)

**Player Interaction:**
- The Void Covenant is primarily an enemy faction
- However, their knowledge of the truth makes them useful informants
- A dark path allows the player to ally with the Covenant
- At the covenant's deepest level, the player meets a fragment of Xal-Nepheth itself — the most dangerous NPC encounter in the game

## 6. The Iron Remnant

**Type:** Secular political faction
**Alignment:** Anti-divine, pro-mortal autonomy
**Members:** ~5,000 active, growing
**Symbol:** A broken chain across an anvil

**Purpose:** The Iron Remnant doesn't care whether the gods are real, fake, good, or evil. They believe that mortal civilization should govern itself without divine interference. They seek to build a society that doesn't depend on the gods for magic, protection, or meaning.

**History:** The Iron Remnant traces its lineage to the Iron Republics — the civilization that, during the Age of Silence, proved that mortals could build a functioning society without gods. The Republics fell during the Ascension (partly due to Khorvan's betrayal at Irongate), but their ideals survived.

**What They Know:**
- The Iron Republics existed and thrived without gods (which the official history downplays)
- Rune magic and blood magic work without divine channels
- The Ascended actively suppress mortal self-sufficiency

**Methods:**
- Promoting secular governance
- Funding Runeweaver and artificing research
- Opposing temple political influence
- Preserving Iron Republic artifacts and philosophy
- Building infrastructure that doesn't depend on divine magic

**Player Interaction:**
- The Iron Remnant offers an alternative to the divine questlines
- Their path emphasizes technology, crafting, and mortal solidarity over divine power
- At high reputation, they reveal Iron Republic artifacts that could shield cities from divine influence
- The endgame choice: do mortals need gods at all?

---

## 7. The Ashen Circle

**Type:** Academic/archaeological order
**Alignment:** Neutral seekers of knowledge
**Members:** ~800, mostly scholars and adventurers
**Symbol:** An open book surrounded by flames (knowledge saved from destruction)

**Purpose:** The Ashen Circle collects, preserves, and studies artifacts and texts from the Age of Silence. They are the world's foremost experts on pre-Ascension civilizations. They don't have a political agenda — they just want to know what happened.

**History:** Founded three centuries ago by a historian named Dorin Ash-Marr, who discovered that the historical record had *gaps* — deliberate erasures where information should exist. The Ashen Circle was created to fill those gaps.

**Methods:**
- Archaeological expeditions to ancient ruins
- Translation of dead languages
- Artifact preservation and study
- Maintaining a secret library of pre-Ascension texts (their most guarded secret)

**Player Interaction:**
- The Ashen Circle is the primary source of lore fragments for the player
- They offer quests to explore ruins, translate texts, and recover artifacts
- At high reputation, they share their secret library — which contains pieces of every god's true history
- They are targeted by both the Veiled Hand (who want to destroy their findings) and the Unmaskers (who want to publicize them)

## Minor Factions

- **The Thornhaven Legacy** — Direct descendants of Sera Thornhaven, dedicated specifically to exposing Verath. Merged with the Unmaskers but maintain their own sub-identity.
- **The Cage Wardens** — Void Touched who use their power to monitor and repair the cage. Mistrusted by everyone, including themselves.
- **The Sleepless** — A cult that believes the world is a dream of the Original Seven and that waking them would end reality. Oppose the Restoration.
- **The Last Luminari** — Descendants of Orinthas's destroyed civilization, preserving what little knowledge survived. Unknowingly worship the man who destroyed them.
- **The Grey Pilgrims** — Druids who make regular pilgrimages to the Grey Wastes, trying to heal the dead Ley Line. They have been at it for centuries with no success.
