# The Magic System of Aethermoor

## The Source of All Magic

Magic in Aethermoor flows from the **Ley Lines** — the living essence of the Original Seven, who sacrificed themselves to become the world's foundation. Every spell, every enchantment, every magical creature draws its power from these invisible veins of divine energy.

Think of the Ley Lines as rivers of purpose. Where they flow strongly, magic is potent. Where they're thin, magic sputters. Where they've been damaged (the Grey Wastes, the Timeless Wastes), magic is dead or corrupted.

## The Two Eras of Magic

### Old Magic (Pre-Ascension)

During the Age of Silence, magic was **wild**. With no conscious will directing the Ley Lines, magical energy surged and ebbed unpredictably. Practitioners of this era had to work *with* the Lines rather than commanding them — reading their flows, riding their currents, timing their spells to natural surges.

Old Magic characteristics:
- **Unpredictable** — the same spell might produce different results depending on Ley Line conditions
- **Powerful** — raw, unfiltered divine energy; peak moments could rival the gods themselves
- **Dangerous** — magical surges could overwhelm a caster; magical droughts could leave them helpless
- **Attuned to nature** — Old Magic worked best when aligned with natural cycles (seasons, moon phases, tides)
- **No divine intermediary** — practitioners drew directly from the Ley Lines

Old Magic still exists in rare places and rare practitioners. It is the magic of druids who follow The Old Ways, of ancient elves who remember the Age of Silence, and of artifacts forged before the Ascension.

### New Magic (Post-Ascension / Tainted Magic)

When the Ascended claimed the divine thrones, they became the conscious will directing the Ley Lines. Magic now flows *through* them — filtered, regulated, and subtly tainted by their nature.

New Magic characteristics:
- **Predictable** — spells produce consistent results because the Ascended regulate the flow
- **Accessible** — more people can learn magic because the barrier to entry is lower
- **Limited** — the Ascended can (and do) restrict certain types of magic
- **Tainted** — because the Ascended are fundamentally flawed, their magic carries traces of their corruption
- **Dependent** — practitioners unknowingly rely on the Ascended's continued existence

**The Taint:** New Magic carries a subtle corruption that most practitioners never notice. A healing spell carries a whisper of Mireya's guilt. A truth-seeking spell carries a flicker of Theron's deceptiveness. A protective ward carries a tremor of Khorvan's fear. These traces are too subtle for most to detect, but sensitive practitioners — especially those attuned to Old Magic — can feel that something is *wrong* with modern magic.

## The Seven Streams

Magic flows through seven distinct "streams," each corresponding to one of the Original Seven's domains (now controlled by the Ascended):

### 1. The Stream of Order (Aum-Varek / Verath)
- **Governs:** Rune magic, wards, barriers, binding, abjuration, law magic
- **Feels like:** The certainty of mathematics, the click of a lock, the weight of a sworn oath
- **Taint:** Spells of order sometimes produce unjust results — a binding spell may trap the innocent, a ward may protect the guilty

### 2. The Stream of Change (Kha-Siel / Mireya)
- **Governs:** Transmutation, shapeshifting, healing, metamorphosis, curse-breaking
- **Feels like:** The vertigo of transformation, the itch of growing, the relief of pain ending
- **Taint:** Healing spells occasionally cause phantom pain in the caster (Mireya's echo); transmutations may be more violent than intended

### 3. The Stream of Time (Tho-Rienne / Orinthas)
- **Governs:** Chronomancy (forbidden), divination, prophecy, haste/slow magic, aging/youth
- **Feels like:** The weight of history, the vertigo of seeing the future, the disorientation of lost time
- **Taint:** Divination spells sometimes show what the caster *fears* rather than what is true (Orinthas's anxiety); time magic is unstable because Tho-Rienne's lines are the most damaged

### 4. The Stream of Space (Vel-Nara / Theron)
- **Governs:** Teleportation, dimensional magic, spatial manipulation, scrying, illusion
- **Feels like:** The stretch of distance, the disorientation of being everywhere, the vertigo of looking too far
- **Taint:** Illusion magic is unnervingly convincing (Theron was a master liar); teleportation occasionally deposits the caster somewhere they didn't intend — always somewhere related to a hidden truth

### 5. The Stream of Matter (Dro-Mahk / Selvara)
- **Governs:** Elemental magic (earth/water), crafting enchantments, golem creation, material conjuration
- **Feels like:** The solidity of stone, the patience of a growing tree, the stubbornness of iron
- **Taint:** Nature magic sometimes creates things that shouldn't exist — plants with teeth, stones that bleed (echoes of Selvara's alchemical abominations)

### 6. The Stream of Energy (Ira-Sethi / Khorvan)
- **Governs:** Elemental magic (fire/lightning), force magic, kinetic magic, battle magic, destruction
- **Feels like:** The rush of adrenaline, the heat of fury, the crack of impact
- **Taint:** Destructive magic is disproportionately powerful (Khorvan overcompensates); casters sometimes feel inexplicable fear when casting (Khorvan's cowardice bleeding through)

### 7. The Stream of Spirit (Pho-Lumen / Aelith)
- **Governs:** Soul magic, spirit communication, emotion magic, enchantment (mind), dreams, necromancy (corrupted)
- **Feels like:** The warmth of connection, the chill of loneliness, the ache of empathy
- **Taint:** Spirit magic can produce overwhelming emotional responses (Aelith's flood of retroactive love); necromancy is particularly corrupt because it perverts Pho-Lumen's gift of consciousness

## Magic Casting Methods

### Incantation (Spoken)
The most common method. Words of Power — syllables derived from the First Sound — shape magical energy into specific effects. Different languages access different streams more effectively:
- **Draconic** — best for Energy magic (fire, destruction)
- **Ancient Elvish** — best for Spirit and Change magic
- **Dwarvish Runic** — best for Order and Matter magic
- **Abyssal** — best for corrupted Spirit magic (necromancy, soul magic)
- **Celestial** — best for Order and Spirit magic (healing, protection)
- **Primordial** — accesses all streams equally (the language of the Original Seven)

### Runecraft (Written)
Symbols inscribed on surfaces channel magic persistently. A rune continues to work as long as the inscription exists. This is the basis of enchanting, warding, and trap-crafting.

### Ritual (Collective)
Multiple casters combine their power for effects too large for individuals. Rituals require precise timing, specific components, and perfect coordination. Most powerful but most dangerous — a botched ritual can produce catastrophic feedback.

### Innate (Bloodline)
Some beings carry magical ability in their blood — dragonborn, tieflings, fey-touched. This magic doesn't require study or incantation; it erupts from the caster's nature. Innate magic is usually limited to a specific stream but is instinctive and fast.

### Primordial Resonance (Old Magic)
The rarest method. Practitioners attune themselves to the Ley Lines directly, bypassing the Ascended's filter. This requires years of meditation, proximity to Ley Line nexuses, and a particular spiritual sensitivity. The result is Old Magic — wilder, stronger, purer, and infinitely more dangerous.

## The Forbidden Streams

Certain magical practices are "forbidden" — not because they're inherently evil, but because they access magic in ways the Ascended cannot fully control:

### Blood Magic
Uses the caster's life force instead of Ley Line energy. This bypasses the Ascended entirely, drawing on the fundamental magical property of living blood (a remnant of Ira-Sethi's original gift of life-energy). The Ascended fear blood magic because it proves that divinity is not necessary for power.

### Soul Magic
Manipulates the fundamental unit of consciousness — Pho-Lumen's greatest gift. Soul magic accesses the Spirit stream so deeply that it touches the original Ley Line essence, not the Ascended's filtered version. Practitioners of soul magic sometimes glimpse the truth about Aelith.

### Necromancy
A perversion of Spirit and Matter — forcing consciousness back into dead substance. The Ascended publicly condemn it but secretly allow it because necromantic energy actually *strengthens* the cage around Xal-Nepheth (death energy and void energy are opposites).

### Chronomancy
Time magic is forbidden because Tho-Rienne's lines are the most damaged. Every use of chronomancy risks widening the cracks in the temporal Ley Lines, which could destabilize time itself. Also, time magic is the most likely to reveal historical truths the Ascended want buried.

### Void Magic
Drawing power from Xal-Nepheth itself — reaching through the cage to touch the Unraveling. This is genuinely dangerous to reality and is the one forbidden magic that the Ascended suppress for legitimate reasons (though they don't explain why — doing so would require explaining the Void Serpent's true nature).

## Magical Corruption

Excessive use of any magic stream can cause **stream corruption** — the caster's body and mind begin to reflect the nature of the magic they channel:

| Stream | Corruption Signs |
|--------|-----------------|
| Order | Rigid thinking, inability to improvise, skin develops geometric patterns |
| Change | Physical instability (random transformations), inability to commit to decisions |
| Time | Premature aging or reverse aging, temporal disorientation, speaking in future tense |
| Space | Spatial disorientation, appearing translucent, accidentally teleporting in sleep |
| Matter | Skin hardens, joints stiffen, eventually petrification |
| Energy | Fever, spontaneous combustion, insomnia, hyperactivity |
| Spirit | Emotional instability, hearing voices, seeing spirits involuntarily |

At extreme corruption levels, the caster begins to hear the voice of the Ascended who controls that stream — their real voice, not the divine mask. Highly corrupted casters have reported hearing Khorvan *whimpering*, Mireya *screaming*, and Theron *laughing at a joke only he understands*.

## The Mana Economy

Mana — the personal magical energy of a caster — is essentially a buffer between the caster and the Ley Lines. It represents how much raw Ley Line energy the caster can safely hold and channel.

- **Mana Pool:** Determined by Intelligence, Wisdom, and level
- **Mana Recovery:** Resting near Ley Lines accelerates recovery; resting in Ley Line dead zones prevents it
- **Mana Overload:** Channeling more than your pool can hold causes backlash damage and potential corruption
- **Mana Starvation:** In dead zones (Grey Wastes, Void Scars), casters are effectively mundane

## Ley Line Geography

Ley Lines are not random. They form a web across the world, with seven major nexuses (one per Original Seven) and hundreds of minor convergence points:

- **Major Nexuses:** The most powerful magical locations in the world, each aligned with one stream. Control of a nexus grants enormous magical advantage. Wars have been fought over them.
- **Convergence Points:** Where two or more lines cross, producing mixed-stream effects. Cities tend to grow around convergences because the ambient magic improves health, crop growth, and general prosperity.
- **Dead Zones:** Where Ley Lines have been severed or drained (the Grey Wastes, Void Scars). No magic functions here. These are the scars of the Primordial War and Selvara's mortal crimes.
- **Wild Zones:** Where Ley Lines are damaged but not dead — magic works but unpredictably. The Timeless Wastes, the Hollow Sea, and the Ashlands are all wild zones.
