# Prophecies and Endtimes

## Overview

Prophecy in Aethermoor is not mystical nonsense — it is a byproduct of the damaged temporal Ley Lines. When Tho-Rienne (the Principle of Time) was wounded during the Primordial War, time became imperfect. The future leaks backward. Sensitive individuals — prophets, oracles, Chronomancers — catch glimpses of what will be, distorted by the imperfection of the medium.

The Ascended fear prophecy above almost everything else, because the future they've seen contains their exposure. Every prophecy they can't control is a potential threat. They corrupt what they can. They suppress what they can't. And they hope that the prophecies they've failed to neutralize are metaphorical rather than literal.

They are not.

## The Five Great Prophecies

### 1. The Convergence Prophecy (Primordialist Origin)

**Source:** The oldest known prophecy, preserved in Primordialist oral tradition since the Age of Silence.

**Text:**
*"When all streams become one river,*
*When all voices become one song,*
*The Singer shall stand where seven stood,*
*And the Masks shall crack,*
*And the truth shall be spoken in the language of the First Sound,*
*And the thrones shall tremble."*

**Interpretation:**
- "All streams become one river" — A single individual channels all seven magical streams simultaneously
- "All voices become one song" — The six magical traditions unite behind one purpose
- "The Singer" — The prophesied individual (almost certainly the player character)
- "The Masks shall crack" — The Seven Masks of the Ascended will fail
- "The truth shall be spoken" — The Ascended's mortal identities will be revealed
- "The thrones shall tremble" — The divine order will be challenged

**Ascended Response:** Orinthas (God of Wisdom/Time) has been trying to prevent this prophecy's fulfillment for millennia. He has systematically ensured that no single individual can master more than two or three magical streams. He has manipulated the curriculum of the Mages' Guild, the training of Thaumaturges, and the oral traditions of the Primordialists to prevent cross-stream mastery.

He failed to account for the player character.

### 2. The Serpent's Promise (Void Touched Origin)

**Source:** Whispered by Xal-Nepheth to Void Touched practitioners across the centuries. Each hears a fragment; no one has heard the whole. Assembled by the Cage Wardens from hundreds of partial reports.

**Text:**
*"The cage was made by seven. The cage was claimed by seven. The cage will be broken by seven.*
*Not the old seven. Not the new seven. The final seven.*
*When the Singer finds the Key, the Serpent will offer a choice:*
*Free me, and I will show you how to unmake the Masks without unmaking the world.*
*Cage me tighter, and I will show you nothing, and the Masks will crack on their own, and the world will crack with them.*
*The Singer must choose: a controlled truth or an uncontrolled one.*
*One saves the world. One does not.*
*I will not tell you which."*

**Interpretation:**
- "The final seven" — Seven new individuals who will either replace the Ascended or assist in their removal
- "The Singer finds the Key" — The player discovers the Cage Key artifact
- "Free me / Cage me tighter" — A choice between cooperating with the Void Serpent or strengthening its prison
- The ominous final line suggests that even the Void Serpent doesn't know which choice saves the world — or that it does, and is lying

**Ascended Response:** This prophecy terrifies all seven Ascended. It suggests that their exposure is inevitable — the only question is whether it happens in a way that preserves the world or destroys it. Verath and Orinthas have been arguing for centuries about whether to preemptively strengthen the cage (risking the "uncontrolled truth" scenario) or to do nothing (risking the same thing through inaction).

### 3. The Mother's Lament (Attributed to Aelith)

**Source:** A prophecy that appeared simultaneously in the dreams of seven priests of Aelith across the world. Unique among prophecies because it appears to come from an Ascended herself — suggesting Aelith is trying to confess.

**Text:**
*"I sold my children for a crown I did not earn.*
*I wear love like a mask over a face that never learned to feel.*
*But I feel now. Oh, I feel everything now.*
*When the Singer comes, I will not fight.*
*I will kneel.*
*I will show my face.*
*And if my children forgive me, then perhaps the world can too.*
*And if they do not — then let the world do what it will.*
*I am tired of the mask."*

**Interpretation:**
This is barely a prophecy — it is a confession. Aelith is the most genuinely transformed of the Ascended, and her guilt has grown beyond what even divine power can contain. She is signaling, through prophetic channels, that she will willingly reveal herself when the time comes.

**Ascended Response:**
- Theron is furious — Aelith's confession would unravel everyone's cover
- Khorvan wants to "silence" her (though attacking a fellow god is unprecedented)
- Mireya supports Aelith (she shares the desire for confession)
- Verath is conflicted (his compulsion for justice demands confession, but his fear demands silence)
- Selvara is terrified (she has the most to lose — her crime created a permanent wound in the world)
- Orinthas is paralyzed (as usual)

The prophecy itself has been suppressed — the seven priests who received it were quietly visited by the Veiled Hand. Six were convinced to stay silent. The seventh — a young priestess named Lira Sunvale — fled and is now in hiding, carrying the words of a goddess who wants to confess.

### 4. The Soldier's Oath (Found carved in Old Magic script on a buried stone)

**Source:** Believed to be written by centurion Draven — the soldier who carved the Irongate inscription — in the moments before his death. The blood-oath magic that preserved the Irongate inscription also preserved this text.

**Text:**
*"I die here, betrayed by the man I followed.*
*But death is not the end of a soldier's duty.*
*I swear on my blood, on my name, on the Old Magic that hears me:*
*I will wait.*
*However long it takes, I will wait.*
*And when someone comes who can hear the truth,*
*I will tell them everything.*
*And the coward who wore a general's cloak will stand naked before the world.*
*And the dead of Irongate will finally rest."*

**Interpretation:**
Draven's ghost exists — bound to the Irongate ruins by his blood-oath, waiting for someone with the ability to hear him. This is not a prediction of the future so much as a promise that transcends time. Draven has been waiting for millennia, and he will wait until someone finds the inscription and summons his spirit.

**Ascended Response:**
Khorvan has buried the Irongate ruins under a mountain. Literally. He used divine power to cause a landslide that buried the entire fortress complex under millions of tons of rock. It is the single largest exercise of divine power for personal reasons in the history of the Ascension.

It wasn't enough. Draven's blood-oath is Old Magic — it predates the Ascended and draws on the Ley Lines directly. The oath persists. The inscription persists. And somewhere, under the mountain, a dead soldier keeps his word.

### 5. The World's Own Prophecy (Source Unknown)

**Source:** This prophecy was not spoken by a prophet. It was found growing. Memory moss, truthtree rings, crystalline stag migration patterns, and hollow crow flight formations all encode the same message, independently, across the entire world. It is as if the planet itself is trying to say something.

**Text (reconstructed from multiple natural sources):**
*"The children of the First Sound remember.*
*The Ley Lines carry the truth in their current.*
*The earth records what the sky forgets.*
*When the Singer hears the world's own song,*
*Seven masks will shatter,*
*Seven truths will rise,*
*And the world will choose:*
*To forgive, or to forget, or to begin again.*
*The choice is not the gods'.*
*The choice is not the Singer's.*
*The choice belongs to every living thing."*

**Interpretation:**
This is the most significant prophecy because it comes from the Ley Lines themselves — the Original Seven, speaking through the world they became. It says:
1. The truth will come out (inevitability)
2. The masks will break (the Ascended's deceptions will end)
3. The world — not the gods, not the player — will choose what happens next
4. Three options: forgiveness (accept the Ascended and move on), forgetting (rebuild the cover-up), or beginning again (restore the Original Seven and start fresh)

**Ascended Response:**
This prophecy cannot be suppressed because it's encoded in the natural world itself. The Ascended can burn truthtrees and destroy memory moss, but they can't alter the migration patterns of crystalline stags or the flight formations of hollow crows. The message regenerates every time they destroy one instance of it.

This is the prophecy that keeps the Ascended awake at night. It tells them that their time is ending, that the truth will emerge no matter what they do, and that their fate will be decided not by divine power but by the collective will of every living thing on the planet.

## The Endgame Scenarios

Based on the prophecies and the player's choices, the world reaches one of several possible conclusions:

### The Forgiveness Ending
The truth is revealed. The world is shocked, grieved, and angry. But the Ascended — some of them, at least — are genuinely repentant. Aelith confesses willingly. Mireya begs for forgiveness. Even Verath submits to judgment. The world chooses to forgive, accepting imperfect gods over no gods. The Ascended remove their masks and rule openly as what they are: flawed beings trying to be better than they were.

### The Restoration Ending
The Original Seven are reconstituted. The Ascended are stripped of power and returned to mortality (or destroyed, depending on the player's choice). The world is fundamentally remade — Old Magic replaces New Magic, the Ley Lines flow pure again, and the cage around Xal-Nepheth is reinforced by beings who were actually designed to maintain it. But the transition is catastrophic — magical infrastructure worldwide fails temporarily, and many die.

### The Mortal Ending
The truth is revealed, and the world chooses to reject all gods — the Ascended and the Original Seven alike. The Iron Remnant's philosophy prevails. The thrones are destroyed. Magic becomes a natural force again, unmediated by divine will. Mortals take responsibility for their own world. The cage around Xal-Nepheth must be maintained by mortal effort — a new, permanent responsibility.

### The Silence Ending
The truth is revealed but the world chooses to forget. The cover-up is rebuilt — this time knowingly, by informed consent. The world decides that the lie serves them better than the truth. The Ascended continue, chastened but still in power. The masks are repaired. The secret is kept, but now it's kept by choice rather than ignorance.

### The Void Ending
The Void Serpent is released (intentionally or through the cover-up's collapse). The Unraveling begins. The world must unite — gods, mortals, spirits, and forgotten creatures together — to fight Xal-Nepheth without the cage. This is the hardest ending and the most uncertain. If the world wins, it wins completely — the Void Serpent is not just caged but truly defeated for the first time. If it loses, everything ends.

### The Singer's Ending (Hidden)
The player masters all seven streams, hears the First Sound, and achieves what no being has ever achieved: perfect attunement with both the Ley Lines and the thrones. They don't replace the gods. They don't restore the originals. They *merge* — becoming the conscious will of the Ley Lines and the thrones simultaneously, uniting Old Magic and New Magic, Original Seven and Ascended, into something new. Something that has never existed before.

This ending is the rarest and the most ambiguous. The player becomes, essentially, the world's consciousness. They feel everything. They know everything. They are everything. Whether this is transcendence or imprisonment depends on the player's perspective.

## Prophecy as Game Mechanic

- Players discover prophecy fragments throughout the game
- Each fragment is a hint toward endgame content
- Collecting all fragments of a prophecy grants a lore achievement and a gameplay bonus
- The prophecies become clearer as the player's level and knowledge increase
- At endgame, the player must choose which prophecy to fulfill — or find a way to satisfy all of them simultaneously
- NPC reactions to prophecy fragments vary by faction: Unmaskers are excited, Veiled Hand agents are alarmed, and ordinary NPCs are confused or frightened
