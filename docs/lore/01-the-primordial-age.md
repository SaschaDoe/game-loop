# The Primordial Age — Creation of Aethermoor

## Before All Things

Before the world existed, there was only the **Eternal Silence** — an infinite void without light, sound, matter, or time. Within this void drifted two forces in an endless, purposeless dance:

- **The Weave** — the potential for all things to exist, formless and yearning
- **The Unraveling** — the certainty that all things must end, patient and absolute

Neither was alive. Neither had will. They were simply forces, like gravity or decay, moving through nothing forever.

## The First Sound

At some unknowable moment — for time did not yet exist — the Weave and the Unraveling collided. Not violently, but the way two waves meet and form a third. From their collision came something neither had possessed alone: **intent**.

This intent became the **First Sound** — a resonance that vibrated through the void and, in vibrating, created the first distinction between *here* and *there*. Space was born.

The First Sound echoed, and each echo was different from the last. From this difference, **time** was born — for now there was a before and an after.

The echoes layered upon each other, harmonizing and clashing, and from their interference patterns, **matter** condensed like frost forming on glass. The first stones. The first waters. The first fires. The first winds.

## The Birth of the Original Seven

The richest harmonies — where the most echoes converged — gained something more than matter. They gained **awareness**. Seven such convergences occurred, and from them emerged the **Original Seven** — not born, not created, but *crystallized* from the music of creation itself.

They were not humanoid. They were not comprehensible. They were living principles:

1. **Aum-Varek** — The Principle of Order (the pattern that holds things together)
2. **Kha-Siel** — The Principle of Change (the force that prevents stagnation)
3. **Tho-Rienne** — The Principle of Time (the river that carries all things forward)
4. **Vel-Nara** — The Principle of Space (the distance between all things)
5. **Dro-Mahk** — The Principle of Matter (the weight and substance of reality)
6. **Ira-Sethi** — The Principle of Energy (the spark that animates the dead)
7. **Pho-Lumen** — The Principle of Spirit (the inner light of consciousness)

They did not speak. They did not decide. They simply *were*, and in being, the world took shape around them like a sculpture emerging from stone.

## The Shaping

The Original Seven did not "build" Aethermoor. Their existence *implied* it.

- **Aum-Varek's** presence meant that matter followed rules — stone was hard, water flowed downhill, fire rose upward.
- **Kha-Siel's** presence meant that nothing stayed the same — rivers carved valleys, mountains eroded, seasons turned.
- **Tho-Rienne's** presence meant that events had consequences — cause led to effect, memory was possible, stories could be told.
- **Vel-Nara's** presence meant that things had location — here was not there, up was not down, the world had geography.
- **Dro-Mahk's** presence meant that things had substance — you could touch stone, drink water, feel the wind.
- **Ira-Sethi's** presence meant that things could happen — lightning struck, hearts beat, volcanoes erupted.
- **Pho-Lumen's** presence meant that things could *know* — the first amoeba could sense light, the first fish could feel fear, the first thinking being could ask "why?"

The world formed in layers:

1. **The Deep Roots** — the bedrock of reality, where the Ley Lines (veins of divine resonance) run
2. **The Green Mantle** — the living surface, where creatures and plants exist
3. **The Bright Veil** — the sky and stars, mirrors of the original harmonies
4. **The Spirit Membrane** — the thin barrier between the material and the immaterial

## The Void Serpent

But the Unraveling had not disappeared when creation began. It had been pushed to the edges, compressed, denied. And denial breeds resentment — even in mindless forces.

The Unraveling coiled itself into a shape: **Xal-Nepheth, the Void Serpent**. Where the Original Seven were crystallized from harmony, Xal-Nepheth was crystallized from dissonance — the anti-song, the sound that unmakes.

Xal-Nepheth did not attack immediately. It waited at the edges of reality, growing, listening, learning the patterns of creation so it could better destroy them. It devoured stars at the margins. It swallowed oceans at the world's edge. It whispered to the things that lived and told them that existence was suffering and ending was mercy.

## The Primordial War

When Xal-Nepheth finally struck, it did not come as an army. It came as *absence*. Wherever it moved, things simply stopped existing. Color drained. Sound died. Memory evaporated. It ate the world from the outside in.

The Original Seven fought — not with weapons, but with their very nature. Aum-Varek imposed order on the chaos. Kha-Siel disrupted the Serpent's patterns. Tho-Rienne anchored events so they couldn't be unwritten. They fought for an age that had no name because the being who named things (Pho-Lumen) was too busy fighting to name it.

The war scarred the world:
- The **Ashlands** — where Ira-Sethi burned so brightly that nothing can grow for eternity
- The **Timeless Wastes** — where Tho-Rienne shattered and time flows in loops
- The **Hollow Sea** — where Dro-Mahk was torn and matter itself is thin
- The **Silence Peaks** — mountains so tall they pierce the Spirit Membrane, forever silent

## The Sacrifice

The Original Seven could not kill Xal-Nepheth. Unraveling cannot be destroyed — it is as fundamental as creation itself. The best they could do was *bind* it.

They chose to sacrifice themselves.

Each of the Seven poured their essence into the world's foundations, becoming the Ley Lines themselves — the veins of magic and reality that hold Aethermoor together. In doing so, they created a cage of living purpose around Xal-Nepheth, trapping it deep beneath the world's roots.

But gods cannot simply stop existing. When they poured themselves into the world, they left behind seven **empty thrones** — seats of divine power that hummed with residual energy, waiting for someone to claim them.

The thrones sat in a place that existed between all places: **The Threshold** — a chamber that could only be reached by those who carried a fragment of divine resonance within them.

## The Age of Silence

With the Original Seven gone and no gods to fill their place, the world entered the **Age of Silence** — an era of roughly two thousand years where:

- Magic existed but was wild and unpredictable (no divine will to shape it)
- Mortal races evolved and built civilizations on their own
- The Ley Lines pulsed with raw energy that could be tapped but not controlled
- Strange creatures born from unguided magic roamed the wilderness
- The spirit world was thin and porous — ghosts walked among the living
- Xal-Nepheth stirred in its cage, weakening the bindings century by century

It was during this age that the mortal civilizations rose and fell — the Aetherian Empire, the Crystalborn Kingdoms, the First Cities of the Dwarves, the Eternal Groves of the Elves. And it was at the end of this age, when the cage began to crack and the Void Serpent's whispers grew loud enough for mortals to hear, that seven very specific, very terrible people found the Threshold.

And everything changed.

## Legacy in the Modern World

Evidence of the Primordial Age can be found:
- **Ley Line nexuses** — places where the Original Seven's essence is strongest, often marked by ancient standing stones
- **Void Scars** — places where Xal-Nepheth touched the world, identifiable by the absence of sound and color
- **Primordial fossils** — creatures from the Shaping era, turned to crystal rather than stone
- **The First Sound** — rumored to still echo in the deepest caverns, where the world's roots are thinnest. Those who hear it gain temporary Primordial magic but risk madness
- **Dream echoes** — sleeping near Ley Lines sometimes produces dreams of the Primordial War, experienced from the perspective of the dying Original Seven
