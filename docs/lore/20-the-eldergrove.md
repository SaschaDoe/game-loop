# The Eldergrove

## Overview

The Eldergrove is a vast, legendary forest that sprawls across hundreds of miles of ancient woodland. Its silverwood trees are among the oldest living things in the world — some predate the Ascension by millennia. The forest is home to the Sylvan Elves, a reclusive civilization that builds its cities among the canopy, far above the forest floor where bandits, beasts, and darker things roam.

The Eldergrove is not merely a forest. It is a living memory. The trees store centuries of whispered conversations in their rings. The moss absorbs sound and keeps it. The Ley Lines run strong and unfiltered here, because the elves never built temples to the Ascended — and they never will.

## The Sylvan Elves

The Sylvan Elves are distinct from the Greenweald wood-elves. Where the Greenweald elves adapted to coexistence with humans and accepted some measure of the Ascended's authority, the Sylvan Elves rejected the new gods entirely. They retreated deeper into the forest after the Ascension and built their civilization vertically — upward into the canopy — to separate themselves from the ground-dwelling world that worshipped stolen thrones.

### Culture

- **Language:** Sylvan — a melodic tongue related to Elvish but more archaic, with vocabulary for concepts that Common lacks (types of silence, degrees of rootedness, the specific sound of starlight through leaves)
- **Governance:** The Council of Boughs — seven elder Wardens who each tend a district of the forest. Decisions are made by consensus, and the trees are consulted through root-reading rituals
- **Architecture:** Living-wood platforms grown from the silverwood trees themselves. Vine bridges span the canopy. Moonlit Archives are libraries hidden inside hollow trunks. Nothing is cut — everything is grown
- **Religion:** The Sylvan Elves worship no gods. They revere the Ley Lines directly, calling them "the Rootsong." They remember the Original Seven as principles, not persons, and practice Old Magic without shame or secrecy

### Key Settlements

- **Silverspire:** The great canopy city. Three thousand elves live on platforms of living wood connected by vine bridges. The Moonlit Archives — a library the Veiled Hand believes it burned two centuries ago — sit at its heart
- **Roothollow:** A trading post where the forest meets the outside world. Humans, elves, and halflings barter under a truce as old as the trees
- **The Thornveil:** A fortified Ranger camp at the forest's southern edge, maintaining watch against bandits and whatever prowls the deep wood at night

### The Wardens

The Wardens are the Eldergrove's ranger-knights — trained from childhood in forest combat, Sylvan magic, and the ancient art of root-reading. They patrol the forest paths, protect the settlements, and enforce the Council's will. Outsiders who earn a Warden's respect may be granted passage to the canopy cities. Those who do not are politely — but firmly — escorted back to the forest edge.

### The Archivists

The Moonlit Archives contain texts that no other library in the world possesses — books the Veiled Hand hunted and burned across three continents. The Sylvan Elves offered sanctuary to every forbidden text, every banned scroll, every heretical treatise. The Archives hold fragments of pre-Ascension history that the Ascended themselves would kill to destroy.

The Archivists are scholars who have dedicated their lives to preserving this knowledge. They maintain the collection, translate ancient texts, and — very carefully — share fragments with outsiders they judge worthy.

## Dangers of the Eldergrove

### The Briarwood Gang

A band of roughly forty human outlaws led by a dispossessed lord who calls himself "The Thorn King." Lord Aldren Voss lost his lands to a Church of Solaris decree and fled to the forest. His gang preys on caravans and pilgrims, fortifying root-hollows as hideouts. The elves tolerate them as a buffer against worse intruders — but their patience has limits.

### Canopy Stalkers

Great cats that hunt from the upper branches. They are silent, patient, and deadly. A Canopy Stalker is heard only when it chooses to be heard — usually when it's too late.

### Forest Trolls

Rare but territorial. They regenerate from wounds unless burned. The Rangers maintain fire stations along the major paths for this reason.

### Giant Spiders

Nest in the eastern groves. Their webs are strong as rope, their venom causes paralysis. They prefer live prey — cocooned victims can survive for days before being consumed.

### The Thornveil Beast

Something ancient prowls the deepest paths. Tracks larger than a bear's appear and then vanish at the treeline, as if the creature climbs. No Ranger has seen it clearly and returned to describe it. The Council of Boughs knows what it is but will not say.

## Lore Connections

### The Verdant Basin

The Eldergrove's elves remember the Verdant Basin — the great forest that Selvara destroyed with the Grey. The Lament for the Verdant Basin is sung at every Sylvan funeral. The elves know that Selvara, who sits upon the throne of Nature and claims to be mother of all growing things, is the woman who committed the greatest act of ecological destruction in history. This knowledge fuels their rejection of the Ascended.

### The Worldseed Tree

The oldest tree in the Eldergrove. Some Archivists believe it is a cutting from the original World Oak — grown from a seed carried by refugees fleeing the Verdant Basin's destruction. If true, it is a living link to the world before the Grey, and its rings may contain memories of what the Verdant Basin was before Selvara killed it.

Voidblooms have been spotted near the Worldseed Tree. The elves watch them with growing unease.

### The Forgotten Moon Temple

Deep in the forest stands a temple that no map records. Its altar predates the Ascension by centuries. The inscriptions name no Ascended god — they name the moon, the roots, and a "principle of Growth" that matches no known member of the Original Seven. The Archivists have debated its meaning for generations. Some believe it names an eighth principle that was lost before even the Original Seven sacrificed themselves.

### The Blight

Black veins have appeared in the bark of trees near the forest's edge — identical to descriptions of "the Grey," the alchemical weapon that destroyed the Verdant Basin. Someone is testing it again. The Archivists believe the Veiled Hand wants the Eldergrove destroyed because of what the Moonlit Archives contain. If the Archives' knowledge were ever made public, the Ascended's cover story would collapse entirely.

## The Sylvan Language

Sylvan is related to Elvish but older and more complex. It contains:

- **Seventeen words for silence** (the silence of a sleeping forest, the silence of a forest listening, the silence before a storm, etc.)
- **Verb tenses that reference root-depth** (an action done at the surface level vs. one done at the root level — shallow vs. deep intent)
- **No word for "god"** — the concept is expressed as "one who sits where they should not"

Learning Sylvan unlocks hidden dialogue with the Wardens, reveals canopy paths invisible to outsiders, and allows reading the Moonlit Archives' oldest texts.
