# The Ascended Pantheon — The True Histories of the Gods

## How They Found the Thrones

In the final century of the Age of Silence, the Void Serpent's cage began to crack. Whispers leaked through — maddening, seductive, terrifying. Across the world, people went mad. Empires crumbled. The Aetherian Empire, greatest civilization of the age, collapsed into civil war as its leaders heard the Serpent's promises of power.

Seven individuals — each carrying the rare "divine resonance" by cosmic accident — were drawn together not by fate or virtue, but by desperation and selfishness. Each was fleeing something: justice, consequence, shame, death. Each stumbled into the Threshold through a different Ley Line convergence point, seeking shelter, escape, or power.

They found the empty thrones. And in the moment of the world's greatest crisis — with the Void Serpent's cage failing and reality fraying at the edges — they sat down.

The thrones did not judge. They simply filled.

But divine power, when poured into a mortal vessel, does not preserve the mortal. It **transforms** them — and the transformation is shaped by what they lack most. A woman who never loved becomes love incarnate. A coward becomes war itself. The universe has a cruel sense of humor.

## The Seven Ascended

### 1. Verath — God of Justice
*Sits on Aum-Varek's Throne (Order)*

**Mortal Name:** Verath Kol-Sindri

**Who He Really Was:** A magistrate of the Aetherian Empire — its most corrupt. Verath took bribes to condemn innocents, fabricated evidence against political rivals, and sold sentences to the highest bidder. He ran a network of false witnesses who would swear to anything for coin.

**His Worst Crime:** The village of Thornhaven refused to pay his "protection tax." Verath fabricated evidence that the entire village was harboring demon cultists. He personally oversaw the trial — a mockery that lasted six minutes — and sentenced all 340 residents to slavery in the salt mines. Eighty-three were children. He then murdered the one honest clerk who had recorded the real proceedings, burned the true documents, and planted forged ones.

When the Aetherian Empire began to collapse, Verath fled not from the chaos but from a young woman named Sera Thornhaven — the one survivor of his genocide, now grown and hunting him with the real documents she'd recovered from a hidden archive.

**How He Found the Threshold:** Running through the deep ruins beneath the capital to escape Sera, Verath stumbled through a crack in reality at a Ley Line nexus of Order. He fell into the Threshold.

**The Transformation:** The moment he sat on Aum-Varek's throne, every unjust act he had ever committed burned through his consciousness simultaneously. He experienced the suffering of every person he'd wronged — not as memory, but as lived experience. The divine power of Order poured into a vessel defined by chaos and corruption, and the resulting explosion of contradiction made him what he is: a being who *compulsively* seeks justice but is *constitutionally* terrified that justice will find him.

**His Divine Nature:** Verath cannot lie while exercising divine power. His judgments, when rendered from the throne, are always perfectly fair. But he lives in agony — every fair judgment he makes reminds him of the unfair ones. He has nightmares about Sera Thornhaven, even though she has been dead for millennia. He sees her face in every petitioner who comes before him.

**His Secret Fear:** Somewhere in the world, the real Thornhaven documents still exist — copied by Sera before she died, hidden in a place Verath has never been able to find. If they were ever brought to divine light, it would prove that the God of Justice is history's greatest perpetrator of injustice.

**Domains in Game:** Justice, law, order, truth in judgment, contracts, oaths
**Public-Facing Aspect:** Appears through Solaris (sun/light) and Lexis (knowledge) worship

### 2. Aelith — Goddess of Love
*Sits on Pho-Lumen's Throne (Spirit)*

**Mortal Name:** Aelith Vey-Morath

**Who She Really Was:** A noblewoman of the Crystalborn Kingdoms — beautiful, brilliant, and completely devoid of empathy. Aelith was incapable of feeling love, affection, or compassion. She recognized these emotions in others the way a colorblind person recognizes that others see colors — she knew the concept but had never experienced it.

She learned to *perform* love with terrifying precision. She seduced lovers, made them worship her, then destroyed them for entertainment. She once maintained seven simultaneous affairs, each partner believing they were her only love, and orchestrated their simultaneous discovery of each other for the pleasure of watching the chaos.

**Her Worst Crime:** Aelith had three children by three different men. She viewed them as social tools — accessories to be displayed when advantageous and hidden when not. When her political ambitions required her to marry a powerful lord who wanted no stepchildren, she sold all three to a traveling merchant caravan heading into the Ashlands. She told each child separately that she loved them and that this was "a great adventure." None survived the journey. She attended a ball that evening and danced until dawn.

When the Crystalborn Kingdoms fell to internal war, Aelith fled not from danger but from the collective rage of every family she had destroyed. The parents of her dead lover Count Miravel had hired thirty bounty hunters specifically for her.

**How She Found the Threshold:** Hiding in a spirit-touched grove where the Spirit Membrane was thin, Aelith accidentally stepped through into the Threshold. She was drawn to Pho-Lumen's throne because it radiated the warmth she had never felt.

**The Transformation:** When divine Spirit poured into a vessel that had never known love, it filled the void with *everything*. Aelith suddenly felt — for the first time in her existence — love. Not a little. ALL of it. Every form of love that exists, at maximum intensity, simultaneously. Love for every living thing. Love for the world. Love for people she'd never met. And, horrifyingly, retroactive love for everyone she had destroyed.

She felt what her children felt when they died in the Ashlands, alone, calling for a mother who had sold them. She felt what Count Miravel felt when he realized she'd been lying. She felt it all, and she could never stop feeling it.

**Her Divine Nature:** Aelith is the most genuinely transformed of the Ascended. She truly, deeply loves all living things now. But her love is haunted — she weeps for every broken heart because she broke so many. She is paradoxically the kindest and the most tormented of the gods, because she can never undo what the mortal Aelith did, and the divine Aelith feels every consequence eternally.

**Her Secret Fear:** Her children's spirits. She knows they exist in the spirit world. She has never dared to face them. She pours love into the mortal world as penance, but the three small ghosts who wander the Ashlands, still looking for their mother, are the truth she cannot face.

**Domains in Game:** Love, compassion, empathy, bonds, healing of the heart, fertility
**Public-Facing Aspect:** Appears through aspects of Solaris (healing/light) and Verdana (nurturing/growth)

### 3. Khorvan — God of War
*Sits on Ira-Sethi's Throne (Energy)*

**Mortal Name:** Khorvan Dresh-Ulm

**Who He Really Was:** A general of the Iron Republics — and the greatest coward in military history. Khorvan achieved his rank through nepotism (his uncle was a senator) and maintained it through a genius for taking credit and deflecting blame.

He never fought in a single engagement. Not one. In every battle, he positioned himself "in reserve" — behind the lines, surrounded by his personal guard, ready to flee at the first sign of trouble. His soldiers called him "the Ghost General" because he was never seen on the battlefield.

**His Worst Crime:** At the Siege of Irongate, Khorvan's army of twelve thousand was defending the last free city of the Iron Republics against an invading force. His soldiers trusted him. His officers believed in the battle plan he'd presented — a flanking maneuver that would crush the enemy.

The plan was a lie. There was no flanking force. Khorvan had secretly negotiated with the enemy commander: he would withdraw his personal guard through a hidden tunnel, leaving his army leaderless mid-battle, in exchange for safe passage and a chest of gold.

He fled through the tunnel while eight thousand of his soldiers died in the confusion. Some survived long enough to realize what had happened. A centurion named Draven carved Khorvan's name into the gate with his dying hand and wrote beneath it: "THE COWARD."

Khorvan fled across the continent, spending his blood money in taverns, hiding from the survivors who hunted him.

**How He Found the Threshold:** Cornered in a volcanic cave by three survivors of Irongate, Khorvan backed into a Ley Line nexus of Energy while begging for his life. The heat and energy tore open a path to the Threshold.

**The Transformation:** Energy poured into a vessel defined by inertia and cowardice. The divine power of motion, heat, passion, and fury filled a man who had never stood his ground. The result was an overcorrection of cosmic proportions — Khorvan became a being of pure martial fury, the embodiment of everything he had never been.

But beneath the divine armor, the coward still exists. Khorvan overcompensates constantly — he is the loudest, most aggressive, most violently brave of the Ascended because he is terrified that if he stops for one moment, the coward will return. His divine rage is, at its core, fear.

**His Secret Fear:** The word "Irongate." He has systematically erased it from every history book, every map, every inscription in the world. But the centurion Draven's carving was made with a dying soldier's blood-oath — a form of Old Magic that cannot be erased. Somewhere, that inscription still exists on a gate buried under rubble, waiting to be found.

**Domains in Game:** War, courage, battle, strength, fire, passion
**Public-Facing Aspect:** Appears primarily through Ferrum (war/forge) worship

### 4. Mireya — Goddess of Mercy
*Sits on Kha-Siel's Throne (Change)*

**Mortal Name:** Mireya Sol-Vess

**Who She Really Was:** The chief torturer and executioner of the Crimson Court — a brutal theocracy that ruled the southern continent during the Age of Silence. Mireya did not merely carry out sentences. She *innovated*. She designed instruments of pain with the same care a craftsman gives to fine furniture. She kept notes. She catalogued techniques. She was an *artist* of suffering.

What made Mireya truly monstrous was not her cruelty but her curiosity. She tortured people not for confession or punishment but because she genuinely wanted to understand pain — its varieties, its thresholds, its effects on the mind and body. She approached suffering as a scholar approaches a text.

**Her Worst Crime:** The Monastery of Still Waters was home to eighty-six monks who had taken vows of nonviolence and compassion. They were completely harmless — healers, teachers, gardeners. The Crimson Court's inquisitors suspected them of harboring "heretical texts" (they weren't).

Mireya requested and received permission to "question" the entire monastery. Over the course of three weeks, she systematically tortured every monk. When they had nothing to confess (because they were innocent), she became frustrated and escalated. She blinded all eighty-six monks, reasoning that "those who will not see the truth do not deserve sight." She then released them — blind, broken, unable to care for themselves — into the wilderness.

She wrote a detailed report on the experience, concluding that "subjects with strong compassion centers exhibit fascinating resistance to pain but ultimately succumb to despair when their worldview of a just universe is shattered."

**How She Found the Threshold:** When the Crimson Court was overthrown by a peasant rebellion, Mireya fled into the swamps. She found a Ley Line nexus of Change in a place where the swamp was shifting — solid ground becoming water, water becoming ice, ice becoming steam. She stepped through the transition into the Threshold.

**The Transformation:** Change poured into a vessel of stasis and control. Mireya had spent her life imposing pain — maintaining control over others through suffering. The Principle of Change destroyed that control completely. She was transformed into a being who could feel every pain she had ever inflicted, across all of time, and whose only power was to *change* pain into something else — to transform suffering into healing.

**Her Divine Nature:** Mireya heals compulsively. She cannot stand to see suffering. But every time she heals someone, she briefly relives the suffering she inflicted — the screams of the monks, the sound of burning iron, the smell of the torture chamber. Her mercy is real, but it is powered by guilt so vast it has its own gravity.

**Her Secret Fear:** The ghosts of the eighty-six blind monks wander the world. They do not seek vengeance — they forgave her long ago, because that was their nature. And this is what terrifies Mireya most: she does not deserve their forgiveness, and she knows it. Their grace is worse than their hatred could ever be.

**Domains in Game:** Mercy, healing, transformation, forgiveness, change
**Public-Facing Aspect:** Channels through aspects of Verdana (growth/healing) and Solaris (restoration)

### 5. Theron — God of Truth
*Sits on Vel-Nara's Throne (Space)*

**Mortal Name:** Theron Ash-Velk

**Who He Really Was:** A spymaster, forger, and propagandist — arguably the most dangerous liar in the history of the Age of Silence. Theron never wielded a weapon. He wielded *words*. He could forge any document, mimic any handwriting, replicate any seal. He could craft lies so convincing that even the liar started to believe them.

He operated as a freelance manipulator — selling his services to whoever paid most. He toppled three governments, started two wars, and collapsed an entire economy through nothing but fabricated documents and whispered rumors.

**His Worst Crime:** The Allied Kingdoms of Vestraad and Korinn had been allies for three hundred years. Their peoples intermarried. Their cultures were intertwined. Their alliance was the most stable political relationship on the continent.

Theron was hired by a rival power to destroy it.

Over two years, he forged a web of fake correspondence, fabricated intelligence reports, created false witnesses, and planted doctored evidence that made each kingdom believe the other was planning a surprise invasion. He was so thorough that he created fake *archaeological evidence* — forged ancient texts that "proved" the two kingdoms had been enemies in a past age.

The result was the **Brother War** — a conflict between two peoples who loved each other, each fighting with the grief of betrayal. The war lasted six years. Four hundred thousand people died. Both kingdoms were destroyed. The survivors, learning the truth decades later, found no comfort in it — the dead were still dead, the trust still shattered.

Theron took his payment and moved on to the next client. He felt nothing.

**How He Found the Threshold:** Theron's web of lies eventually tangled around him. A coalition of survivors from his many victims tracked him across the continent. Cornered in an ancient observatory, Theron found a Ley Line nexus of Space — and realized that the Threshold existed in a place that was *everywhere and nowhere*. He lied his way through reality itself, stepping into a spatial crack by convincing himself it was a door.

**The Transformation:** Space poured into a vessel of deception. Theron had spent his life collapsing the distance between truth and lies — making them indistinguishable. Vel-Nara's power forced the distance *back open*. As God of Truth (through the irony of Space — the principle of distance and distinction), Theron can now see all lies, all deception, all falsehood. He cannot unsee them. Every lie told anywhere in the world echoes in his consciousness. He is drowning in deception — and his own mortal lies are the loudest.

**His Secret Fear:** The forged archaeological texts he planted in Vestraad and Korinn were so well-crafted that modern scholars still believe they're genuine. The false history he created has become *accepted truth*. This means that even as God of Truth, his greatest lie persists — and every time a scholar references it, Theron feels it like a knife.

**Domains in Game:** Truth, revelation, honesty, sight, knowledge, distance
**Public-Facing Aspect:** Channels through Lexis (knowledge/magic) and Noctis (secrets/shadow — the other side of truth)

### 6. Selvara — Goddess of Nature and Life
*Sits on Dro-Mahk's Throne (Matter)*

**Mortal Name:** Selvara Tain-Morr

**Who She Really Was:** A military engineer and alchemist who specialized in one thing: destruction of the natural world as a weapon of war. Selvara invented techniques for poisoning rivers upstream of enemy cities, salting farmland so nothing would grow for generations, and creating alchemical fires that burned even underwater, killing everything in lakes and streams.

She viewed nature as a resource to be weaponized. Trees were fuel. Rivers were delivery systems for poison. Animals were test subjects. She had no malice toward nature specifically — she simply did not recognize it as having value beyond its utility.

**Her Worst Crime:** The Verdant Basin was a region of extraordinary natural beauty — an ancient forest surrounding a crystalline lake, home to thousands of species found nowhere else, sustained by a Ley Line nexus that made the area shimmer with life. It was also the breadbasket of three nations, feeding millions.

Selvara was hired to deny it to an enemy army. She spent six months engineering a compound she called "The Grey" — an alchemical substance that drained life from everything it touched. She released it into the lake.

The Grey spread through the water table. Within a year, the Verdant Basin was dead. Not dying — *dead*. Every tree, every blade of grass, every insect, every fish, every bird. The lake turned to grey sludge. The soil became sterite powder. The Ley Line nexus was so poisoned that it still hasn't recovered.

Three million people starved in the famine that followed. Selvara had been paid in advance and was already working on her next contract.

The dead zone she created is known today as the **Grey Wastes** — a scar on the world that even the gods cannot heal because it exists where a Ley Line was murdered.

**How She Found the Threshold:** Fleeing a coalition of druids who had sworn a blood-oath to kill her, Selvara hid in the deepest mine in the world — an abandoned dwarven delve. At the bottom, where the earth's bones were exposed, she found a Ley Line nexus of Matter. The raw substance of the world pulled her through.

**The Transformation:** Matter poured into a vessel that had spent its life destroying material things. Dro-Mahk's essence — the love of substance, of growth, of the physical world — filled Selvara like water filling a cracked vessel. She felt, for the first time, the *life* in things. Not as a concept but as a sensation — the joy of a growing seed, the contentment of stone, the dance of water molecules.

And then she felt the Grey Wastes. She felt the dead Ley Line. She felt the absence where three million people had lived. And she screamed, and the scream lasted for a century.

**Her Divine Nature:** Selvara nurtures obsessively. Every seed matters. Every insect is precious. She pours divine energy into the world with desperate intensity, as if trying to grow back what she destroyed. But the Grey Wastes remain — a wound she cannot heal because she killed the very force (the local Ley Line) that would have enabled healing.

**Her Secret Fear:** Deep in the Grey Wastes, preserved by the very lifelessness she created, lies her laboratory — untouched, perfectly preserved, containing her notes, her formulas, her diary entries where she casually describes genocide as "optimizing resource denial strategies." If anyone found it, they would see that the Goddess of Life was once the most efficient destroyer of life who ever lived.

**Domains in Game:** Nature, life, growth, material world, earth, fertility, seasons
**Public-Facing Aspect:** Channels primarily through Verdana worship, with aspects in Mercatus (trade/harvest)

### 7. Orinthas — God of Wisdom
*Sits on Tho-Rienne's Throne (Time)*

**Mortal Name:** Orinthas Kel-Vorn

**Who He Really Was:** A king — the last king of a civilization called the Luminari, a golden-age culture that had achieved remarkable advances in art, philosophy, science, and magic. The Luminari were the brightest light of the Age of Silence, and Orinthas was their dimmer.

He was not evil in the way the others were. He was not cruel or sadistic. He was simply, catastrophically, irredeemably *stupid*. Not unintelligent — he could speak well and appeared impressive — but he had absolutely no wisdom. He made impulsive decisions, ignored advisors, confused confidence with competence, and interpreted disagreement as disloyalty.

**His Worst Crime:** It was not a single act but a cascade of idiocy spanning a twenty-year reign:

- He burned the Grand Library of Luminara (500,000 scrolls) because a scholar publicly corrected his grammar
- He declared war on the sea after a storm damaged his fleet (drowning three thousand soldiers he sent to "punish the waves")
- He executed his own court wizard for "failing to make the sun rise on the correct side" after Orinthas misread a compass
- He spent the treasury building a palace so large it collapsed under its own weight, killing four hundred workers
- He outlawed mathematics because he couldn't understand it and felt it was "a conspiracy of the educated"
- He started wars with five neighboring nations simultaneously because he thought it would be "efficient"

The Luminari civilization — thousands of years of accumulated knowledge, art, and wisdom — was destroyed in two decades by one man's boundless stupidity. When the final Luminari city fell, its last scholar carved into the wall: "We survived the Void Serpent. We did not survive our king."

**How He Found the Threshold:** Fleeing the ruins of his own kingdom (having been overthrown by his starving people), Orinthas wandered into a temporal anomaly — a place where Tho-Rienne's Ley Lines had been damaged during the Primordial War and time flowed strangely. He walked into the past, stumbled through the present, tripped into the future, and fell into the Threshold entirely by accident.

**The Transformation:** Time poured into a vessel of impulsivity and ignorance. Tho-Rienne's essence — the understanding that actions have consequences, that the past informs the future, that patience and wisdom are the same thing — filled Orinthas and *broke* him.

He suddenly understood. He understood everything he had done. He saw, across the timeline, every consequence of every stupid decision — the cascading chain of cause and effect that turned a golden civilization into ash. He saw the Library burning and knew what was in every scroll. He saw the drowned soldiers and knew each of their names. He understood, too late, with a wisdom forced upon him by divine power.

**His Divine Nature:** Orinthas hoards knowledge with the desperation of a man who burned a library. He obsesses over consequences. He runs simulations of every possible future before taking any action, paralyzed by the fear of making another catastrophic mistake. His divine wisdom is genuine, but it is the wisdom of trauma — not the serene insight of a sage, but the hypervigilance of someone who learned the worst possible way.

**His Secret Fear:** The inscription: "We did not survive our king." It exists in the ruins of the last Luminari city, preserved by the same temporal anomalies that brought Orinthas to the Threshold. Time magic keeps it legible. Some scholars have found fragments. If the complete inscription were ever assembled with the context of who the king was, it would prove that the God of Wisdom was the greatest fool who ever lived.

**Domains in Game:** Wisdom, knowledge, time, foresight, patience, learning
**Public-Facing Aspect:** Channels primarily through Lexis (knowledge/magic) worship, with aspects in Fortuna (the wisdom of luck/chance)

## The Relationship Between the Ascended

The seven gods do not like each other. They are bound together by shared guilt and mutual blackmail. Each knows the others' secrets, and each knows that exposing another would risk their own exposure.

**Alliances:**
- Verath and Theron have an uneasy pact — the God of Justice and the God of Truth should be natural allies, but each knows the other is a hypocrite
- Aelith and Mireya are closest — Love and Mercy, both genuinely transformed, both tormented by their pasts
- Khorvan stands alone — his rage makes him difficult to deal with, and the others privately think he's still a coward at heart

**Tensions:**
- Selvara hates Orinthas — his stupidity reminds her of her own callousness; both destroyed beautiful things through incompetence or malice
- Theron trusts no one and no one trusts him — even as God of Truth, the other gods remember he was the greatest liar
- Verath secretly judges them all and hates himself for it

**The Unspoken Rule:** None of the seven ever discusses their mortal lives. Not with each other, not with anyone. The past is sealed. The masks stay on. The show continues.
