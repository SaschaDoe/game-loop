# Flora and Fauna of Aethermoor

## Overview

The natural world of Aethermoor is shaped by three forces: the Original Seven's lingering essence in the Ley Lines, the Ascended's corrupting influence, and the scars of the Primordial War. Plants and animals in this world are not merely decorative — they are living records of history, sources of magical ingredients, and sometimes, witnesses to truths the gods want buried.

---

## Magical Flora

### Truthtrees (Veritas Arborum)

**Appearance:** Ancient oaks with silver-white bark and leaves that shimmer between green and gold. Their roots glow faintly at night.

**Properties:** Truthtrees grow exclusively on Ley Line convergence points. Their roots tap directly into the Lines, absorbing not just magical energy but *information*. The trees "remember" events that occur near them, storing them as patterns in their growth rings.

**Lore Significance:** A truthtree that has grown for millennia may contain memories of the Age of Silence — including events the Ascended want forgotten. Primordialists know how to "read" the rings through a ritual that takes three days of meditation. The Veiled Hand has a standing order to burn any truthtree older than one thousand years. Fortunately, truthtrees are nearly indestructible — their roots draw directly on Ley Line energy to heal.

**Gameplay:** Players who learn the meditation ritual can gain lore fragments from ancient truthtrees. Older trees yield deeper secrets.

---

### Voidbloom (Flora Nihilis)

**Appearance:** Flowers with petals that are *not there*. They appear as flower-shaped holes in reality — the petals are visible as an absence of color and light, surrounded by a faint corona of distortion.

**Properties:** Voidblooms grow where the Void Serpent's influence has leaked through the cage. They absorb magic and light, creating small dead zones around them. Their "nectar" is a concentrated anti-magical substance that can temporarily nullify enchantments.

**Lore Significance:** Voidblooms are getting more common. In the last century, their range has expanded — quietly, without alarm, but druids have noticed. The cage is weakening.

**Gameplay:** Harvesting voidblooms is dangerous (they drain mana from nearby magic users) but valuable. Alchemists use them to create dispel potions. Void Touched cultivate them as magical focusing tools.

---

### Memory Moss (Mnemos Muscus)

**Appearance:** A soft, luminescent moss that glows a warm amber. It grows on stone surfaces in damp, dark environments — cave walls, tomb interiors, ruin foundations.

**Properties:** Memory moss absorbs the memories of beings who die in its vicinity. The memories are stored in the moss's bioluminescent structures and can be accessed by touch — a person pressing their palm to memory moss will experience a flash of the stored memory.

**Lore Significance:** Memory moss in ancient ruins often contains the final memories of people who died during the Purge of Memories — scholars, witnesses, and truth-keepers killed by the Veiled Hand. These fragments are incomplete and disorienting but potentially contain crucial pieces of the truth.

**Gameplay:** Memory moss patches function as lore dispensers. Each patch contains one memory fragment. Some are mundane (a miner's last thoughts), some are significant (a scholar realizing the truth about the Ascended moments before assassination), and a rare few are critical (direct witnesses to the Ascension itself).

---

### Ashbloom (Ignis Flora)

**Appearance:** Flowers that look like they're made of glowing embers — red and orange petals that radiate heat, black stems that smolder without burning.

**Properties:** Ashblooms grow only in the Ashlands, where Ira-Sethi burned so brightly during the Primordial War that the ground still holds residual energy. They are the only things that grow in the Ashlands, and they do so by metabolizing pure energy rather than sunlight and water.

**Lore Significance:** Ashblooms are living proof that the Ashlands were created by the Original Seven, not the Ascended. Their biology is incompatible with post-Ascension magical ecology — they predate the current divine order.

**Gameplay:** Ashbloom petals are a rare alchemical ingredient. They can be used in fire-resistance potions, energy restoration elixirs, and (in forbidden alchemy) to create explosives of devastating power.

---

### Weeping Willowshade (Lacrimosa Salix)

**Appearance:** Willow trees with bark that appears wet, as if perpetually crying. Their leaves are deep blue, and their hanging branches drip a clear, slightly luminous sap.

**Properties:** Weeping willowshades grow near sites of great tragedy. They are drawn to areas saturated with grief — battlefields, massacre sites, places where many people died in pain. The sap they produce has powerful healing properties but induces deep sadness in anyone who consumes it.

**Lore Significance:** The concentration of weeping willowshades in an area is a reliable indicator of forgotten tragedies. Several groves of weeping willowshades grow in locations that official history records as unremarkable — revealing that something terrible happened there that was covered up.

---

### Godrot Fungus (Divinus Putrescens)

**Appearance:** A black, spreading fungus with faintly glowing purple veins. Smells of ozone and old parchment.

**Properties:** Godrot grows on objects that have been touched by divine power and then abandoned. It feeds on residual divine energy, breaking it down into its component Ley Line streams. A godrot colony on an old temple wall slowly returns the divine energy to the raw Ley Lines beneath.

**Lore Significance:** Godrot is the natural world's immune response to the Ascended's corruption. It exists because the Ley Lines — the Original Seven — are trying to reclaim energy that was filtered through illegitimate divine intermediaries. The Ascended despise godrot and have ordered its destruction wherever found, claiming it's "a corruption of the Void Serpent." It isn't.

---

### Crystalvine (Prismatica Vitis)

**Appearance:** A climbing vine with translucent, crystal-like leaves that refract light into rainbows. Produces small, faceted fruits that look like gemstones.

**Properties:** Crystalvines are the last legacy of the Crystalborn Kingdoms. They were originally cultivated as living crystal technology — the vines channel Ley Line energy through their crystalline structures, creating ambient magical effects. Wild crystalvines produce minor magical phenomena: lights, warmth, gentle music.

**Lore Significance:** Crystalvines grow most vigorously in the Prismatic Ruins (former Crystal Citadel). Their persistence, millennia after the civilization that created them was destroyed, suggests that the Crystalborn's technology was more deeply integrated with the Ley Lines than anyone realized.

## Magical Fauna

### Ashwolves (Ignis Lupus)

**Appearance:** Wolves with fur that smolders constantly — dark grey base coat with ember-bright tips. Their eyes glow like coals. Their pawprints leave scorch marks.

**Behavior:** Ashwolves are pack hunters native to the Ashlands and surrounding regions. They are sustained by Ira-Sethi's residual energy rather than food — they eat for pleasure, not necessity. They are intelligent, possibly sentient, and have a complex social structure.

**Lore Significance:** Ashwolves were born from the Primordial War itself. They are not descendants of normal wolves — they are manifestations of Ira-Sethi's energy given animal form. Some Primordialists believe ashwolves carry fragments of Ira-Sethi's will, and that their behavior patterns encode messages from the sleeping Original God.

---

### Crystalline Stags (Prisma Cervidae)

**Appearance:** Deer with antlers and hooves made of living crystal. Their bodies are partially transparent, and Ley Line energy is visible flowing through them like light through fiber optics. When they run, they leave trails of prismatic light.

**Behavior:** Solitary, elusive, found only in deep forests near Ley Line convergences. They are drawn to Old Magic practitioners and flee from anyone carrying divine (Ascended) magic items. They can sense the difference between Old Magic and New Magic and react accordingly.

**Lore Significance:** Crystalline stags are living remnants of the Crystalborn era — animals that were enhanced by crystal technology and survived the civilization's destruction. They are nearly extinct, hunted by collectors for their crystal components and driven from their habitats by expanding civilization. The Grey Pilgrims protect them fiercely.

---

### Hollow Crows (Vacuus Corvidae)

**Appearance:** Crows that appear normal from a distance but, up close, are *hollow*. Their bodies are shells — feathered exteriors with nothing inside. They cast no shadow and make no sound when they fly.

**Behavior:** Hollow crows gather at Void Scars and places where reality is thin. They act as scouts for something — watching, waiting, reporting back to a presence that scholars cannot identify. They are harmless but deeply unsettling.

**Lore Significance:** Hollow crows are believed to be the Void Serpent's eyes — extensions of Xal-Nepheth's awareness that leak through the cage. They watch the world for the Serpent. Their increasing numbers (like the voidblooms) suggest the cage is weakening. Void Touched can communicate with them, but the information they provide is always tinged with the Serpent's agenda.

---

### Stone Turtles (Petra Testudo)

**Appearance:** Enormous turtles (house-sized to building-sized) with shells made of actual stone — granite, marble, or basite. Moss, trees, and even small ecosystems grow on their backs. The largest ones are mistaken for hills.

**Behavior:** Incredibly slow-moving, essentially mobile habitats. Stone turtles traverse ancient paths that follow Ley Lines, completing a circuit over decades or centuries. The ecosystems on their backs are unique micro-environments that don't exist anywhere else.

**Lore Significance:** Stone turtles are believed to be the smallest surviving Shapers — the entities that repaired the world after the Primordial War. They continue their repair work on a tiny scale, their movements subtly reinforcing Ley Lines wherever they walk. Harming a stone turtle is considered the gravest of ecological crimes among druids.

---

### Mirrorfish (Specularis Piscis)

**Appearance:** Fish with perfectly reflective scales that show not a reflection of the viewer but a reflection of *a different time*. Looking at a mirrorfish might show you the landscape as it was a thousand years ago.

**Behavior:** Found in bodies of water near temporal Ley Line damage (Tho-Rienne's scars). They swim in patterns that correspond to temporal flows — faster when time is stable, slower when it fluctuates. During temporal storms, they swim backward.

**Lore Significance:** Chronomancers prize mirrorfish — their scales are a key ingredient in time-manipulation potions and scrying devices. But harvesting them is dangerous because looking at their scales for too long can cause temporal disorientation.

---

### Griefmoths (Lacrimosa Lepidoptera)

**Appearance:** Large moths with wings patterned like teardrops. They glow with a soft blue-white bioluminescence. Their wingbeats produce a sound like distant weeping.

**Behavior:** Griefmoths are attracted to emotional pain. They gather around grieving individuals, recent battle sites, and places of tragedy. Despite their morbid attraction, they are beneficial — their presence absorbs and disperses concentrated negative emotional energy, preventing it from solidifying into haunts or emotional undead.

**Lore Significance:** Griefmoths are believed to be Aelith's (Goddess of Love) unconscious creation — manifestations of her overwhelming empathy given physical form. They appeared only after the Ascension. Scholars note that an entity who genuinely loved all things might unconsciously create beings whose purpose is to ease suffering.

## Dangerous Flora and Fauna

### Blightroot (Corruptus Radix)
A parasitic vine that grows on Ley Lines and slowly drains their energy. Where blightroot spreads, magic weakens. Large blightroot colonies can create temporary dead zones. Origin unknown — possibly a natural phenomenon, possibly planted by the Void Covenant.

### Screamcaps (Ululatus Fungus)
Mushrooms that grow in deep caves near Spirit Membrane thin-spots. When disturbed, they emit a psychic shriek that causes temporary madness. They feed on spirit energy that leaks through the Membrane. Used in forbidden alchemy for madness-inducing poisons.

### Ironjaw Beetles (Ferrum Mandibula)
Insects with mandibles made of naturally-occurring metal. They consume iron and steel, excreting purified ore. Useful to miners, devastating to armored warriors. Some colonies have been weaponized by placing them in enemy supply lines.

### Mana Leeches (Arcanus Hirudo)
Transparent, slug-like creatures found near magical water sources. They attach to magic users and drain mana. A severe infestation can leave a mage unable to cast for days. Alchemists breed them for mana-absorbing poultices.

### Veil Spiders (Membrana Aranea)
Spiders that spin webs across the Spirit Membrane, trapping spirits. Their venom can temporarily sever a soul from its body (inducing out-of-body experiences). Shamans use controlled veil spider bites for spirit world travel. Uncontrolled bites can cause permanent soul damage.

## Extinct or Near-Extinct Species

### Ley Line Whales
Enormous cetaceans that swam through Ley Lines like rivers — traveling through magical currents beneath the earth. Hunted to extinction during the War of Silence for their magically-saturated bones and oil. Only skeletal remains in deep caves remain.

### Sunbirds
Birds with feathers of literal fire that nested near Ira-Sethi's nexus. They went dormant when the Ascended took the thrones — the change in magical frequency made their environment uninhabitable. Eggs may exist in stasis, waiting for Old Magic to return.

### Dreamcats
Feline creatures that existed partially in the dream world, visible only to sleeping or meditating individuals. They went extinct during the Spirit Silencing phase of the Purge of Memories, when the Spirit Membrane was thickened. Rare sightings are still reported by deep meditators.
