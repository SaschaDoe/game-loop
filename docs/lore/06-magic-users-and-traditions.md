# Magic Users and Traditions

## The Six Traditions

Magic users in Aethermoor are not a monolith. They belong to distinct traditions, each with its own philosophy, history, methods, and relationship to the truth about the Ascended.

---

## 1. The Thaumaturges (Temple Mages)

**Philosophy:** Magic is a gift from the Ascended, channeled through prayer and devotion.

**History:** The Thaumaturges arose in the first century after the Ascension, when the newly-throned gods began actively channeling magic to their worshippers. The first Thaumaturges were priests who discovered they could heal, smite, and protect through faith alone.

Over the millennia, Thaumaturgy became the dominant magical tradition — it was reliable, learnable, and came with the full institutional support of the major religions. Every temple has Thaumaturges. Every army wants them.

**Methods:**
- Prayer-based casting (spoken words directed at a specific Ascended)
- Holy symbols as focusing tools
- Divine channeling — the Ascended actively provides the magic, the caster is a conduit
- Restricted to spells the patron deity approves of (Khorvan won't grant healing; Mireya won't grant destruction)

**Relationship to Truth:**
Thaumaturges are the most deeply invested in the official mythology. Their magic literally comes from the Ascended, creating a dependency that makes questioning the gods existentially terrifying. If the gods are frauds, what is their magic?

In truth, Thaumaturgy works because the Ascended *are* gods — they did claim the thrones, they do control the Ley Lines. The magic is real. But it flows through corrupted intermediaries, which is why Thaumaturgy carries the strongest taint (see [05-magic-system.md](05-magic-system.md)).

**Notable Organizations:**
- The Church of the Radiant Sun (mainstream Thaumaturgy)
- Paladins of the Dawn (holy warriors)
- Sisters/Brothers of Mercy (divine healers)
- Inquisitors (divine enforcers — who hunt practitioners of other traditions)

---

## 2. The Arcanists (Academic Mages)

**Philosophy:** Magic is a natural force that can be understood, measured, and controlled through study.

**History:** The Arcanists trace their lineage to the Luminari civilization — specifically, to the scholars who survived Orinthas's destruction of their culture. After the Ascension, scattered Luminari academics gathered in secret to preserve what knowledge had survived their king's idiotic purges.

They established the first Mages' Guilds — institutions dedicated to the systematic study of magic as a natural phenomenon rather than a divine gift. This put them in immediate tension with the Thaumaturges, who viewed magic as sacred.

**Methods:**
- Incantation (memorized formulas derived from mathematical patterns in the Ley Lines)
- Rune inscription for persistent effects
- Ritual casting for large-scale magic
- Research-based spell development (Arcanists *invent* new spells through experimentation)

**Relationship to Truth:**
Arcanists are closest to discovering the truth through pure research. Their mathematical analysis of the Ley Lines has revealed inconsistencies — the Lines predate the Ascended, which contradicts official mythology. Some Arcanist researchers have been quietly disappeared by the Veiled Hand (see [07-factions-and-orders.md](07-factions-and-orders.md)) when they got too close.

The irony is exquisite: the Arcanists descend from the Luminari, whose destruction was caused by Orinthas — now the God of Wisdom, patron of learning. Some Arcanists worship Lexis (one of Orinthas's aspects) without knowing they're praying to the fool who burned their ancestors' libraries.

**Notable Organizations:**
- The Mages' Guild (international network of academic magic)
- The Arcane Conservatory (elite research institution)
- The Grey Researchers (Arcanists who study dead zones and forbidden topics)

---

## 3. The Primordialists (Old Magic Practitioners)

**Philosophy:** True magic predates the gods. The Ley Lines are the source, not the Ascended.

**History:** The Primordialists are the oldest continuous magical tradition — they predate the Ascension itself. During the Age of Silence, all magic was Primordial. When the Ascended took the thrones and began filtering magic through themselves, most practitioners adapted. A few refused.

These holdouts — mostly druids, elder elves, and isolated hermits — continued to draw directly from the Ley Lines, bypassing the Ascended's control. This made them both more powerful (access to raw, unfiltered magic) and more dangerous (no divine safety net).

**Methods:**
- Direct Ley Line attunement (meditation at nexus points, years of practice)
- Nature-based casting (aligned with seasonal and lunar cycles)
- Oral tradition (spells passed down through memory, never written — writing them down would make them detectable)
- Symbiotic magic (working with the Ley Lines rather than commanding them)

**Relationship to Truth:**
The Primordialists *know* the Ascended are not the original gods. Their oral traditions preserve fragments of the truth — stories of a time before the thrones were claimed, memories of the Original Seven's sacrifice. But their knowledge is incomplete and fragmented after millennia of suppression.

The Primordialists are hunted by the Inquisitors (who call them heretics) and watched by the Veiled Hand (who view them as a threat to the status quo). Practicing Primordial magic is not illegal in most places, but it is viewed with deep suspicion.

**Notable Organizations:**
- Druids of the Wild (the most visible Primordialist group)
- The Root Singers (underground network of Primordial practitioners)
- The Listening Stones (hermit order that meditates at Ley Line nexuses)

---

## 4. The Blood Singers (Blood Mages)

**Philosophy:** The body is a Ley Line. Life itself is magic. No god required.

**History:** Blood magic is the oldest *mortal* magical tradition — older even than Primordialism. In the earliest days of the Age of Silence, before anyone understood Ley Lines, some mortals discovered that their own blood carried magical potential. Life energy — the fundamental spark that separates living from dead — could be channeled, shaped, and directed.

Blood singing was originally not sinister. The first Blood Singers were healers who used their own vitality to cure the sick. Warriors who burned their life force for superhuman strength. Mothers who sang blood-songs to protect their children.

It became forbidden after the Ascension because Blood Magic is the one tradition that requires *nothing* from the gods. No prayer, no Ley Line access, no divine permission. Just a heartbeat and the will to spend it. The Ascended cannot monitor, regulate, or control Blood Magic, and this terrifies them.

**Methods:**
- HP-based casting (spending life force instead of mana)
- Blood rituals (collective life-force pooling)
- Sanguine inscription (writing spells in blood for persistent effects)
- Vitality manipulation (healing or harming by redirecting life energy)

**Relationship to Truth:**
Blood Singers are pragmatists. They don't particularly care about the gods' true nature because their magic doesn't depend on the gods. However, the very existence of Blood Magic is evidence against the official mythology — if the Ascended created all magic, why does Blood Magic exist independently?

Some Blood Singer elders preserve stories of the pre-Ascension world that contradict temple teachings. They don't evangelize these stories, but they don't hide them either.

**Notable Organizations:**
- The Crimson Pact (secret society of blood mages)
- The Heart's Fire (combat-focused blood mage warriors)
- Wandering Blood Singers (traveling healers in rural areas where temples are scarce)

---

## 5. The Void Touched (Void Mages)

**Philosophy:** The Void is not evil. It is the other half of creation. To understand reality, you must understand its absence.

**History:** The Void Touched are the most controversial tradition. They draw power from Xal-Nepheth — the Void Serpent caged beneath the world. Their detractors call them mad cultists. Their defenders (few as they are) argue that understanding the Void is necessary to ensure the cage holds.

The first Void Touched appeared during the Age of Silence, when the Void Serpent's whispers first began leaking through the weakening cage. Most who heard the whispers went mad. A few — those with unusual mental fortitude — learned to listen without losing themselves.

After the Ascension, the Ascended suppressed the Void Touched with extreme prejudice. The gods know better than anyone how dangerous the Void Serpent truly is — and they also know that Void Magic can see through divine deception, which makes it existentially threatening to their secret.

**Methods:**
- Void attunement (meditating in Void Scars until the whispers become coherent)
- Unmaking (dissolving matter, energy, or magic back into nothing)
- Void Step (moving through the spaces between spaces — a form of teleportation that passes through the Void)
- Anti-magic (creating zones where magic cannot function — essentially, invoking local Unraveling)

**Relationship to Truth:**
The Void Touched are the most likely to discover the full truth, because Xal-Nepheth *remembers*. The Void Serpent was present for the Primordial War, witnessed the Original Seven's sacrifice, and observed the Ascended claiming the thrones. It whispers fragments of this truth to its practitioners — not out of benevolence, but because the truth about the Ascended weakens mortal faith, which weakens the Ascended, which weakens the cage.

The great paradox of Void Magic: pursuing truth through the enemy of all existence.

**Notable Organizations:**
- Cult of the Void (the most visible — and most unstable — Void practitioners)
- The Silent Listeners (scholars who study the Void academically without drawing power from it)
- The Cage Wardens (a tiny, secret order that monitors the cage's integrity — they use Void Magic defensively)

## 6. The Runeweavers (Symbol Mages)

**Philosophy:** The universe is a language. Magic is grammar. Spells are sentences.

**History:** Runeweavers arose from the Dwarven runic tradition — the discovery that certain symbols, when inscribed correctly, channel Ley Line energy without any incantation or divine invocation. The symbol itself does the work.

This discovery was revolutionary because it suggested that magic had *rules* — not divine whims, but consistent, learnable, exploitable rules. If the right symbol in the right material at the right convergence point always produces the same effect, then magic is not supernatural at all. It's a technology.

**Methods:**
- Runic inscription (permanent spells carved into materials)
- Sigil casting (temporary symbols drawn in the air, on the ground, or on paper)
- Glyph weaving (combining multiple runes into complex programs)
- Artificing (building magical devices — the Clockwork Creed's specialty)

**Relationship to Truth:**
Runeweavers have inadvertently discovered that the rune system predates the Ascended. The most fundamental runes — the ones dwarven records trace back to their earliest history — correspond to the Original Seven's domains, not the Ascended's public-facing aspects. This is a quiet scandal in Runeweaver academic circles, but most practitioners are too focused on practical applications to pursue the theological implications.

**Notable Organizations:**
- The Clockwork Creed (rune-based artificing religion)
- Dwarven Runemasters (traditional craftsmen-mages)
- The Living Library (scholars who tattoo runes on their bodies for portable magic)

## Persecution and Politics

The six traditions exist in a complex web of alliances and conflicts:

| Tradition | Allies | Enemies | Status |
|-----------|--------|---------|--------|
| Thaumaturges | Runeweavers | Blood Singers, Void Touched | Dominant, institutional power |
| Arcanists | Runeweavers | Void Touched (publicly) | Respected, academic power |
| Primordialists | Blood Singers | Thaumaturges (Inquisitors) | Marginalized, rural |
| Blood Singers | Primordialists | Thaumaturges | Hunted, underground |
| Void Touched | None | Everyone | Persecuted, hidden |
| Runeweavers | Arcanists, Thaumaturges | None (neutral) | Respected, practical |

The Inquisitors (Thaumaturge enforcers) actively hunt Blood Singers and Void Touched. Primordialists are tolerated in rural areas but harassed in cities. Arcanists maintain a careful neutrality — they know too much to be comfortable but not enough to be targets.

## The Convergence Prophecy

An ancient Primordialist prophecy — predating the Ascension — speaks of a time when "all streams become one river, all voices become one song, and the Singer unmakes the Mask."

Modern interpreters believe this refers to a practitioner who can channel all seven streams simultaneously — something that should be impossible since each stream is controlled by a different Ascended. Such a person could, in theory, access the raw Ley Lines beneath the Ascended's filter and see the truth.

The Ascended are aware of this prophecy. They have been quietly ensuring that no one masters more than two or three streams.

The player character, of course, has no such limitation.
