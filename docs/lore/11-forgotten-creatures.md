# Forgotten Creatures — Beings Lost to Time and Memory

## Overview

Before the gods, before the civilizations, before the mortal races walked the earth, other things existed. The Primordial Age and the Age of Silence produced beings so vast, so strange, and so fundamentally *different* from modern life that they have been forgotten — erased not just by the Ascended's cover-up but by the sheer inability of mortal minds to comprehend them.

Some sleep. Some are dead. Some are something in between. All of them are dangerous — not necessarily because they're hostile, but because encountering something that shouldn't exist tends to break the people who witness it.

---

## The Architects

**What They Were:**
The Architects were the first sapient beings after the Original Seven — entities that arose during the world's formation, shaped by the raw creative energy of the Shaping. They were not alive in the biological sense. They were *processes* — living construction programs, each dedicated to building a specific aspect of the world.

Some Architects shaped mountains. Others carved rivers. Others wove weather patterns. They were enormous — the smallest was the size of a hill, the largest stretched across horizons — and they were tireless.

**What Happened to Them:**
When the Shaping was complete — when the world was built — the Architects had no more purpose. Most went dormant, their vast bodies becoming geological features. The Sleeping Giants (mountain ranges shaped like enormous humanoid bodies) are dormant Architects.

A few Architects did not sleep. These "Wakeful Architects" continued building, modifying, reshaping — unable to stop. They became problems. Mountains appeared where there shouldn't be mountains. Rivers changed course overnight. Entire forests grew in hours. The early mortal civilizations learned to avoid areas where Wakeful Architects roamed.

**Where They Are Now:**
Most Architects are so deeply dormant that they are indistinguishable from geography. But deep earthquakes sometimes reveal hollow spaces inside mountains that contain dormant Architect *organs* — pumps the size of cathedrals, valves made of living stone, cooling systems that span kilometers.

Three Wakeful Architects are known to still be active:
- **The Groundwalker** — A continent-spanning entity that moves so slowly it's only detectable by seismic instruments. It has been walking in the same direction for ten thousand years. No one knows where it's going.
- **The Rivermother** — An Architect that shapes waterways. Rivers near its location change course every few decades. Settlements have learned to build on stilts.
- **The Cloudshaper** — An Architect that maintains weather patterns. It's the reason certain regions have reliable seasons. Some scholars suspect it's the only reason weather works at all.

**Player Interaction:**
- Finding and entering a dormant Architect is the equivalent of exploring a living dungeon
- Waking an Architect is a world-altering event (and possibly a quest objective)
- Communicating with a Wakeful Architect requires Primordial language fluency and extreme patience

## The Dreamweavers

**What They Were:**
The Dreamweavers existed in the space between consciousness and reality — born from Pho-Lumen's (Spirit) influence during the Shaping. They were not physical beings. They existed purely in the realm of thought, dream, and imagination.

Dreamweavers appeared in the dreams of sleeping beings as shimmering, constantly-shifting forms — part light, part music, part emotion. They communicated through feelings rather than words and experienced time as a non-linear tapestry.

Their purpose (if they had one) seemed to be the maintenance of the dream world — the vast, shared unconscious space that all sleeping minds briefly touch. They wove dreams like tapestries, using the emotional energy of dreamers as thread.

**What Happened to Them:**
The Ascension disrupted the Spirit stream. When Aelith claimed Pho-Lumen's throne, the flood of her retroactive love energy overwhelmed the delicate frequency that the Dreamweavers existed on. Most were dissolved — their consciousness scattered into the ambient emotional background of the world.

A handful survived by retreating into the deepest layers of dream — the places so far from waking consciousness that even the Ascended's influence doesn't reach. These survivor Dreamweavers have become something different: no longer weavers of pleasant dreams but guardians of the deep unconscious, where the most primal fears and desires live.

**Where They Are Now:**
The surviving Dreamweavers exist in the **Dreaming Deep** — the lowest layer of the dream world, accessible only through profound meditation, specific alchemical compounds, or natural talent. They are not hostile, but they are alien. Their communication is pure emotion, which can be overwhelming — some who encounter them go mad.

**Player Interaction:**
- Players with high Wisdom and Spirit attunement may encounter Dreamweavers during rest at Ley Line nexuses
- Dreamweaver encounters take the form of surreal dream sequences with hidden lore
- A friendly Dreamweaver can serve as a guide through the dream world, revealing memories the Ascended tried to suppress
- The Dreamweaver questline involves helping them rebuild the dream tapestry — and discovering what it shows about the world's true history

## The Hollow Ones

**What They Were:**
The Hollow Ones were the servants of the Original Seven — not created deliberately but arising naturally from the Seven's existence. Just as a fire creates light and a river creates current, the Original Seven's presence created the Hollow Ones: beings whose purpose was to serve, maintain, and protect the principles they embodied.

Each Original Seven had a type of Hollow One:
- **Aum-Varek's Hollow Ones:** Pattern Keepers — beings that maintained mathematical constants and physical laws
- **Kha-Siel's Hollow Ones:** Change Dancers — beings that guided transformation and evolution
- **Tho-Rienne's Hollow Ones:** Moment Wardens — beings that preserved cause-and-effect
- **Vel-Nara's Hollow Ones:** Distance Singers — beings that maintained spatial relationships
- **Dro-Mahk's Hollow Ones:** Substance Guardians — beings that ensured material integrity
- **Ira-Sethi's Hollow Ones:** Spark Carriers — beings that distributed energy
- **Pho-Lumen's Hollow Ones:** Soul Shepherds — beings that guided consciousness

**What Happened to Them:**
When the Original Seven sacrificed themselves, the Hollow Ones lost their masters. They continued their tasks autonomously for a time — Pattern Keepers maintaining physics, Change Dancers guiding seasons, Soul Shepherds guiding the dead. But without direction, they gradually *emptied*. Their purpose drained away, leaving shells — animate but mindless, going through motions without understanding.

When the Ascended claimed the thrones, they tried to repurpose the Hollow Ones. Some accepted the new masters. Most didn't — the Hollow Ones were tuned to the Original Seven's frequency, and the Ascended's corrupted signal was incompatible.

**Where They Are Now:**
Most Hollow Ones have degenerated into:
- **Wandering Shells:** Empty entities that walk ancient paths, performing tasks that no longer have meaning. A Pattern Keeper might endlessly arrange stones in mathematical patterns. A Change Dancer might transform the same rock from iron to gold to lead to iron, forever.
- **Broken Guardians:** Hollow Ones that were partially repurposed by the Ascended but incompletely. They guard locations for reasons no one remembers, attacking anyone who approaches. Many "dungeon guardians" are degraded Hollow Ones.
- **Merged Entities:** Some Hollow Ones, desperate for purpose, merged with natural features — becoming the spirits of rivers, mountains, and forests. These are the "nature spirits" that druids commune with.

A rare few Hollow Ones remain fully conscious — those that retreated to Ley Line nexuses and drew sustenance directly from the Original Seven's essence. These are incredibly powerful and incredibly lonely. They remember their masters. They feel the wrongness of the Ascended. And they have waited millennia for someone to listen.

**Player Interaction:**
- Conscious Hollow Ones are among the most important NPCs in the game
- They possess knowledge of the Original Seven that no other source can provide
- Earning their trust requires proving you can hear the Old Magic (high Primordial attunement)
- They can teach Old Magic techniques unavailable from any other source
- At endgame, the Hollow Ones are crucial allies for the Restoration questline

## The Void-Born

**What They Were:**
When the Void Serpent attacked during the Primordial War, its influence warped some creatures — twisting them into things that should not exist. These Void-Born were not the Serpent's servants; they were *mistakes* — accidents of destruction, created when the Unraveling touched living things without fully destroying them.

Void-Born defy categorization. They exist in a state between being and non-being. They have bodies that are partially absent — a Void-Born wolf might have three legs and the memory of a fourth that shifts in and out of existence. A Void-Born bird might fly with wings that are sometimes there and sometimes not, its flight pattern stuttering between physical and impossible.

**What Happened to Them:**
Most Void-Born were destroyed during the Primordial War or shortly after — their unstable existence meant most dissolved within years. But a few proved stable enough to reproduce, creating lineages of Void-touched creatures that persist to this day.

**Where They Are Now:**
Void-Born are found near Void Scars and in the deep underground, where the Void Serpent's influence seeps through the cage. They are not inherently malicious — they are suffering. Their partial existence is painful. Some lash out from pain. Others are eerily gentle, as if grateful for any interaction that acknowledges their existence.

**Player Interaction:**
- Void-Born are encountered as enemies in Void Scar areas
- Some can be communicated with (requires Void Magic or specific items)
- A Void-Born companion is possible through a specific questline
- The most ancient Void-Born carry memories of the Primordial War — distorted, nightmarish, but genuine

## The First Dead

**What They Were:**
Before Pho-Lumen's sacrifice created the Spirit Membrane, there was no barrier between life and death. The dead didn't go "somewhere" — they simply persisted, their consciousness fading slowly like echoes. The First Dead are the oldest echoes — beings who died during the Shaping or the early Age of Silence, whose fading was arrested when the Spirit Membrane formed, trapping them in a state of permanent almost-gone.

**Where They Are Now:**
The First Dead exist in the deepest layer of the spirit world — a place called the **Grey Shore**, where consciousness is so faded that individual identity barely exists. They are less ghosts than *impressions* — feelings of ancient, alien minds that experienced a world completely different from the modern one.

Shamans who reach the Grey Shore report:
- Feelings of vast age and vast patience
- Sensory experiences that don't correspond to mortal senses (seeing sounds, tasting colors)
- Fragments of knowledge about the world's creation that predate even the Original Seven
- A profound, almost unbearable loneliness

**Player Interaction:**
- Reaching the Grey Shore is an endgame spirit world challenge
- The First Dead can provide the oldest lore in the game — information about the Weave and the Unraveling
- Communicating with them requires abandoning normal perception (a gameplay mechanic involving altered UI)
- One First Dead — the oldest consciousness in existence — may hold the key to understanding why the universe exists at all

## The Leviathans

**What They Were:**
The Leviathans were the deep-sea equivalents of the Architects — vast entities that shaped the oceans, the ocean floors, and the underwater Ley Lines during the Shaping. Where the Architects worked in stone and earth, the Leviathans worked in water, pressure, and current.

**Where They Are Now:**
The Leviathans are still active — they simply operate so deep and so slowly that surface-dwellers rarely encounter them. The deepest ocean trenches are shaped by Leviathan movement. The great ocean currents are maintained by their passage.

Three named Leviathans are known:
- **Thalassion** — The largest, maintaining the global ocean current system. Its body is hundreds of miles long.
- **Abyssia** — A Leviathan that maintains the deep-sea thermal vents. Without it, the ocean floor ecosystem would collapse.
- **The Dreaming Deep** — A Leviathan that may or may not be alive. It rests at the bottom of the deepest trench, and the pressure around it is so intense that reality behaves differently. Sailors who pass above it have nightmares of vast, patient intelligence.

**Player Interaction:**
- Leviathans are encountered during underwater questlines
- They are not enemies — they are forces of nature
- Communicating with a Leviathan requires Primordial language and a willingness to think on a geological timescale
- A Leviathan's favor can open underwater paths to otherwise unreachable locations

## Why They Were Forgotten

The Ascended erased knowledge of these beings for different reasons:
- **Architects and Leviathans:** Proof that the world predates the Ascended
- **Dreamweavers:** Witnesses to the original dream-tapestry, which showed the truth
- **Hollow Ones:** Servants of the Original Seven — living proof of the true gods
- **Void-Born:** Evidence of the Primordial War's true nature
- **First Dead:** Memories older than the Ascended's lies

The Ascended couldn't destroy these beings — they're too fundamental to the world's functioning. So they settled for making mortals forget they existed. In a world where the official history begins with "the Ascended created everything," there's no room for beings that predate the creators.

But forgotten is not gone. And some things remember, even when no one remembers them.
