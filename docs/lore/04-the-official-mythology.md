# The Official Mythology — The Lies the Gods Tell

## Overview

What follows is the mythology as mortals know it — the version taught in temples, written in scriptures, and accepted as truth by nearly everyone in the world. Every word is a fabrication crafted by the Ascended to hide their mortal identities.

The Ascended spent their first centuries of divinity meticulously constructing this false history. They rewrote the world's memories, burned contradicting records, silenced witnesses (through the Purge of Memories), and seeded false artifacts and scriptures across the world.

This is what the average person in Aethermoor believes:

---

## The Official Creation Story

*As taught by the Church of the Radiant Sun and most major temples:*

"In the beginning, there was the Void — chaos and nothing. From the Void, by some mystery beyond mortal comprehension, seven divine beings emerged — born of light, forged in purpose, destined to create.

These Seven — whom we know as the Ascended — looked upon the Void and found it wanting. They spoke the **Words of Making**, and the world took shape: Verath laid down the law of nature, Khorvan sparked the fire of life, Selvara wove the green mantle, Theron mapped the stars, Aelith breathed love into the first creatures, Mireya taught them mercy, and Orinthas gave them the gift of thought.

Then came the Void Serpent — a remnant of the chaos that resented creation. The Seven fought it for a hundred years and sealed it beneath the world. They did not sacrifice themselves — they *triumphed*, and from their victory built the divine order that sustains us."

**The Lies:**
- The Ascended did not emerge from the Void — the Original Seven did
- The Ascended did not speak Words of Making — the world existed long before them
- The Ascended did not fight the Void Serpent — the Original Seven did, and died doing it
- The Ascended merely sat on empty thrones and claimed credit for everything

## The Official Biographies

### Verath's Official Story
*"The Eternal Judge"*

"Verath was the first to perceive injustice in the Void — the imbalance between something and nothing. From this perception, he forged the concept of fairness itself. He is the foundation upon which civilization rests, the scale upon which all things are measured.

In mortal form, before his ascension, Verath walked among the people as a humble traveling judge, settling disputes between villages, asking nothing in return. He is said to have judged ten thousand cases and never once erred. The scales he carries are the original Scales of Creation, used to measure the first sunrise against the first sunset."

**The Truth:** Every element is invented. Verath never judged fairly in his life. The "humble traveling judge" story was fabricated to explain his knowledge of legal systems.

---

### Aelith's Official Story
*"The First Heart"*

"Aelith looked upon the newly created creatures and saw they were alone. She wept, and from her tears grew the first flowers. She laughed, and from her laughter came the first birdsong. She touched the first two mortals and gave them the ability to love — the greatest gift of all.

Before ascending, Aelith was said to be a healer who wandered the world, mending broken hearts with nothing but her presence. She adopted orphans, reconciled enemies, and once stopped a war by standing between two armies and singing a lullaby."

**The Truth:** The most cruelly ironic biography. Every warm detail is the opposite of the real Aelith, who sold her own children and destroyed every relationship she touched.

---

### Khorvan's Official Story
*"The Shield-Bearer"*

"Khorvan stood at the front of the divine host when the Void Serpent attacked. While others planned, Khorvan charged. His war cry shook the foundations of reality. He held the line for seven days while the other gods prepared the binding ritual, taking wound after wound but never retreating. Never yielding. Never afraid.

In mortal form, Khorvan was said to be a warrior-king who defended the weak, charged into every battle first, and personally fought three dragons in single combat. His famous last words before ascending were: 'I have never known fear.'"

**The Truth:** The most outrageous lie. Khorvan ran from *every* fight. "I have never known fear" was fabricated to be the exact opposite of his truth — fear was the only thing Khorvan ever knew.

### Mireya's Official Story
*"The Gentle Hand"*

"Mireya saw suffering in the world and could not bear it. She knelt beside the first wounded creature and placed her hands upon its injury, and from her touch flowed healing. She vowed that no being would suffer while she had power to prevent it.

Before ascending, Mireya was a physician who traveled to plague-stricken villages, tended the sick without payment, and once cured an entire city of a magical disease by absorbing it into herself — nearly dying in the process."

**The Truth:** Mireya caused more suffering than any plague. Her "healing hands" were the same hands that operated torture instruments.

### Theron's Official Story
*"The All-Seeing Eye"*

"Theron opened his eyes and saw truth in all things — the truth of the stars, the truth of the earth, the truth of the heart. He could not be deceived, and in turn, he deceived no one. He gifted mortals with the capacity for honest speech and the ability to distinguish truth from falsehood.

Before ascending, Theron was a monk who took a vow of absolute truth, never speaking a false word even when truth brought him harm. He was imprisoned, beaten, and exiled, but never once compromised his commitment to honesty."

**The Truth:** The most deliberately constructed lie, crafted by history's greatest liar. Theron wrote this biography himself, knowing that the irony would amuse him for eternity.

### Selvara's Official Story
*"The Green Mother"*

"Selvara sang, and the world bloomed. From her song grew the forests, the grasslands, the flowers, and the vines. She breathed upon the seas and they teemed with life. She touched the mountains and they grew gardens in their folds.

Before ascending, Selvara was a druid of extraordinary power who spent her mortal life planting forests, healing blighted lands, and nurturing endangered species back from extinction. She once spent forty years growing a single tree — the World Oak — which still stands as the oldest living thing."

**The Truth:** Selvara destroyed more living things than any natural disaster. The Grey Wastes — which the official mythology blames on "a Void Serpent wound" — are her doing.

---

### Orinthas' Official Story
*"The Sage Eternal"*

"Orinthas contemplated the universe and understood it. He perceived the patterns in chaos, the order in entropy, the meaning in randomness. From his understanding came wisdom, and from his wisdom came the gift of reason that elevates mortals above beasts.

Before ascending, Orinthas was a philosopher-king whose reign was a golden age of learning. He built great libraries, funded scholars, encouraged debate, and made decisions so wise that his kingdom knew a century of unbroken peace and prosperity."

**The Truth:** Every detail is the exact opposite. His kingdom fell into ruin. He burned libraries. His decisions were catastrophically idiotic.

## How the Lies Are Maintained

### The Divine Scriptures
The Ascended dictated their official biographies to the first priests through divine visions — carefully crafted dreams that felt absolutely real. These visions included sensory details (the smell of flowers for Aelith's story, the clash of swords for Khorvan's) that made them vivid and memorable. The first priests genuinely believed what they wrote.

### Temple Education
Every major temple teaches the official mythology as absolute truth. Questioning it is considered blasphemy in the Church of the Radiant Sun and most mainstream religions. Children learn these stories before they learn to read.

### The Memory Purge
(See [13-the-cover-up.md](13-the-cover-up.md) for full details)
In the first century after the Ascension, the gods systematically destroyed evidence of the Age of Silence — burning libraries, collapsing ruins, erasing inscriptions, and even altering memories of the eldest immortal beings (elves, dragons, spirits).

### Divine Miracles
The Ascended occasionally perform miracles that seem to confirm their official stories — healing the sick (Mireya), bringing rain to drought-stricken lands (Selvara), delivering justice to the wicked (Verath). These are genuine divine acts that serve double duty as propaganda.

### Self-Reinforcing Belief
After millennia, the lies have become so deeply embedded in culture that they are self-reinforcing. Scholars who find contradicting evidence usually conclude they've misread the evidence, not that the gods are lying.

## Cracks in the Official Story

Despite the Ascended's thoroughness, contradictions exist:

1. **The Grey Wastes:** Official mythology blames the Void Serpent, but geological evidence shows the destruction came from *above* (alchemical), not *below* (divine)

2. **The Irongate Inscription:** Khorvan's name carved in a buried gate with the word "COWARD" — dismissed by temples as "demonic graffiti"

3. **The Thornhaven Documents:** Copies of legal documents from a trial that should not exist, found occasionally in ancient caches

4. **The Luminari Ruins:** Temporal anomalies preserve fragments of a civilization that the official history claims never existed

5. **The Brother War Records:** Historians have noted that the forged documents that caused the war are *too perfect* — suggesting a master forger, not political incompetence

6. **Spirit World Witnesses:** The very old ghosts — those from the Age of Silence — tell stories that contradict temple teachings. The temples explain this as "spirit confusion" caused by the trauma of death

7. **The World Oak:** Selvara's official story claims she grew it, but arborist analysis shows the tree predates the Ascension by thousands of years

8. **Dragon Memories:** The eldest dragons remember a time before the current gods. They do not speak of it, but their silence is telling.
