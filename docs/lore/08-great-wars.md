# The Great Wars of Aethermoor

## Timeline

The history of Aethermoor is written in blood. Seven major wars shaped the world, each leaving scars that persist to this day — in the land, in the people, and in the lies the gods tell about them.

---

## 1. The Primordial War (Before Recorded Time)

**Combatants:** The Original Seven vs. Xal-Nepheth (the Void Serpent)
**Duration:** Unknown — time itself was unstable during the conflict
**Outcome:** Mutual destruction — the Original Seven sacrificed themselves to cage the Serpent

**What Happened:**
The Void Serpent attacked creation itself, eating reality from the outside in. The Original Seven fought back with everything they were — not armies, not weapons, but their very existence as living principles.

The war was not fought on battlefields. It was fought in the fabric of reality. Where the Serpent advanced, space collapsed, time looped, matter dissolved. Where the Seven held, reality crystalized with desperate intensity — mountains that shouldn't exist, forests that grew in seconds, rivers that flow uphill.

**Lasting Scars:**
- **The Ashlands** — where Ira-Sethi (Energy) burned so brightly that the ground still glows
- **The Timeless Wastes** — where Tho-Rienne (Time) was wounded and time became unreliable
- **The Hollow Sea** — where Dro-Mahk (Matter) was torn and reality is thin
- **The Silence Peaks** — where Pho-Lumen (Spirit) screamed so loud that sound itself broke
- **Void Scars** — scattered wounds where the Serpent's touch erased reality permanently

**What the Official History Says:**
"The Ascended fought the Void Serpent and triumphed through courage and divine power."

**The Truth:**
The Ascended weren't born yet. They had nothing to do with it.

## 2. The War of Silence (Age of Silence, ~1,800 years before Ascension)

**Combatants:** The Aetherian Empire vs. the Crystalborn Kingdoms vs. various independent nations
**Duration:** 140 years (on and off)
**Outcome:** Stalemate — both empires survived but were permanently weakened

**What Happened:**
Without gods to impose divine order, mortal civilizations competed for control of Ley Line nexuses — the most powerful magical resources in the world. The Aetherian Empire (centralized, military, human-dominated) and the Crystalborn Kingdoms (decentralized, magical, multi-racial) were the two superpowers of the Age of Silence.

The War of Silence was not a single conflict but a series of border wars, proxy conflicts, trade embargoes, and magical arms races spanning over a century. Neither side could gain decisive advantage because both controlled major Ley Line nexuses.

**Key Battles:**
- **The Siege of Crystal Gate** — The Aetherians besieged the Crystalborn capital for three years. The city's crystal walls, charged by a Ley Line nexus, proved impenetrable.
- **The Battle of the Burning Plains** — Aetherian war-mages set fire to a thousand square miles of grassland to deny it to the Crystalborn. The fire burned for six months and permanently altered the climate.
- **The Treaty of Broken Spears** — After 140 years, both sides negotiated a treaty. The treaty established the Neutral Zones — areas around Ley Line nexuses that neither power could claim.

**Lasting Scars:**
- The Burning Plains — still a scorched desert, scattered with ancient weapons
- The Neutral Zones — now the wild magical areas between major nations
- Deep mutual distrust between human-dominated and multi-racial nations

**Historical Significance:**
This war proved that mortal civilizations could wage war on a continental scale without divine intervention. It also established the precedent that Ley Line nexuses are strategic resources — a fact that would become critical later.

## 3. The Fall of the Luminari (Age of Silence, ~200 years before Ascension)

**Combatants:** Orinthas Kel-Vorn (the Fool-King) vs. his own civilization vs. five neighboring nations
**Duration:** 20 years (Orinthas's reign)
**Outcome:** Total destruction of the Luminari civilization

**What Happened:**
This was not a war in the traditional sense. It was a catastrophe caused by one man's catastrophic incompetence. (See [03-the-ascended-pantheon.md](03-the-ascended-pantheon.md) for Orinthas's full biography.)

The Luminari were the most advanced civilization of the Age of Silence — masters of art, philosophy, science, and magic. Their libraries contained knowledge accumulated over two thousand years. Their cities were marvels of architecture. Their society was the closest any mortal civilization has come to a golden age.

Orinthas inherited the throne and proceeded to destroy everything:
- **Year 1-3:** Burned the Grand Library (500,000 scrolls), executed dissenters
- **Year 4-8:** Started wars with five nations simultaneously, drained the treasury
- **Year 9-15:** Oversaw a series of increasingly insane construction projects that bankrupted the economy
- **Year 16-18:** Famine, plague, and civil unrest as the infrastructure collapsed
- **Year 19-20:** Overthrown by his starving people, fled into the wilderness

**Lasting Scars:**
- The Luminari Ruins — temporal anomalies preserve fragments of the civilization in pockets of frozen time
- The Lost Knowledge — an estimated 80% of the Age of Silence's accumulated knowledge was destroyed
- Cultural trauma — the fall of the Luminari taught the world that golden ages can end in a single generation

**What the Official History Says:**
The official mythology does not mention the Luminari at all. Orinthas, as God of Wisdom, has systematically erased them from history. Any scholar who discovers Luminari ruins is told they belonged to "a minor civilization of no significance."

## 4. The Brother War (Age of Silence, ~100 years before Ascension)

**Combatants:** The Allied Kingdom of Vestraad vs. the Allied Kingdom of Korinn
**Duration:** 6 years
**Outcome:** Both kingdoms destroyed
**Casualties:** ~400,000

**What Happened:**
The Brother War is the greatest tragedy in Aethermoor's history — two nations that loved each other, manipulated into mutual destruction by one man: Theron Ash-Velk, the future God of Truth.

Vestraad and Korinn had been allies for 300 years. Their peoples intermarried. Their cultures blended. Their alliance was the bedrock of regional stability.

Theron was hired by a rival power to destroy the alliance. Over two years, he forged a web of fake intelligence, fabricated letters between officials, created false witnesses, and planted doctored archaeological evidence suggesting an ancient blood feud between the two peoples.

The war began with a border incident — a Korinn patrol killed by mercenaries disguised as Vestraad soldiers (hired by Theron). Each escalation was fed by new "evidence" from Theron's forgery workshop.

**Key Events:**
- **The Korinn Letters** — Forged correspondence showing Korinn's queen planning to assassinate Vestraad's king. Perfectly crafted — even handwriting experts were fooled.
- **The Ancient Feud** — Theron planted forged historical texts "proving" that Vestraad and Korinn had been enemies in a forgotten age. Scholars authenticated them.
- **The Betrayal at Mirrow Ford** — The worst single day of fighting, where former trading partners killed each other in a river that ran red for three days.
- **The Revelation** — Decades after the war, a survivor found Theron's workshop (he had fled by then). The complete set of forgeries, with his notes, proved the entire war was manufactured.

**Lasting Scars:**
- The Mirrow Wastes — the river valley where the worst fighting occurred, now haunted by the spirits of soldiers who died fighting friends
- Generational trauma — descendants of both kingdoms carry deep-seated grief
- The phrase "brother war" became a synonym for the most senseless kind of conflict
- Theron's workshop (later hidden by the Veiled Hand) contains the evidence that could expose the God of Truth

**What the Official History Says:**
"The Brother War was caused by political miscalculation and cultural misunderstanding. It stands as a warning against the failure of diplomacy."

## 5. The Ascension War (Year 0 — The Ascension)

**Combatants:** The Void Serpent's influence vs. all mortal civilizations vs. the seven future Ascended
**Duration:** ~5 years (the crisis period)
**Outcome:** The Ascension — seven mortals claimed the divine thrones

**What Happened:**
The Void Serpent's cage, weakened by two millennia without divine maintenance, began to fail catastrophically. The Serpent's whispers — previously only audible to the sensitive — became a roar that drove people mad across the continent.

Civilizations collapsed. The Aetherian Empire, already weakened by the War of Silence, fractured into warring states. The Crystalborn Kingdoms turned inward, their crystal cities dimming as Ley Line energy was disrupted. Mass insanity, magical plagues, and reality distortions swept the world.

In the chaos, seven individuals — each carrying divine resonance, each fleeing their own crimes — stumbled into the Threshold and found the empty thrones. (See [03-the-ascended-pantheon.md](03-the-ascended-pantheon.md) for individual stories.)

The moment they sat, the cage was reinforced. The whispers stopped. The world stabilized.

To the people of the world, it was a miracle — the sudden, inexplicable end of the apocalypse. The new gods announced themselves through divine visions and immediately began establishing their official mythology.

**Lasting Scars:**
- The Five Years of Madness — a period of global trauma that made people desperate for divine salvation
- The willing acceptance of the official mythology (people *needed* to believe the gods had saved them)
- The foundations of the major religions
- The beginning of the Purge of Memories

## 6. The Purge of Memories (Years 1-100 After Ascension)

**Combatants:** The Ascended (through the Veiled Hand) vs. truth-keepers, elder races, ancient spirits
**Duration:** ~100 years
**Outcome:** Near-total erasure of pre-Ascension history

**What Happened:**
The Ascended's first act as gods was to destroy all evidence of their mortal lives and the truth of the world's creation. This was not a military campaign — it was an epistemological one. A war against memory itself.

**Phase 1: The Great Burning (Years 1-20)**
- Agents (later formalized as the Veiled Hand) infiltrated every library, archive, and repository in the world
- Pre-Ascension texts were systematically identified and destroyed
- Replacement texts (the official mythology) were planted
- Scholars who resisted were discredited, imprisoned, or killed

**Phase 2: The Spirit Silencing (Years 20-50)**
- Ghosts and spirits from the Age of Silence were the greatest threat — they had memories
- The Ascended performed mass exorcisms at haunted sites
- Spirit-binding rituals trapped the most dangerous witnesses in soul gems hidden across the world
- The Spirit Membrane was thickened (by Aelith) to make casual spirit contact harder

**Phase 3: The Elder Compromise (Years 50-100)**
- Elder elves, ancient dragons, and long-lived beings could not be silenced by force
- The Ascended negotiated: in exchange for keeping the secret, elder beings received divine boons
- Most accepted — the alternative was a war they couldn't win
- A few refused and were exiled, imprisoned in dimensional pockets, or (in one case) petrified

**Lasting Scars:**
- The near-total loss of pre-Ascension historical records
- A handful of survivors who refused to forget — the seeds of the Restoration
- Soul gems scattered across the world, containing witnesses who could testify to the truth
- The deep, institutionalized culture of accepting the official mythology without question

## 7. The Fracture War (Years 800-850 After Ascension)

**Combatants:** The Unmaskers (and sympathizers) vs. the Veiled Hand (and temple forces)
**Duration:** 50 years
**Outcome:** Unmasker defeat (militarily), but the movement survived underground

**What Happened:**
By the 8th century, the Unmaskers had grown from a small family network into a continental movement. Sera Thornhaven's descendants had connected with Brother War survivors, Luminari remnants, and Primordialist elders. They had accumulated enough evidence to prove that the official mythology was, at minimum, incomplete.

The Unmaskers attempted a public revelation — printing pamphlets, organizing debates, presenting evidence in public forums. They called it the "Great Unveiling."

The Veiled Hand responded with overwhelming force. They orchestrated a propaganda campaign painting the Unmaskers as agents of the Void Serpent. Temple armies (Paladins of the Dawn, Inquisitors) were mobilized. The Fracture War was fought not on battlefields but in cities, libraries, and courts.

**Key Events:**
- **The Printing of the Truth** — The Unmaskers published "The Seven Lies" — a document detailing contradictions in the official mythology. It spread across the continent.
- **The Temple's Response** — The Church of the Radiant Sun declared the document heretical. Possession became a crime.
- **The Burning of Ashfall** — A city that sheltered the Unmaskers' printing press was burned by Inquisitors. 2,000 civilians died.
- **The Trial of Dorek Thornhaven** — Sera's direct descendant, leader of the Unmaskers, was captured and tried for heresy. He was executed publicly. His final words were: "The masks will crack."
- **The Underground** — Surviving Unmaskers went underground, adopting the cell structure they maintain to this day

**Lasting Scars:**
- The Inquisition's expanded powers (originally created for this war, now permanent)
- Deep societal distrust of anyone who questions the gods
- The Unmaskers' evolution from an open movement to a secret society
- A handful of copies of "The Seven Lies" still circulating — the most banned book in the world
- The ruins of Ashfall, where the ghosts of burned civilians still cry out

## Minor Conflicts

### The Dragon Schism (Year 400 After Ascension)
The eldest dragons — who remembered the Original Seven — disagreed on how to respond to the Ascended. Some accepted the new order. Others refused. The resulting conflict split dragonkind into the modern six chromatic types, each aligned with a different philosophical position regarding the gods.

### The Clockwork Rebellion (Year 1,200 After Ascension)
The Clockwork Creed — worshippers of mechanical logic and anti-magic — attempted to build a device that could shield an entire city from divine influence. The Ascended sent a coordinated strike of all seven divine aspects simultaneously. The device was destroyed. The city survived but the Creed was permanently marginalized.

### The Vampire Emergence (Year 600 After Ascension)
The first vampires were created when a blood mage attempted to use blood magic to achieve immortality. The resulting curse — vampirism — spread uncontrollably. The Ascended, who could have cured it, chose not to — vampires are dependent on mortal blood, which makes them useful agents of chaos that distract mortals from asking uncomfortable questions about the gods.

### The Werewolf Wars (Year 900 After Ascension)
A lycanthropic plague swept through the northern nations, turning thousands. The resulting wars between infected and uninfected populations lasted decades. The plague's origin was traced to a corrupted Ley Line nexus — though whether the corruption was natural or deliberate remains debated.

### The Grey Pilgrimage Wars (Years 1,500-1,520 After Ascension)
Multiple factions fought for control of the Grey Wastes — some to study them, some to heal them, some to exploit the dead Ley Line beneath. The wars ended in stalemate when the Grey Wastes proved too hostile for permanent occupation. The Grey Pilgrims (a druidic order) were granted custody by treaty.
