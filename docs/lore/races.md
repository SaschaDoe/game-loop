# Races of Aethermoor

## Elves -- The Remembering People

The elves are the oldest mortal race, with lifespans stretching across centuries. They settled the great forests near Ley Line convergences in the Primordial Age, long before the Ascension reshaped the world. Their oral traditions carry memories that predate written history.

### Culture and Society

Elven civilization is decentralized -- loose networks of groves and councils rather than kingdoms. The Eldergrove, their oldest settlement, is said to have been planted by the first elves who heard the ley lines singing. They do not build in stone; their architecture grows from living wood, shaped over decades by patient hands.

### The Primordialist Tradition

Before the Ascension, the elves practiced Old Magic -- not the formalized schools taught in human academies, but a direct communion with the ley lines themselves. The First Song was their method: a wordless melody that asked the earth for power rather than commanding it. When the gods ascended and demanded worship, the elves were the first to notice that the new divine magic felt different -- imposed rather than shared. The Primordialist tradition preserves what fragments of the old ways survived.

### The Luminari

Elves were co-founders of the Luminari, the secret order that guards knowledge of the gods' true nature. Their long memories make them natural archivists of forbidden truths. However, this role has bred a quiet distrust of divine institutions that colors all elven diplomacy.

### Racial Traits

Elves possess heightened magical sensitivity, reflected in superior intelligence and agility. Their attunement to ley lines grants them the ability to sense magical currents that other races cannot perceive. In forests -- their ancestral home -- their sight extends further than any other race.

### Exclusive Class: Primordial

The Primordial is an elven spellcaster who practices the old communion with ley lines rather than the formalized magic of the academies. They channel raw magical energy through song and memory.

## Dwarves -- The Makers Beneath

The dwarves built the first underground civilizations, carving holds from living rock with tools of their own design. Their engineering predates all other mortal construction, and their runic traditions represent the oldest written magical system in the world.

### Culture and Society

Dwarven society is organized around holds -- self-sufficient underground cities governed by councils of elders and master craftsmen. The Iron Republics, their greatest political achievement, were a federation of holds that operated through consensus and trade. Dwarven culture prizes endurance, craftsmanship, and institutional memory.

### The Runeweaver Tradition

The earliest dwarven runes did not invoke gods. They described forces -- weight, heat, pressure, time -- and the relationships between them. The Runeweaver tradition was a science before it was a religion. Each rune is an instruction to reality itself, carved in a grammar that predates all known scripts.

When the gods ascended and demanded the runes be rewritten to name them, the deep holds refused. They sealed their doors and kept the old grammar alive in the Undermountain Holds. The surface holds compromised, incorporating divine names into their runic practice. This schism still divides dwarven society.

### Seven Forces, Not Seven Gods

The dwarven rune system describes seven fundamental forces that shape the world. These forces have no names, no personalities, no thrones. They simply are. The fact that the gods number seven as well is, in dwarven scholarly tradition, not a coincidence but a theft -- the gods claimed dominion over forces that existed long before them.

### Racial Traits

Dwarves are the hardiest race, with exceptional strength and vitality. Their constitution grants them natural resistance to poisons, and centuries of underground living have given them an instinctive understanding of enclosed spaces that manifests as enhanced defense in dungeons.

### Exclusive Class: Runesmith

The Runesmith practices the old dwarven runic tradition, binding forces to metal and stone through carved instructions. Their magic is slow and deliberate but extraordinarily durable.

## Humans -- The Inheritors

Humans are the youngest and most numerous race. What they lack in lifespan they compensate for with adaptability, ambition, and a stubborn refusal to accept limitations. They have built and lost more civilizations than any other race.

### Culture and Society

Human civilization is characterized by its variety. From the vast Aetherian Empire to the Free Cities of Korthaven, humans organize themselves in every conceivable way. They are traders, conquerors, scholars, and wanderers. They build fast, dream big, and sometimes burn spectacularly.

### The Aetherian Empire

The greatest human civilization was the Aetherian Empire, founded by King Valdris the Bold. At its height, the Empire's Spellblade corps -- warriors who wove magic into swordplay -- were the most formidable military force in the world. Valdris discovered the truth about the Ascension and marched on the divine sanctum. The gods struck him down and sank his kingdom in a single night. The Valdris tragedy is the defining moment of human history, though most humans know only the sanitized version: a proud king punished for hubris.

### Adaptability

Humans can be found everywhere -- in elven forests, dwarven mines, and places neither race would choose to settle. Their short lifespans breed urgency; they learn faster, adapt more readily, and form bonds more quickly than the longer-lived races. This makes them natural diplomats and traders.

### Racial Traits

Humans are the most balanced race, with no extreme strengths or weaknesses. Their social adaptability grants them bonuses in dialogue and negotiation. Their will to survive manifests as Second Wind -- the ability to recover from near-fatal wounds through sheer determination.

### Exclusive Class: Spellblade

The Spellblade is a human martial tradition that combines swordplay with elemental magic, inherited from the lost Aetherian Empire's military corps. They fight with blade in one hand and spell in the other.

## Racial Tensions

### Elf-Dwarf Rivalry

The oldest rivalry in the world. Elves view dwarves as crude materialists who reduce the wonder of creation to engineering problems. Dwarves view elves as pretentious idealists who talk in riddles while the real work goes undone. Both races suspect the other of hoarding knowledge about the world before the Ascension. Both are correct.

Despite this friction, the two races share a deep, rarely acknowledged kinship: both remember a world before the gods, and both have paid a price for that memory.

### Elf-Human Dynamics

Elves regard humans with a mixture of condescension and grudging admiration. Humans live such brief lives yet accomplish so much. The Luminari was founded as an elf-human partnership, though the elves sometimes forget this. Humans, for their part, alternately revere and resent elven longevity.

### Dwarf-Human Respect

Of all racial relationships, dwarf-human interactions are the most pragmatic. Both races build things. Both races value results. The Iron Republics stood because humans and dwarves worked together without trying to change each other. This mutual respect endures, even when individual dwarves complain about human impatience and individual humans grumble about dwarven stubbornness.
