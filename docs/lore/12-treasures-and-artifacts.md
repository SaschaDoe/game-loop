# Treasures and Artifacts

## Overview

The most powerful artifacts in Aethermoor are not just items — they are pieces of history. Each one tells a story, carries a secret, and could potentially unravel the gods' carefully constructed lies. The Ascended fear these objects more than any army because artifacts don't forget, don't lie, and don't die.

---

## The Seven Masks

The most important artifacts in existence. When the seven mortals ascended, the divine power transformed them — but not completely. Their mortal faces remained beneath the divine. To hide this, each Ascended created a **Mask** — a divine artifact that projects their godly appearance and suppresses their mortal identity.

### Mask of the Just (Verath's Mask)
**Appearance:** A blindfolded face carved from white marble, with scales balanced on the forehead
**Power:** Grants the wearer perfect judgment — the ability to determine guilt or innocence with absolute certainty
**Hidden Truth:** The blindfold hides Verath's mortal eyes, which still carry the expression of a man who condemned innocents. If the blindfold were removed, anyone looking would see the face of Verath Kol-Sindri, corrupt magistrate
**Location:** Worn by Verath at all times. Only in moments of extreme divine exertion does the mask flicker, revealing a glimpse of the mortal beneath

### Mask of the Beloved (Aelith's Mask)
**Appearance:** A serene, beautiful face of shifting features — it appears as the face of whoever the viewer loves most
**Power:** Inspires unconditional love and trust in anyone who sees it
**Hidden Truth:** Behind the shifting beauty is Aelith's mortal face — cold, calculating, empty. The face of a woman who felt nothing. The contrast between the mask's warmth and the mortal's emptiness is the most disturbing of all the masks
**Location:** Worn by Aelith. The mask occasionally slips when Aelith is overwhelmed by guilt — during these moments, her priests report that the goddess briefly appears "hollow"

### Mask of the Valiant (Khorvan's Mask)
**Appearance:** A war-scarred face of bronze and iron, with eyes blazing like furnaces
**Power:** Grants the wearer absolute courage — immunity to fear in any form
**Hidden Truth:** The mask was not just created to hide Khorvan's face. It was created to hide his *expression*. Beneath the bronze war-hero is the face of a man in permanent, frozen terror. Khorvan has not stopped being afraid since the moment he sat on the throne. The mask hides this from everyone, including himself
**Location:** Worn by Khorvan. The mask has never slipped — Khorvan's need to hide his fear is so intense that his will reinforces the mask constantly

### Mask of the Gentle (Mireya's Mask)
**Appearance:** A soft, maternal face with closed eyes and a slight smile, carved from warm wood
**Power:** Grants the wearer the ability to absorb and transmute pain — touching a wounded person while wearing the mask heals them instantly
**Hidden Truth:** The closed eyes hide Mireya's mortal gaze — the eyes of a torturer, clinical and curious. The gentle smile hides the mouth that ordered the blinding of eighty-six monks. Of all the masks, this one is the most fragile — Mireya's self-loathing weakens it from within
**Location:** Worn by Mireya. The mask cracks slightly whenever Mireya encounters suffering she cannot heal — the Grey Wastes, the cage's weakening, the structural injustices of the world. Each crack reveals a flash of the torturer's face

### Mask of the Truthful (Theron's Mask)
**Appearance:** A face of pure crystal, transparent and reflective, showing everything and hiding nothing
**Power:** Allows the wearer to see all lies, all deception, all falsehood
**Hidden Truth:** The ultimate irony — a mask of transparency hiding the greatest liar in history. The crystal face shows everything *except* what's behind it. Theron designed it himself, and it is his masterwork of deception: a mask that appears to be the absence of a mask
**Location:** Worn by Theron. The only mask that has never shown any sign of weakness — because Theron is too good a liar to let it

### Mask of the Green (Selvara's Mask)
**Appearance:** A face woven from living vines and flowers, constantly growing and blooming
**Power:** Grants the wearer communion with all living things — the ability to feel the life in every plant, animal, and microorganism
**Hidden Truth:** Behind the living mask is the face of the woman who created the Grey Wastes. The vines grow so aggressively because Selvara's guilt drives them — she is trying to bury her mortal face under an infinity of life
**Location:** Worn by Selvara. The mask occasionally withers — leaves browning, flowers dying — when Selvara's attention turns to the Grey Wastes. In these moments, her priests report the goddess appearing "grey and tired"

### Mask of the Wise (Orinthas's Mask)
**Appearance:** An elderly face with deep-set eyes that seem to contain galaxies — vast, knowing, patient
**Power:** Grants the wearer perfect recall and the ability to perceive all possible consequences of any action
**Hidden Truth:** Behind the wise elder's face is the face of a young, confused, arrogant king — Orinthas looks the same as he did the day he burned the Grand Library. The mask ages him by millennia to disguise this. The "galaxies" in the mask's eyes are not wisdom — they are the infinite regret of a man who sees, eternally, every stupid thing he ever did
**Location:** Worn by Orinthas. The mask occasionally flickers during moments of divine indecision — revealing, for a split second, a young man's face twisted with panic

## Pre-Ascension Artifacts

### The Thornhaven Documents
**Type:** Legal records (parchment, ink, wax seals)
**Origin:** The trial of Thornhaven village, conducted by Verath Kol-Sindri
**Power:** None — they are mundane documents. But their content is more dangerous than any weapon.
**Significance:** These are the genuine legal records of Verath's genocide — the real documents, copied by Sera Thornhaven before she died. They contain the original trial transcript (six minutes of farce), the forged evidence, the sentencing order, and the names of all 340 villagers condemned to slavery. They prove that the God of Justice committed the worst miscarriage of justice in history.
**Location:** Multiple copies exist, hidden in different locations. The Veiled Hand has found and destroyed most. At least three remain.
**Player Interaction:** Finding a copy is a major quest milestone. Bringing it to certain NPCs triggers faction reactions ranging from devotion (Unmaskers) to assassination attempts (Veiled Hand).

### The Irongate Inscription
**Type:** Carved stone inscription
**Origin:** Written in blood by centurion Draven as he died, during Khorvan's betrayal
**Power:** The inscription was carved with a dying soldier's blood-oath — a form of Old Magic. It cannot be erased, cannot be weathered, and cannot be destroyed by any force short of divine intervention (and even the Ascended haven't found it).
**Content:** "KHORVAN DRESH-ULM: THE COWARD. Eight thousand died while he fled. I write this with my last breath so that no lie can cover what he did."
**Location:** Buried beneath the ruins of Irongate, preserved in a collapsed tunnel. Finding it requires navigating the ruined fortress and solving architectural puzzles.
**Player Interaction:** Discovering the inscription and connecting it to Khorvan is a pivotal lore revelation.

### Theron's Workshop
**Type:** Hidden room containing forgery tools, fake documents, and personal journals
**Origin:** The workspace where Theron Ash-Velk crafted the forgeries that caused the Brother War
**Power:** The documents themselves are mundane, but Theron's journals contain his personal notes — casual descriptions of how he manipulated two nations into destroying each other. The handwriting matches certain "ancient historical texts" still accepted as genuine by modern scholars.
**Location:** Hidden by the Veiled Hand in an undisclosed location. The Ashen Circle believes it's somewhere in the Mirrow Wastes.
**Player Interaction:** Finding the workshop allows the player to cross-reference Theron's forgeries with "authentic" historical texts, proving them false.

### The Last Luminari Inscription
**Type:** Carved wall inscription preserved by temporal anomaly
**Origin:** Written by the last Luminari scholar as the civilization fell
**Content:** "We survived the Void Serpent. We did not survive our king. Orinthas Kel-Vorn — who outlawed thought, burned knowledge, and drowned soldiers in his madness — has destroyed what the Void could not."
**Power:** Preserved by temporal magic that keeps it legible across millennia
**Location:** In the Temporal Ruins of Luminara, accessible only by navigating time-distorted areas
**Player Interaction:** The inscription directly names Orinthas, connecting the God of Wisdom to the destruction of the most learned civilization in history.

## Primordial Artifacts

### The First Singing Stone
**Type:** A smooth, resonant stone the size of a fist
**Origin:** Condensed from the First Sound — the original vibration that created space and time
**Power:** When struck, it produces the First Sound — a harmonic that resonates with all Ley Lines simultaneously. In the hands of a skilled practitioner, it can temporarily override the Ascended's control of a local Ley Line, restoring Old Magic to the area.
**Danger:** The First Sound is not gentle. Prolonged exposure causes reality to soften — objects lose solidity, time becomes elastic, consciousness expands uncontrollably.
**Location:** Lost. The Restoration has been searching for it for millennia. Last known location: the ruins of the Crystalborn capital.

### The Cage Key
**Type:** A complex, crystalline structure that looks like a three-dimensional key
**Origin:** Created by the Original Seven as a failsafe — a way to open the cage around Xal-Nepheth if it ever needed maintenance
**Power:** Can open (or strengthen) the cage around the Void Serpent. Can also, theoretically, release the Serpent entirely.
**Danger:** Obvious. This is the most dangerous artifact in existence.
**Location:** Hidden in a place where no mortal was ever supposed to go — inside a dormant Architect, in a chamber that exists only when the Architect's "heart" beats (once every few centuries). The Ascended know of it but cannot retrieve it without alerting the Restoration to its location.

### Shaper's Tears
**Type:** Small, luminous droplets of a liquid that isn't quite liquid
**Origin:** Condensed from the Shapers' construction fluid — the substance they used to build the world
**Power:** A single Shaper's Tear can reshape reality in a small area. It can heal a dead Ley Line, grow a mountain, fill a lake, or create an ecosystem. It is raw creative power in concentrated form.
**Limitation:** Each Tear is single-use. There are estimated to be fewer than fifty in the entire world.
**Location:** Found in the deepest chambers of dormant Architects, in pools at the bottom of Shaper-carved valleys, and occasionally in the hands of very old, very secretive collectors.

## Post-Ascension Divine Artifacts

### The Scales of Verath
**Type:** A pair of golden scales
**Official Story:** The scales Verath used to weigh the first sunrise against the first sunset
**Real Origin:** Created by Verath after the Ascension to externalize his compulsion for justice. The scales weigh truth against lies and always find the truth heavier.
**Power:** Any question posed while holding the scales is answered truthfully — the scales tip toward truth with divine certainty.
**Ironic Danger:** If someone asked the scales "Were the Ascended good people in their mortal lives?" the scales would tip to "no." Verath knows this and has ensured the scales are kept in a temple vault, used only for pre-approved questions.

### The Coward's Chains
**Type:** A set of iron shackles, rusted and ancient
**Official Story:** Does not exist (classified)
**Real Origin:** The actual chains Khorvan wore in a military prison after his first desertion — before he achieved his rank through nepotism. He kept them as a reminder of what he never wanted to experience again.
**Power:** The chains reveal the wearer's deepest fear — and make them face it. For most people, this is a harrowing but survivable experience. For Khorvan, touching them would strip his Mask and reveal the coward beneath.
**Location:** Khorvan hid them himself, in the most dangerous location he could find — the deepest chamber of the Void Serpent's cage. No one can reach them without passing through the Void itself.

### The Torturer's Notebook
**Type:** A leather-bound journal, stained and worn
**Official Story:** Does not exist (classified)
**Real Origin:** Mireya's personal journal from her years as chief torturer of the Crimson Court. Contains detailed notes on techniques, observations, and — most damningly — her casual, scholarly tone about inflicting suffering.
**Power:** None. It's a mundane journal. But its contents could destroy the world's faith in the Goddess of Mercy.
**Location:** Mireya destroyed her notebook during the Purge of Memories. Or so she believes. Memory Moss in the ruins of the Crimson Court absorbed the contents when Mireya's tears fell on it as she burned the journal. The moss contains every word.

### The Fool's Crown
**Type:** A golden circlet, surprisingly plain
**Official Story:** Does not exist (classified)
**Real Origin:** Orinthas's crown as King of the Luminari. He wore it while burning libraries, declaring war on the sea, and outlawing mathematics.
**Power:** The crown remembers. Anyone who wears it experiences Orinthas's reign from his perspective — his confidence, his certainty, his absolute inability to see the consequences of his actions. It is a terrifying experience because the wearer feels how *easy* it was for a fool to destroy everything. They feel the seductive certainty of stupidity.
**Location:** In the Temporal Ruins of Luminara, preserved by the same time anomalies that keep the Last Inscription legible. The crown sits on a throne in a frozen throne room, surrounded by temporal distortion.

## Lost Treasures

### The Archives of Light
Crystal matrices from the Crystalborn Kingdoms that stored centuries of knowledge. Shattered during the Purge of Memories. Individual crystals — each containing fragments of the archive — are scattered across the world. Collecting enough fragments could reconstruct lost knowledge.

### The Iron Heart
The core mechanism of the Clockwork Creed's anti-divine device. After the Ascended destroyed the device, the Heart was salvaged by Iron Remnant agents. It still functions — it just needs a power source capable of generating enough energy to shield a city from divine influence.

### The Song of the Deep
A musical composition created by the Sunken Dominion that, when played, opens a temporary portal to Pelagathis — the sunken capital lost in a dimensional rift. The score was divided into seven parts and hidden in seven underwater locations.

### The Dreaming Seed
A seed from the World Oak — the oldest tree in existence, grown by Ley Line energy long before the Ascension. If planted at a Ley Line nexus, it would grow a new World Oak that could serve as a direct connection to the Original Seven's essence, bypassing the Ascended entirely. The Ascended have been searching for it for millennia. The tree itself has hidden it.
