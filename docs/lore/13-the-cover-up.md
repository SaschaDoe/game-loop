# The Cover-Up — How the Gods Erase Their Past

## Overview

The Ascended's greatest achievement is not their divine power — it's the fact that no one knows they don't deserve it. Maintaining this deception across millennia, against countless threats of exposure, requires a vast, multi-layered apparatus of suppression, manipulation, and selective violence.

This document describes the systems, methods, and agents the Ascended use to keep the truth buried.

---

## The Three Layers of Deception

### Layer 1: The Grand Narrative
**What it is:** The official mythology — the creation stories, divine biographies, and theological frameworks taught in every temple, school, and home.
**How it works:** By controlling the foundational story of reality, the Ascended ensure that any contradicting evidence is interpreted through the lens of the official narrative. A scholar who finds a pre-Ascension artifact doesn't think "the gods are lying" — they think "this must fit into the existing story somehow."
**Maintained by:** Temples, schools, cultural traditions, religious festivals, art, music, literature

### Layer 2: The Active Suppression
**What it is:** The ongoing work of finding and neutralizing threats to the Grand Narrative.
**How it works:** The Veiled Hand, Inquisitors, and divine agents constantly monitor the world for dangerous knowledge. When they find it, they destroy it, discredit it, or lock it away.
**Maintained by:** The Veiled Hand, the Inquisition, divine intervention

### Layer 3: The Structural Barriers
**What it is:** The physical and magical barriers the Ascended have built into the world to prevent discovery.
**How it works:** The Spirit Membrane was thickened to prevent casual contact with pre-Ascension ghosts. The Ley Lines were restructured to make Old Magic harder to access. Ancient languages were allowed to die. Truthtrees older than a thousand years are destroyed. The Temporal Ruins are surrounded by dangerous anomalies (not all of which are natural).
**Maintained by:** Divine power, the natural entropy of time, strategic infrastructure

## The Veiled Hand's Operations

### Division of Acquisition
**Mission:** Find dangerous artifacts, documents, and knowledge before anyone else does.
**Methods:**
- A network of informants in every major library, university, and archaeological society
- Agents embedded in the Ashen Circle (the world's largest archaeological organization)
- Magical sensors at known ancient sites that alert the Hand when artifacts are disturbed
- Funding of legitimate research (to stay ahead of independent scholars)

### Division of Erasure
**Mission:** Destroy or neutralize acquired threats.
**Methods:**
- Artifact destruction (preferred — if it doesn't exist, it can't be found)
- Artifact imprisonment (for items too powerful to destroy — locked in Veiled Hand vaults)
- Memory alteration (rare, requires divine intervention — used only on high-value targets)
- Assassination (last resort — creates martyrs; preferred method is discrediting)

### Division of Narrative
**Mission:** Shape public understanding to prevent dangerous questions.
**Methods:**
- Ghost-writing theological texts that reinforce the Grand Narrative
- Placing agents in teaching positions at major institutions
- Funding artists and musicians who create work celebrating the official mythology
- Infiltrating the Unmaskers to misdirect their investigations
- Creating "controlled opposition" — allowing minor heresies that make the Veiled Hand look tolerant while suppressing genuinely dangerous ones

### Division of Silence
**Mission:** Handle threats in the spirit world.
**Methods:**
- Exorcism of pre-Ascension ghosts
- Soul-binding of dangerous witnesses (trapping them in hidden soul gems)
- Spirit Membrane reinforcement at sites where the Membrane is thin
- Monitoring shaman and spirit-walker activity
- Maintaining the "spirit confusion" doctrine — the official explanation for why old ghosts tell contradictory stories

## The Inquisition

The Inquisition is the Veiled Hand's blunt instrument. Where the Hand operates in shadow, the Inquisition operates in the open — with the full authority of the Church of the Radiant Sun.

### Official Purpose
"To protect the faithful from heresy, demonic influence, and threats to divine order."

### Actual Purpose
To terrify anyone who might question the gods into silence.

### Methods
- Public trials of heretics (carefully staged to reinforce the Grand Narrative)
- Book burning and censorship
- Monitoring of academic research
- Suppression of Primordialist, Blood Singer, and Void Touched activity
- Imprisonment and "re-education" of persistent questioners
- Violence (rare, but the Burning of Ashfall serves as a permanent reminder of what happens to those who push too far)

### The Inquisitor's Dilemma
Most Inquisitors genuinely believe they're protecting the world from dangerous ideas. They don't know the truth about the Ascended. They think they're serving real gods against real threats. This makes them effective but also tragic — they're zealots in service of a lie, and many would be horrified to learn the truth.

A handful of senior Inquisitors have been inducted into the Veiled Hand and know the full truth. These individuals carry the heaviest burden — they enforce a system they know is founded on deception, because they believe the alternative (the truth coming out) would be worse.

## Divine Intervention in the Cover-Up

The Ascended rarely act directly — divine intervention is expensive (it requires Ley Line energy) and conspicuous (mortals notice when gods meddle). But certain threats require a divine response:

### Memory Storms
When a particularly dangerous piece of truth surfaces, the relevant Ascended can generate a "memory storm" — a localized magical phenomenon that causes everyone in an area to forget the last few hours or days. Memory storms are disguised as natural magical weather events, but experienced Arcanists have noted they always seem to occur near archaeological discoveries.

### Prophetic Redirection
The Ascended can implant false visions in prophets and oracles, redirecting their divinations away from the truth. This is why prophecies about the gods are always vague and poetic — specific prophecies would be too dangerous to leave uncorrupted.

### Selective Miracles
When faith wavers, the Ascended perform miracles — healings, divine appearances, smiting of evildoers. These are genuine exercises of divine power, but their timing is strategic. A miracle after a heresy trial reinforces the official story. A divine healing in a region where Unmasker activity has been high reminds people that the gods are real and powerful, whatever the doubters say.

### The Nuclear Option: Direct Intervention
In the direst circumstances, an Ascended will personally intervene. This has happened twice in recorded history:
1. **The Clockwork Rebellion** — All seven Ascended struck simultaneously to destroy an anti-divine device
2. **The Drowning of Thal-Vessa** — A coastal city where an entire population had been converted to the Unmasker cause. The city was destroyed by a "divine storm." (The Ascended claimed it was punishment for heresy. The Unmaskers claim it was genocide.)

## Vulnerabilities in the Cover-Up

Despite millennia of effort, the cover-up has weaknesses:

### 1. The Ascended Cannot Cooperate Perfectly
Seven gods with guilty consciences and mutual distrust do not make an effective team. Each Ascended prioritizes hiding their own secrets over maintaining the collective deception. Verath focuses obsessively on the Thornhaven documents. Khorvan obsesses over the Irongate inscription. This means the cover-up has gaps where no individual god feels responsible.

### 2. The World Remembers
The Ley Lines carry the Original Seven's essence — and that essence remembers the truth. Truthtrees grow. Memory moss absorbs. Temporal anomalies preserve. The natural world is slowly, passively working against the cover-up.

### 3. The Spirit World Is Leaky
Despite the thickened Spirit Membrane, ghosts from the Age of Silence occasionally contact the living. Each contact is a potential breach. The Veiled Hand's Division of Silence works overtime, but they can't monitor every thin spot in the Membrane.

### 4. The Void Serpent Talks
Xal-Nepheth knows the truth and whispers it to anyone who listens. The Void Touched are a constant threat because they have access to a witness the Ascended cannot silence.

### 5. Elder Beings Keep Secrets
The elder elves, ancient dragons, and surviving Hollow Ones who accepted the Ascended's deal (silence in exchange for boons) are not all perfectly loyal. Some have grown restless. Some have begun dropping hints to younger beings. Some are waiting for the right moment — the right person — to tell everything.

### 6. The Player Character
The player carries divine resonance. They can hear the Ley Lines. They can walk in the spirit world. They can learn Old Magic. They can access Void knowledge. They are, potentially, the convergence point of every thread the cover-up has failed to sever.

The Ascended sense this. They disagree on what to do about it:
- **Verath** wants to co-opt the player (bind them with oaths of silence)
- **Aelith** wants to confess (she's tired of lying)
- **Khorvan** wants to destroy the player (the coward's response to any threat)
- **Mireya** wants to beg the player for forgiveness
- **Theron** wants to manipulate the player (old habits)
- **Selvara** wants to show the player the Grey Wastes (penance through truth)
- **Orinthas** wants to think about it more (paralysis by analysis)

## The Player's Role in the Cover-Up

Depending on faction alignment, the player can:

1. **Join the cover-up** (Veiled Hand path) — Help maintain the secret, gaining divine favor and power
2. **Expose the truth** (Unmasker path) — Reveal the Ascended's mortal crimes, destabilizing world religion
3. **Replace the gods** (Throne Seeker path) — Use the truth as leverage to claim a throne
4. **Restore the originals** (Restoration path) — Work to bring back the Original Seven
5. **Destroy the system** (Void Covenant path) — Use the truth to weaken the Ascended and the cage simultaneously
6. **Build a new path** (Iron Remnant path) — Use the truth to argue that mortals don't need gods at all
7. **Forgive the gods** (unique path) — Accept the truth and offer the Ascended redemption, creating a new divine order based on honesty rather than deception
